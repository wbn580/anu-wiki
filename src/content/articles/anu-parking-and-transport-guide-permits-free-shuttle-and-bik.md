The Australian National University (ANU) campus in Acton spans over 145 hectares and hosts more than 20,000 students and 4,000 staff members each year. With **Canberra's public transport network** undergoing significant upgrades through the Light Rail Stage 2A project, understanding your commuting options has never been more important. A 2025 ANU Transport Survey revealed that 38% of campus users still drive to campus at least three days per week, while cycling adoption has grown by 14% since 2023. This guide consolidates the latest information on **ANU parking permit costs**, the **free shuttle bus route**, **bike storage locations**, and **public transport to campus** for 2026, helping you plan a smoother, more affordable commute.

## ANU Parking Permit Costs and Zone Options for 2026

Parking on the ANU Acton campus operates under a zoned permit system managed by the ANU Parking Office. As of Semester 1, 2026, **ANU parking permit costs** vary by zone, permit type, and eligibility status. Staff permits in high-demand zones such as Zone 1 (central campus near the Chifley Library and Kambri precinct) are priced at $1,248 annually, while Zone 2 permits covering peripheral car parks cost $876 per year. Students face a different fee structure: a **Zone 3 student parking permit** costs $624 annually, with semester-only options available at $312.

**Casual parking rates** apply for visitors and those without permits. Pay-by-app parking through the CellOPark system charges $2.20 per hour in most campus zones, with a daily maximum of $12.00 in Zone 3 areas. The **ANU Parking Office** introduced a new flexible permit in January 2026 that allows 10 days of parking per month at a reduced rate of $520 annually, targeting part-time staff and postgraduate students with irregular schedules. Eligibility for staff permits requires a formal employment contract of at least 12 months, while student permits are limited to those enrolled in at least 18 units per semester. Applications open online through the ANU Parking Portal, and approval typically takes three business days.

Motorcycle and scooter parking remains free in designated bays across campus, with no permit required. Electric vehicle charging stations have expanded to 24 bays across six locations, including the **Kambri underground car park** and near the Research School of Physics. EV charging costs $0.35 per kWh, with a four-hour maximum stay during peak hours.

## ANU Free Shuttle Bus Route and Timetable

The **ANU free shuttle bus service** connects key points across the Acton campus and extends to nearby residential hubs. Operated by Transport Canberra under a partnership with the university, the shuttle runs on a continuous loop from 7:30 AM to 7:00 PM on weekdays during teaching periods. The 2026 route map includes 11 stops, starting at the **ANU Exchange bus interchange** near the city centre and looping through Daley Road, the Kambri precinct, the John Curtin School of Medical Research, and the residences along Daley Road and Lennox Crossing.

Peak-hour frequency has been increased to every 12 minutes between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM and again from 4:00 PM to 6:00 PM, responding to a 22% rise in shuttle ridership recorded in late 2025. During off-peak hours, the shuttle arrives every 20 minutes. The service is free for all ANU students, staff, and visitors with no ticket or ID check required. Real-time tracking is available through the **ANU Transport App**, which displays live shuttle locations and estimated arrival times at each stop.

A notable addition in 2026 is the weekend loop trial running on Saturdays from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM, serving the **ANU Sport and Recreation Centre**, the School of Art and Design, and the city interchange. This pilot program responds to increased weekend campus activity and will be evaluated in December 2026 for permanent adoption. Accessibility features include low-floor boarding on all shuttle buses and priority seating for passengers with mobility needs.

## ANU Bike Storage Locations and Cycling Facilities

Cycling to ANU has grown substantially, with the 2025 Transport Survey indicating that 19% of students and 11% of staff now commute by bike at least twice per week. The university has responded by expanding **ANU bike storage locations** across campus. Secure bike cages requiring swipe-card access are located at 14 sites, including the **Kambri underground bike hub** (capacity 180 bikes), the Hanna Neumann Building, the Research School of Social Sciences, and Fenner Hall. Access to these cages is free but requires registration through the ANU Security Office.

Open-air bike racks are distributed across more than 60 locations, with high concentrations near the Chifley Library, the ANU Sport centre, and the Copland Building. In 2026, ANU added 120 new rack spaces near the **Health and Wellbeing Centre** and upgraded lighting and CCTV coverage at eight existing bike parking areas. The university also operates two bike repair stations equipped with pumps, basic tools, and repair stands—one near Union Court and another outside the Research School of Biology.

For those considering an electric bike, ANU allows e-bike parking in all standard bike areas, but charging is only permitted in designated zones near the bike cages at Kambri and the Research School of Physics. The **ANU Bike Shop** on North Road offers discounted servicing for students and staff, with a basic tune-up priced at $45. End-of-trip facilities including showers and lockers are available at the ANU Sport building, the Kambri precinct, and the Marie Reay Teaching Centre.

## Public Transport to ANU Campus

**ANU public transport to campus** options have improved markedly with Canberra's integrated bus and light rail network. The **Light Rail Stage 2A** construction is advancing along London Circuit, with the City West stop projected to open in early 2028, placing the campus within a five-minute walk of a light rail station. Until then, bus services remain the primary public transport link. The **R2, R3, and R4 rapid bus routes** stop at the ANU Exchange interchange on Marcus Clarke Street, providing connections to Belconnen, Gungahlin, and Tuggeranong within 30 to 45 minutes.

Transport Canberra's **MyWay+ smart ticketing system**, fully rolled out in mid-2025, allows contactless payment via credit card, smartphone, or the MyWay+ card. A single adult fare from Belconnen to the ANU Exchange costs $3.22 during peak hours and $2.59 off-peak. Students enrolled at ANU are eligible for a **tertiary concession MyWay+ card**, reducing fares by approximately 50%. The card application requires proof of full-time enrolment and can be processed at the ANU Student Central office.

Bus frequency to campus peaks at every 8 to 12 minutes on the R2 and R3 routes during morning and afternoon rush hours. The **ANU Exchange interchange** itself underwent a $2.1 million upgrade in late 2025, adding covered waiting areas, real-time departure displays, and improved lighting. For late-night commuters, the **NightRider services** operate on Friday and Saturday nights, with the N2 route stopping near campus until 2:00 AM during semester.

## Carpooling and Sustainable Transport Incentives

ANU actively encourages sustainable commuting through its **Green Transport Incentive Program**, relaunched in 2026 with expanded benefits. Staff and students who commit to using public transport, cycling, or walking at least three days per week can register for the program and receive a $15 monthly credit toward on-campus food and beverage outlets, up from $10 in 2025. Registration requires logging trips through the ANU Transport App, which verifies sustainable journeys via GPS data.

**Carpooling** is facilitated through the ANU RideShare platform, which matches drivers and passengers based on postcode and schedule. Registered carpool vehicles with at least three occupants can access discounted parking at a flat rate of $6 per day in designated carpool bays near the **Crawford School of Public Policy** and the ANU Exchange. In 2026, the carpool bay network expanded from six to ten locations, adding 34 dedicated spaces.

The university also participates in the **Canberra Cycle to Work scheme**, allowing staff to salary-sacrifice the purchase of a bicycle or e-bike up to a value of $2,500. This benefit is available to ongoing and fixed-term staff with contracts exceeding 24 months. Combined with the expanding bike storage network, these incentives reflect ANU's target of reducing single-occupancy vehicle trips to campus by 25% by 2030, a goal outlined in the university's 2025-2030 Campus Sustainability Plan.

## Accessible Parking and Transport Services

ANU provides dedicated **accessible parking** across all permit zones, with 87 designated bays distributed throughout the campus as of 2026. These spaces require a valid Australian Disability Parking Permit displayed alongside an ANU accessible parking permit, which is issued free of charge to eligible staff and students. The application process involves submitting the state-issued disability permit and a completed ANU Accessible Parking form to the Parking Office, with approval typically granted within one business day.

The **ANU shuttle bus** fleet includes two fully accessible vehicles with low-floor entry, wheelchair ramps, and priority seating. Additionally, the university offers a **door-to-door campus mobility service** for students and staff with temporary or permanent mobility impairments. This service operates on weekdays from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM and requires booking at least 24 hours in advance through the ANU Access and Inclusion Office. In 2026, the service expanded from one to two vehicles, reducing average wait times from 22 minutes to 14 minutes based on early semester data.

Accessible drop-off zones are located near major building entrances, including the **Chifley Library**, the Kambri precinct, and the ANU Medical School. These zones allow 15-minute stops for vehicles displaying a valid disability permit, with no parking fee required.

## Parking Enforcement, Appeals, and Policy Updates

ANU enforces parking regulations through a combination of licence plate recognition technology and regular patrols by **ANU Security**. As of 2026, parking infringement notices carry a fine of $98, reduced to $65 if paid within 14 days. The university issued 6,340 parking infringements in 2025, a 9% decrease from 2024, attributed to improved signage and the expansion of the CellOPark app coverage to all campus car parks.

**Appealing a parking fine** requires submitting an online form within 28 days of the infringement date, providing evidence such as a valid permit, medical emergency documentation, or proof of payment through the parking app. The ANU Parking Review Panel assesses appeals and typically responds within 10 business days. In 2025, 31% of appeals were upheld, resulting in fine cancellations.

A significant policy change effective from February 2026 is the introduction of **graduated penalties for repeat offenders**. Vehicles accumulating three or more unpaid infringements within a 12-month period may be subject to wheel clamping, with a release fee of $175. This policy aligns with practices at other Australian universities and follows a 18-month consultation period with the ANU Student Association and the National Tertiary Education Union.

## FAQ

**How much does an ANU parking permit cost for students in 2026?**
A Zone 3 student parking permit costs $624 annually or $312 per semester. This applies to peripheral car parks such as those near North Road and the ANU Sport centre. Students must be enrolled in at least 18 units per semester to qualify. Casual parking through the CellOPark app costs $2.20 per hour with a $12 daily maximum in Zone 3.

**What are the operating hours of the ANU free shuttle bus in 2026?**
The shuttle runs from 7:30 AM to 7:00 PM on weekdays during teaching periods, with peak frequency every 12 minutes between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM and 4:00 PM to 6:00 PM. A weekend trial operates on Saturdays from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM, serving the ANU Sport centre and city interchange. The service is free for all users with no ID required.

**Where can I find secure bike storage at ANU, and is there a cost?**
Secure swipe-card bike cages are available at 14 locations including Kambri (180-bike capacity), the Hanna Neumann Building, and Fenner Hall. Access is free but requires registration through ANU Security. Open-air racks exist at over 60 sites, with 120 new spaces added near the Health and Wellbeing Centre in 2026. Bike repair stations are located near Union Court and the Research School of Biology.

**Which public transport options serve the ANU campus in 2026?**
The R2, R3, and R4 rapid bus routes stop at the ANU Exchange interchange on Marcus Clarke Street, connecting to Belconnen, Gungahlin, and Tuggeranong. A single adult peak fare from Belconnen costs $3.22. ANU students can access a 50% fare reduction through the tertiary concession MyWay+ card. Light Rail Stage 2A is under construction with the City West stop expected to serve campus by early 2028.
