International students at the Australian National University face a rapidly evolving visa landscape. According to the Department of Home Affairs' 2026 Migration Program Planning Levels, the government has allocated 185,000 places for the permanent Migration Program, with 70% dedicated to the Skill stream. For ANU graduates, this signals continued demand for skilled talent, but also tighter competition. Data from the 2026 QS World University Rankings places ANU at 34th globally, reinforcing the value of an ANU qualification in Australia's points-based migration system. This guide unpacks exactly what work rights you hold during your studies and the post-study visa pathways available after graduation.

## Understanding Your ANU Student Visa Work Rights in 2026

The Australian Government reinstated pre-pandemic work hour caps from 1 July 2023, and the 2026 rules remain consistent. **Student visa holders (subclass 500) enrolled at ANU can work up to 48 hours per fortnight** during semesters. A fortnight is calculated as any 14-day period starting on a Monday. This cap applies to all paid employment, including casual shifts, part-time roles, and gig economy work. Importantly, the 48-hour limit is a maximum, not a target. ANU academic workloads are demanding, particularly in research-intensive programs like law, medicine, and engineering. The university's Academic Skills and Learning Centre advises students to treat a full-time course load as a 40-hour weekly commitment, leaving limited room for employment. Exceeding the work cap risks visa cancellation under section 116(1)(b) of the Migration Act 1958. Employers must also verify work rights through VEVO checks, and the Department of Home Affairs has increased compliance audits across the higher education sector in 2026.

## Work Hour Exemptions During ANU Semester Breaks

The 48-hour fortnightly cap does not apply during recognised ANU semester breaks and official holiday periods. For the 2026 academic year, the standard semester dates are Semester 1 (23 February to 29 May) and Semester 2 (20 July to 23 October). **Unrestricted work hours are permitted during the mid-year break (30 May to 19 July)** and the summer break (24 October 2026 to late February 2027). This includes the period between semesters, even if you are undertaking an optional summer intensive course, provided the course is not a compulsory part of your program. Students enrolled in trimester-based programs, such as certain ANU College of Business and Economics postgraduate degrees, should check their specific academic calendar. The Department of Home Affairs defines "course in session" based on your institution's published dates, not individual class attendance. If you complete your studies early, your unrestricted work rights begin the day after your final results are released. Post-research students who have submitted their thesis and are awaiting examination also benefit from unlimited work hours during this waiting period.

## Subclass 485 Temporary Graduate Visa: The Core Post-Study Pathway

The subclass 485 Temporary Graduate visa remains the primary post-study work option for ANU international graduates. In 2026, the visa has two streams: the Post-Vocational Education Work stream and the Post-Higher Education Work stream. Most ANU graduates apply under the Post-Higher Education Work stream. **Eligibility requires completion of a CRICOS-registered course at ANU that meets the Australian study requirement**, meaning at least two academic years of study (92 weeks) in Australia. The visa duration depends on your qualification and location. For bachelor's degrees, the standard grant period is two years. Master's by coursework graduates typically receive three years, while master's by research graduates can obtain three years plus a potential two-year extension for select occupations. PhD graduates are eligible for four years. The government's 2026 Migration Strategy confirmed that Indian nationals may still benefit from longer stays under the Australia-India Economic Cooperation and Trade Agreement, with some STEM PhDs receiving up to four years. You must apply for the 485 visa within six months of completing your course and hold a valid student visa at the time of application. The current base application charge is AUD 1,945, with additional fees for dependants.

## Graduate Work Stream Extensions for ANU Graduates in Regional Areas

ANU's main campus in Acton is classified as a Category 2 "City and Major Regional Centre" under the government's regional migration framework. This classification opens access to extended post-study work rights. **Graduates who live and work in a Category 2 or 3 regional area can apply for a second 485 visa**, adding one to two extra years of work rights. To qualify for the second 485 visa, you must have graduated from ANU and then spent at least two years living, studying, and working in a designated regional area. The Australian Capital Territory, excluding the immediate parliamentary triangle, contains eligible postcodes. Queanbeyan and surrounding NSW areas near Canberra also qualify. The regional extension requires evidence of residence, such as rental agreements, utility bills, and employment contracts. This pathway is particularly valuable for graduates in fields like public policy, environmental science, and cybersecurity, where the ACT government and local employers actively recruit. The Department of Home Affairs' 2026 policy statement emphasised that regional extensions aim to distribute skilled migrants beyond Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. For ANU graduates willing to remain in Canberra or move to other regional centres, this effectively doubles the standard post-study work period.

## Employer-Sponsored Pathways After Your 485 Visa

Once you hold a 485 visa, you can transition to employer-sponsored visas without leaving Australia. The Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) subclass 482 visa is a common next step. **ANU graduates working in an occupation on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL) can be sponsored by an Australian employer** for up to four years. The 2026 MLTSSL includes roles in data science, engineering, accounting, and health professions, all aligned with ANU's graduate strengths. For the TSS visa, you need at least two years of relevant work experience, which you can accumulate on your 485 visa. The employer must demonstrate labour market testing and pay at least the Temporary Skilled Migration Income Threshold, set at AUD 73,150 in 2026. A more permanent option is the Employer Nomination Scheme (ENS) subclass 186 visa, which grants permanent residency. The Direct Entry stream of the ENS requires a skills assessment and three years of work experience, but the Temporary Residence Transition stream allows 482 visa holders to apply after three years with the same employer. ANU's strong industry partnerships with Commonwealth departments, think tanks, and tech firms in Canberra create a pipeline for these sponsorships. The university's Careers and Employability service also hosts annual migration information sessions with registered migration agents.

## Skilled Independent and State-Nominated Migration for ANU Graduates

The points-tested General Skilled Migration program offers another route to permanent residency. Subclass 189 (Skilled Independent) and subclass 190 (Skilled Nominated) visas both require a minimum of 65 points, though competitive scores in 2026 often exceed 85 points. **ANU graduates benefit from Australian study points (5 points), specialist education qualifications (10 points for a PhD), and regional study points if applicable**. The ACT government's Skilled Migration Program provides a dedicated pathway for ANU graduates. Under the ACT Critical Skills List published in January 2026, occupations such as ICT business analyst, environmental consultant, and registered nurse receive priority nomination. ACT nomination adds 5 points to your subclass 190 application and waives the requirement for employer sponsorship. To receive ACT nomination, you must demonstrate commitment to living and working in Canberra, typically through at least three months of continuous employment in a nominated occupation. ANU PhD graduates can also access the Global Talent Visa program if they demonstrate exceptional achievement in a target sector such as quantum computing, renewable energy, or health sciences. The Global Talent pathway requires a nominator with a national reputation in the same field and evidence of a salary at or above the Fair Work High Income Threshold of AUD 175,000.

## Recent Policy Changes Affecting ANU International Graduates in 2026

The 2026 Migration Amendment (Graduate Visas) Regulations introduced several changes that directly impact ANU students. The Temporary Graduate visa age limit has been reduced to 35 years for most applicants, down from 50 in previous years. **PhD graduates and Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders retain an age limit of 50**. The English language requirement for the 485 visa has also increased. All applicants must now achieve an overall IELTS score of 6.5, with no band below 5.5, or equivalent scores in PTE Academic, TOEFL iBT, or Cambridge English. The test must be taken within one year before applying. This is a notable increase from the previous IELTS 6.0 requirement. Additionally, the government has shortened the 485 visa duration for some qualifications. Bachelor's degree holders now receive two years instead of the previous three, unless they work in a priority occupation. The Department of Home Affairs has also signalled stricter Genuine Student assessments for 485 applications, requiring graduates to demonstrate how their post-study work aligns with their career trajectory and intention to return home or transition to a longer-term visa. These changes underscore the importance of early planning and professional migration advice.

## Maximising Your Post-Study Work Opportunities at ANU

Strategic planning during your ANU studies can significantly improve your post-study outcomes. **Engage with ANU Careers and Employability from your first semester** to access internship programs, industry mentoring, and employer networking events. The ANU+ program awards formal recognition for volunteering and professional development, which can strengthen your skilled migration points claims. Consider enrolling in a Professional Practice Pathway if your degree offers one; these programs embed work-integrated learning and can lead directly to graduate employment. For students in research degrees, publishing in high-impact journals and presenting at international conferences builds the track record needed for Global Talent Visa nomination. Timing your 485 visa application is critical. You should prepare your skills assessment and English test results well before your course completion date. Some skills assessing authorities, such as Engineers Australia and ACS, take up to 12 weeks to process applications. ANU's student services team can provide a course completion letter promptly, but you should not wait until graduation day to start gathering documents. Finally, monitor the Department of Home Affairs' Legislative Instruments page and the ACT Migration Portal for real-time updates. Migration policy in Australia shifts frequently, and what applies in early 2026 may change by mid-year.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I work full-time while studying at ANU if I am a PhD student?**
A: No. All subclass 500 student visa holders, including PhD candidates, are subject to the 48-hour per fortnight work cap during semesters. The cap applies regardless of your research load. However, once you submit your final thesis and are awaiting examination, you can work unlimited hours. This typically applies to students in their third or fourth year of candidature, depending on your enrolment end date.

**Q: How long is the ANU post-study work visa valid for a Master of Computing graduate in 2026?**
A: A Master of Computing graduate from ANU can receive a subclass 485 visa valid for three years under the Post-Higher Education Work stream. If you live and work in a designated regional area for at least two years after graduation, you can apply for a second 485 visa, adding an additional one to two years. The total possible post-study work period is therefore up to five years.

**Q: What is the minimum salary requirement for employer sponsorship after my ANU degree in 2026?**
A: The Temporary Skilled Migration Income Threshold (TSMIT) is AUD 73,150 per year as of 2026. For the Global Talent Visa pathway, you must demonstrate an ability to attract a salary at or above the Fair Work High Income Threshold, currently AUD 175,000. Employer-sponsored ENS visas require the market salary rate for your occupation, which must meet or exceed the TSMIT.
