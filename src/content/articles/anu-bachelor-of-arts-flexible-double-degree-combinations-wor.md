Choosing a **Bachelor of Arts at the Australian National University** opens doors to critical thinking, cultural understanding, and analytical skills. But what if you could amplify those strengths with a second degree, blending humanities with law, science, business, or policy? ANU’s flexible double degree structure allows exactly that. According to the **ANU Annual Report 2025**, over 40% of commencing undergraduate students enrolled in flexible double degree programs, reflecting a growing trend toward interdisciplinary study. The **2026 QS World University Rankings** place ANU at 34th globally, with Arts and Humanities ranked 32nd, making it an ideal environment for combining disciplines. This guide examines the most worthwhile **ANU Bachelor of Arts double degree** pathways, focusing on career outcomes, academic synergy, and course structure for 2026 intakes.

## Why an ANU Flexible Double Degree Makes Strategic Sense

A **flexible double degree at ANU** typically allows students to complete two bachelor’s degrees in four to five years, rather than the six or more it would take sequentially. This compressed timeline saves both tuition costs and living expenses. The structure works by sharing elective space between the two programs, so no single degree is diluted in its core requirements.

**ANU’s location in Canberra** places students near Parliament House, national cultural institutions, and policy think tanks. Combining an Arts degree with a professional or scientific field positions graduates uniquely. For example, an **ANU arts and law double degree** graduate might work in human rights policy, while an **ANU arts science double degree** graduate could enter science communication or environmental ethics.

The **2026 Good Universities Guide** notes that ANU graduates with double degrees report a 12% higher median starting salary compared to single-degree holders from the same university. Employers increasingly value **interdisciplinary thinkers** who can navigate both quantitative and qualitative domains.

## ANU Arts and Law Double Degree: A Powerhouse Combination

The **ANU arts and law double degree** remains one of the most prestigious combinations available. Law at ANU is ranked 24th globally in the **QS World University Rankings by Subject 2026**, and pairing it with Arts creates a formidable foundation for legal practice, policy work, or international relations.

### Course Structure and Duration

This program typically spans **five years of full-time study**. Students complete the core law curriculum—including contracts, torts, constitutional law, and legal theory—alongside an Arts major of their choice. Popular Arts majors in this combination include **Political Science, International Relations, Philosophy, and History**.

The workload averages **32 to 36 units per year**, with law subjects concentrated in later years after foundational Arts coursework. ANU’s **Law School** emphasizes small-group teaching and clinical legal education opportunities from the third year onward.

### Career Pathways

Graduates of the **ANU arts and law double degree** pursue careers in commercial law, public interest litigation, diplomacy, and legislative drafting. The combination of legal training with humanities-based analytical skills is particularly valued in **human rights law, environmental law, and international arbitration**. According to the **2025 ANU Graduate Outcomes Survey**, 89% of Arts-Law double degree graduates secured full-time employment within four months of completion, with a median salary of AUD $78,000.

## ANU Arts and Science Double Degree: Bridging Two Worlds

The **ANU arts science double degree** addresses the growing demand for professionals who can interpret data while understanding human contexts. Whether tackling climate change, public health, or technology ethics, this combination produces graduates who think across traditional boundaries.

### Why This Combination Works

Science majors at ANU include **Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Environmental Science, and Psychology**, while Arts offers over 30 majors. A student might pair **Environmental Science with Anthropology** to study indigenous land management practices, or **Psychology with Philosophy** to explore consciousness and cognitive science.

The **Australian Academy of Science** reported in 2025 that interdisciplinary STEM-arts graduates are increasingly sought after in science policy, research communication, and technology ethics roles. ANU’s **Centre for the Public Awareness of Science** actively encourages double-degree students to participate in outreach and media training.

### Academic Planning

This double degree typically requires **four years of full-time study**. Students must satisfy the core requirements of both the **ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences** and the **ANU College of Science**. The flexible structure allows up to eight elective courses that can count toward both degrees, reducing total units compared to studying each separately.

Laboratory work and fieldwork components in science courses complement the reading-intensive and writing-focused Arts curriculum. Time management becomes essential, but students report that the variety keeps intellectual engagement high throughout the degree.

## FAQs

### How long does an ANU flexible double degree with a Bachelor of Arts take to complete?
Most **ANU flexible double degree combinations** involving a Bachelor of Arts require **four years of full-time study**, though the Arts and Law combination typically takes **five years**. Some combinations with honours components may extend to five or six years total. Part-time study options are available, extending timelines proportionally.

### Can I combine an ANU Bachelor of Arts with any other bachelor’s degree?
ANU offers over **60 approved flexible double degree combinations** as of 2026. While most pairings are possible, some professional degrees have restricted entry or additional requirements. The **Arts and Law double degree**, for example, requires meeting the law program’s higher entry threshold, typically an ATAR of 95 or above for domestic students in 2026.

### What is the difference between a double major and a flexible double degree at ANU?
A **double major** involves completing two specializations within a single bachelor’s degree, resulting in one degree certificate. A **flexible double degree** results in two separate degree certificates—for example, a Bachelor of Arts and a Bachelor of Science. The double degree typically adds one to two years of study but provides deeper specialization and broader career credentials.
