## Understanding ANU Parking Zones and Permit Types

The Acton campus is divided into distinct **ANU parking zones**, each with specific eligibility criteria and time restrictions. Zone A covers the central academic precinct near the Chifley Library and is reserved for **annual permit holders** with high-priority needs, such as staff with disabilities or essential service vehicles. Zone B extends to peripheral areas like the sports fields and typically offers daily casual rates starting at $8.50 for up to four hours in 2026. **ANU parking permits** fall into three main categories: annual staff permits, semester-based student permits, and short-term virtual permits purchased via the CellOPark app. The university has phased out physical stickers entirely since 2025, relying on licence plate recognition technology to enforce compliance.

**Annual staff permits** cost $1,200 in 2026, a 4% increase from the previous year, and allow access to designated zones based on employment status. **Student semester permits** are priced at $380 per semester and are limited to 500 allocations per intake, with a waitlist opening three weeks before classes begin. Casual visitors can use **pay-as-you-go options** through the CellOPark app, which charges $2.50 per hour in most zones, capped at $12 daily. It is critical to check zone signage carefully, as fines for unauthorised parking reach $120 per offence, and ANU Parking Services issued over 8,000 infringement notices in 2025 alone.

## How to Apply for an ANU Parking Permit in 2026

Applying for an **ANU parking permit** requires navigating an online portal that opens for applications on 15 January 2026 for Semester 1. Eligible staff must provide their university ID number and vehicle registration details, with approvals prioritised for those living more than 15 kilometres from campus or with documented medical conditions. Students can apply through the ANU Parking Portal starting 1 February 2026, but demand is fierce—only 18% of applicants received a permit in the 2025 academic year due to the limited **semester permit cap**. The application system uses a points-based algorithm that factors in travel distance, course load, and carpool registrations.

To improve your chances, consider forming a **carpool group** of three or more registered students, which earns bonus points equivalent to a 20-kilometre distance buffer. Once approved, payment must be completed within 72 hours via credit card or BPAY, or the offer expires. Virtual permits activate immediately after payment, and the system automatically links your plate to the approved zone. Remember that **ANU parking zones** are enforced 24/7, and permits do not guarantee a space during peak hours between 9:00 AM and 2:00 PM, when occupancy rates hit 97%, based on ANU Facilities and Services data from March 2026.

## Parking Enforcement and Appeals Process

**ANU parking enforcement** operates under strict regulations managed by ANU Facilities and Services, with 12 patrol officers monitoring lots daily. Infringement fines are $120 for unauthorised parking in restricted **ANU parking zones**, and $80 for expired meter sessions. To appeal, you must provide evidence such as a valid permit screenshot, payment receipt, or witness statement. ## FAQ
