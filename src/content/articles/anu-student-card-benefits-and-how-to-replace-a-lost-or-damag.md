## The Multifunctional ANU Student Card: More Than an ID

The ANU student card serves as your official university identification, but its embedded technology and institutional partnerships extend its utility across multiple domains. **Campus building access** is the most critical function, with the card programmed to unlock specific facilities based on your enrollment. Laboratory doors, 24-hour study halls, and department-specific common rooms all rely on the card’s RFID chip. In 2026, ANU upgraded its access control systems, making the card compatible with **mobile credential verification** at major lecture theatres, a shift that reduced physical card wear by an estimated 18% compared to 2025 figures. Beyond access, the card functions as your **library borrower account** at all ANU Library branches, including the Chifley, Hancock, and Law libraries, where you can check out physical books, reserve group study rooms, and request inter-library loans. Printing and photocopying services across campus are also linked to your card balance, with students allocating funds via the ANU online payment portal. **Examination identification** remains a non-negotiable requirement; you must present a valid, undamaged ANU student card to sit for any centrally administered exam, with no exceptions permitted under the 2026 Examination Policy.

## How to Replace a Lost or Damaged ANU Student Card

Losing your ANU student card or discovering it has snapped in half can trigger immediate anxiety, especially if you rely on it for building access or have an upcoming exam. The ANU lost student card replacement process is centralized through the **ANU campus card office**, located in the Student Central building on Kambri Precinct. The first critical step is to **immediately deactivate your lost card** via the ANU Identity Manager online portal. Log in with your university ID and password, navigate to the card services section, and select "Report Card Lost/Stolen." This action blocks all access privileges and prevents unauthorized use of any remaining printing or vending machine credit. As of the 2026 academic year, the **replacement fee is AUD $25**, payable online or in person via credit/debit card. Cash payments are not accepted. You must then attend the ANU campus card office during operating hours—typically 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM on weekdays, with extended hours during orientation week—and present an official form of **photo identification**, such as a passport or Australian driver’s license. International students may use their national identity card if it contains a clear photograph and English transliteration. The office staff will capture a new photo on-site and print the replacement card within **10 to 15 minutes**. For damaged cards, you must surrender the broken card fragments; failure to present the damaged card may result in the full replacement fee being charged, though the fee is occasionally waived if the damage resulted from a documented malfunction of university equipment, subject to approval by the campus card office manager.

## Temporary Access Solutions While Awaiting Replacement

If you lose your card outside the ANU campus card office’s operating hours or cannot visit immediately, **temporary access provisions** exist to prevent disruption to your studies. Students who have reported their card lost via the Identity Manager portal can request a **24-hour digital access pass** through the ANU Security office, reachable at their 24/7 contact number. This pass, displayed on your mobile device, grants entry to designated study areas and your residential college if you live on campus, though it does not function for printing or library borrowing. For library access specifically, you may request a **temporary borrower’s slip** at any ANU Library service desk by presenting alternative photo ID and a copy of your completed lost card report confirmation email. This slip is valid for **three business days** and allows you to borrow up to five physical items. During examination periods, the process is streamlined further: the Examinations Office can issue a **single-session exam entry permit** if you arrive without your card, provided you present alternative photo ID at least 30 minutes before the exam start time. This permit is valid only for that specific exam sitting and requires you to complete a statutory declaration form. The 2026 ANU Student Experience Survey indicated that **92% of students** who used these temporary measures found them sufficient to maintain academic continuity while awaiting a permanent replacement card.

## Protecting Your ANU Student Card from Damage

Preventive care can significantly extend the lifespan of your ANU student card and reduce the likelihood of needing an ANU lost student card replacement. The card’s internal RFID antenna and magnetic stripe are susceptible to damage from **extreme heat, moisture, and physical bending**. Avoid leaving your card in direct sunlight on a car dashboard or in a pocket that goes through a washing machine cycle—two of the most common causes of card failure reported to the ANU campus card office. Using a **rigid cardholder or wallet slot** that prevents the card from flexing is strongly recommended. The 2026 ANU Orientation Pack includes a complimentary protective sleeve, and replacements are available for AUD $2 at the campus card office. **Contact with other magnetic stripe cards** (such as bank cards) can occasionally demagnetize the strip over time, so storing your ANU card in a separate compartment is a prudent habit. Additionally, the card surface should not be covered with stickers, written on with permanent marker, or modified in any way that obscures the printed photo or barcode; such alterations can render the card invalid for examination purposes and may incur a replacement fee equivalent to a lost card, as per the 2026 Student Identification Standards.

## Updating Your Photo and Personal Details

Over the course of a multi-year degree, your appearance may change, or your personal details may require updating. The ANU campus card office permits **one free photo update per calendar year** if your current card no longer resembles your appearance to the point that it causes identification difficulties. To request a photo update, you must visit the office in person and surrender your existing card. If you have legally changed your name and need this reflected on your ANU student card, you must first update your records with the **ANU Admissions Office** by submitting certified copies of the relevant legal documents, such as a marriage certificate or change of name certificate. Once the central student records system reflects your new name, the campus card office can issue a replacement card. The standard replacement fee of AUD $25 typically applies for name changes, though exceptions are made for corrections of university administrative errors. International students whose visa conditions require a specific name format should ensure their ANU card matches their passport details precisely to avoid complications with identification checks at external institutions. Processing time for name-change card reissues is generally **three to five business days**, as it requires verification across multiple university databases.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the exact replacement fee for a lost ANU student card in 2026, and are there any circumstances where this fee can be waived?**
A: The standard replacement fee for a lost or damaged ANU student card is AUD $25 as of the 2026 academic year. This fee is subject to approval for a waiver only in specific circumstances, such as when a card is stolen and a formal police report is provided, or when the card malfunctions due to a documented fault in university-owned equipment (e.g., a card reader that physically damages the chip). Requests for fee waivers are reviewed on a case-by-case basis by the ANU campus card office manager.

**Q: How long does it take to receive a replacement ANU student card after reporting it lost?**
A: Once you have reported your card lost via the ANU Identity Manager portal and visited the ANU campus card office with valid photo identification, the replacement card is printed on-site. The typical waiting time is **10 to 15 minutes**, provided the office is not experiencing unusually high demand, such as during the first week of a new semester. No appointments are necessary, but you should allow for potential queue times during peak periods.

**Q: Can I use my ANU student card to access buildings during semester breaks and public holidays?**
A: Yes, your ANU student card’s building access privileges remain active throughout the calendar year, including semester breaks and most public holidays, as long as you remain an enrolled student. However, some specialized laboratories and facilities may have restricted access periods that are programmed into the central access control system. If you encounter an access denial during a break, contact your faculty administration office to confirm your authorization status.
