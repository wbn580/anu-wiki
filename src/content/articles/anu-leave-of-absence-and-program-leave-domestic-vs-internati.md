The **ANU leave of absence** framework for 2026 introduces updated provisions that every student must understand before pausing their studies. According to the Australian National University's 2026 Enrolment Policy, approximately 8.3% of the total student population applies for some form of program leave annually, with international students accounting for nearly 42% of those applications. The Department of Home Affairs reported in early 2026 that visa compliance issues related to improper leave arrangements affected over 1,200 international students across Australian universities in the previous academic year. Navigating the distinction between **program leave** and **leave of absence** at ANU is not merely an administrative formality—it carries significant consequences for your enrolment status, student visa conditions, and future re-enrolment eligibility. This guide examines the rules governing both domestic and international students, explains visa implications in detail, and outlines the maximum permissible leave durations under current university statutes.

## Understanding ANU Leave of Absence vs Program Leave

The terminology used by ANU can cause confusion, yet the distinction between **program leave** and **leave of absence** is fundamental to your enrolment record. Program leave refers to an approved break from a specific degree program while maintaining your formal enrolment at the university. The **ANU program leave policy** treats this as a temporary suspension of active coursework rather than a withdrawal. Your student record remains open, your program place is preserved, and you retain access to university systems including email and the library during the approved leave period.

A **leave of absence** at ANU, by contrast, typically applies to shorter interruptions within a single teaching period or semester. This category covers situations where a student cannot attend classes for a defined stretch—perhaps four to six weeks—due to medical reasons, compassionate grounds, or unforeseen personal circumstances. Unlike program leave, a leave of absence does not always pause your enrolment progression and may require you to catch up on missed assessments through special consideration processes rather than a formal deferral of your study load.

The critical operational difference lies in how each leave type interacts with **census dates** and academic progress rules. Program leave, when approved before the relevant census date, removes your liability for tuition fees and prevents a fail grade from appearing on your transcript. A leave of absence granted mid-semester may not carry the same financial protection, especially if the application is lodged after the census date has passed. Understanding this distinction before submitting any application can save thousands of dollars in unnecessary tuition charges.

## ANU Domestic Student Leave Policy: Eligibility and Procedures

**ANU domestic student leave policy** provisions in 2026 offer greater flexibility compared to the regulations governing international students, though they remain subject to strict maximum duration limits. Domestic students—defined as Australian citizens, New Zealand citizens, and Australian permanent residents—can apply for program leave of up to 12 months in a single application. The **ANU maximum leave duration** for domestic students across the entire lifespan of a degree program is capped at 24 months in total, except where exceptional circumstances are documented and approved by the relevant Associate Dean.

To initiate a **program leave application**, domestic students must submit their request through ISIS, the university's online student administration portal, at least 10 working days before the commencement of the teaching period in which the leave would take effect. The application requires a clear statement outlining the reasons for seeking leave, though ANU does not mandate supporting documentation for domestic students unless the leave extends beyond one calendar year. Common grounds accepted for domestic program leave include medical conditions, carer responsibilities, financial hardship, and employment commitments that temporarily conflict with full-time study requirements.

Domestic students should note that **Commonwealth Supported Place** (CSP) entitlements are not indefinitely preserved during extended leave periods. Under the 2026 Higher Education Support Act provisions, a CSP may lapse if a student remains on program leave for more than two consecutive calendar years without re-enrolling in at least one course. Students receiving **Austudy, Youth Allowance, or ABSTUDY** payments must also notify Services Australia of any leave arrangements, as extended absences from study can affect eligibility for income support. ANU Student Central provides dedicated financial advisers who can assist domestic students in understanding the interaction between Centrelink benefits and program leave decisions.

## International Student Visa Implications of ANU Program Leave

The intersection of **ANU program leave international student visa** conditions and university leave policies represents the highest-stakes dimension of this topic. International students holding a **Student Visa (subclass 500)** are bound by Condition 8202, which mandates that the visa holder must remain enrolled in a registered course and maintain satisfactory course progress. When an international student applies for program leave at ANU, the university is legally required to report the change in enrolment status to the Department of Home Affairs through the Provider Registration and International Student Management System (PRISMS).

The visa implications differ sharply depending on whether the leave is classified as **compassionate or compelling circumstances**. The Department of Home Affairs recognises medical incapacity supported by a treating practitioner's certification, bereavement of a close family member, traumatic events including natural disasters or political upheaval in the student's home country, and university-initiated program changes as valid grounds for approved leave without visa cancellation. In these cases, ANU can approve program leave while the student remains offshore, and the Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) status is updated to "deferred" or "suspended" rather than "cancelled."

Conversely, if an international student applies for program leave on grounds that do not meet the compassionate or compelling threshold—such as travel, voluntary work, or employment opportunities—the university must cancel the CoE. This triggers a **28-day period** during which the student must either depart Australia, enrol in an alternative registered course, or apply for a different visa subclass. The **ANU maximum leave duration** for international students on compassionate grounds is generally limited to 12 months, after which a new CoE and potentially a new student visa application become necessary. Students who remain outside Australia on program leave for more than six months should also verify whether their Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) policy remains valid or requires a suspension arrangement with their insurer.

## Maximum Leave Duration Rules Across Student Categories

The **ANU maximum leave duration** framework operates on a tiered structure that reflects the different regulatory obligations attached to each student category. For **domestic undergraduate students**, the standard maximum is 12 months of continuous program leave, with a cumulative cap of 24 months over the duration of a single degree program. Postgraduate research students operate under separate provisions: PhD candidates may apply for up to 12 months of program leave per candidature, with extensions possible for parental leave or long-term illness, while Masters by Research students are limited to 6 months of total leave across their typically shorter enrolment period.

**International students** face more restrictive maximum durations due to visa compliance requirements. The standard maximum for international undergraduate and postgraduate coursework students is 12 months, but only when supported by compassionate or compelling evidence. Without such evidence, program leave is rarely approved for more than 6 months, and the CoE cancellation procedure described above applies. International research students are subject to the same leave limits as their domestic counterparts in terms of university policy, but must additionally satisfy the Department of Home Affairs that their leave does not breach visa conditions. In practice, this means any leave beyond 12 months for an international PhD candidate will almost certainly require a new visa application.

A frequently overlooked constraint is the **cumulative leave calculation** across multiple programs. If a student transfers from one ANU degree to another, any program leave taken under the original enrolment is counted toward the cumulative maximum for the new program. This rule prevents students from circumventing leave limits by switching degrees. Students who have exhausted their maximum leave allowance and face ongoing circumstances that prevent study should consult with ANU Student Wellbeing about the possibility of a **compassionate withdrawal** rather than accumulating further leave periods that exceed the allowable caps.

## Re-enrolment Procedures After ANU Leave

**ANU re-enrolment after leave** is not automatic, and students who misunderstand this process risk losing their place in their program. Approximately three months before the approved leave end date, ANU Student Administration sends a re-enrolment notification to the student's university email account. This communication outlines the steps required to reactivate enrolment, including course selection for the upcoming teaching period, confirmation of personal details, and, for international students, verification of current visa status and OSHC coverage.

Domestic students must log into ISIS and complete the online re-enrolment checklist, which includes confirming their **Unique Student Identifier (USI)** , updating contact information, and selecting courses for the return semester. The re-enrolment deadline typically falls four weeks before the start of the teaching period. Failure to complete re-enrolment by this deadline results in the automatic cancellation of the program leave and a status change to "discontinued," which requires a new admission application to resume studies. Students who miss the deadline but wish to return should contact their academic college's student services team immediately, as late re-enrolment may be permitted at the discretion of the Associate Dean.

International students face additional **re-enrolment conditions** tied to their visa status. Before re-enrolment can be processed, the student must provide ANU with a copy of their current student visa grant notice, evidence of valid OSHC for the intended period of study, and, if the leave exceeded six months, a completed **return to study health assessment** if the leave was medical in nature. ANU will then issue a new CoE or reactivate the existing one in PRISMS. International students should be aware that the Department of Home Affairs may request evidence of the reasons for leave and the student's continued capacity to meet course requirements before permitting re-entry to Australia. Planning for re-enrolment should begin at least eight weeks before the intended return date to accommodate these additional processing requirements.

## Consequences of Unauthorised Leave and Non-Compliance

Taking **unauthorised leave**—that is, ceasing attendance and assessment submission without formal program leave approval—carries severe academic and administrative penalties. Under the ANU Academic Progress Rule 2026, students who do not complete any courses in a teaching period and have not been granted program leave are automatically classified as having made **unsatisfactory academic progress**. This triggers a show cause process requiring the student to explain their absence and may result in exclusion from the program for a minimum of 12 months.

For international students, the consequences are amplified by **visa compliance reporting obligations**. ANU is legally required under the Education Services for Overseas Students (ESOS) Act to report any international student who ceases enrolment without approved leave. Such reporting leads to CoE cancellation and, typically, the Department of Home Affairs issuing a notice of intention to cancel the student visa. Once a student visa is cancelled under these circumstances, the individual may be subject to a **three-year exclusion period** preventing the grant of further Australian visas. The financial and reputational damage of unauthorised leave far outweighs the administrative effort required to submit a proper program leave application.

Students who realise they have inadvertently entered a period of unauthorised leave should act immediately. ANU Student Compliance can, in limited circumstances, **retrospectively approve program leave** if the application is submitted within 20 working days of the census date for the relevant teaching period and is supported by compelling documentation. Retrospective approval is never guaranteed and becomes increasingly difficult to obtain as time passes. The university's position is that students bear the responsibility for understanding and adhering to leave application deadlines, and ignorance of the rules does not constitute grounds for an exception.

## Special Considerations: Research Students, Honours, and Coursework Thresholds

**Research degree candidates** operate within a distinct leave framework that merits separate examination. Higher Degree by Research (HDR) students at ANU, including PhD and MPhil candidates, are governed by the Research Training Program (RTP) scholarship conditions as well as university enrolment policy. Program leave for HDR candidates is typically referred to as **candidature suspension** and must be approved by the primary supervisor, the Delegated Authority within the relevant academic college, and the Dean of Graduate Research. The grounds for suspension mirror those for coursework students but additionally include research-specific circumstances such as fieldwork delays, equipment access problems, and ethics approval timelines.

Scholarship holders must pay particular attention to the interaction between program leave and **stipend payments**. ANU RTP scholarships provide for up to 12 months of paid sick leave during candidature, which operates separately from program leave. If a research student suspends candidature for non-medical reasons, stipend payments cease for the duration of the suspension and the scholarship end date is extended accordingly. Students receiving externally funded scholarships should verify the leave provisions of their specific funding agreement, as some external bodies impose stricter conditions than ANU's standard policy.

**Honours students** face unique constraints because of the compressed 10-month timeline of most ANU Honours programs. Program leave is generally discouraged for Honours candidates, and where approved, is limited to a single teaching period. Any leave extending beyond one semester typically requires the student to withdraw from the Honours year and reapply for the following intake, as the thesis submission deadlines and coursework sequencing cannot accommodate extended interruptions. Students considering Honours should factor this reduced flexibility into their decision-making and address any foreseeable need for leave before commencing the program.

## FAQ

**How long can I take program leave at ANU as an international student without affecting my student visa?**

International students can take up to 12 months of program leave on compassionate or compelling grounds without automatic visa cancellation, provided ANU updates the CoE status to "suspended" rather than "cancelled" in PRISMS. Leave exceeding 12 months will require a new CoE and, in most cases, a new student visa application. Leave taken on non-compassionate grounds typically results in CoE cancellation regardless of duration, triggering the 28-day departure or alternative enrolment requirement under Condition 8202 of the Student Visa (subclass 500).

**What happens to my tuition fees if I apply for program leave after the 2026 census date?**

Program leave applications submitted after the census date for the relevant teaching period do not provide retrospective fee remission. You will remain liable for the full tuition fees of any courses in which you were enrolled at the census date, and those courses will appear on your transcript with a withdrawal without failure (WD) grade only if you withdraw before the final withdrawal deadline. After that deadline, fail grades apply. To avoid financial liability, program leave applications must be submitted and approved before the census date of the teaching period from which you intend to take leave.

**Can I work in Australia while on approved program leave from ANU?**

International students on approved program leave with a suspended CoE retain their student visa but are generally not permitted to work in Australia during the leave period, as the work rights attached to the Student Visa (subclass 500) are conditional on active enrolment. Students who remain in Australia during program leave should seek advice from the Department of Home Affairs regarding their work eligibility. Domestic students on program leave face no work restrictions from the university but should check any scholarship or Centrelink conditions that may apply.

**What is the cumulative maximum leave I can take across multiple ANU degrees?**

The 24-month cumulative maximum for domestic students and the 12-month maximum for international students apply per degree program, but ANU tracks leave across all enrolments. If you complete a Bachelor degree having taken 18 months of program leave, and subsequently enrol in a Master degree at ANU, the leave taken in the previous program is not deducted from your new entitlement. However, if you transfer from one program to another without completing the first, any leave taken in the original program counts toward the cumulative cap in the new program.
