The Australian National University (ANU) engages approximately 1,200 casual tutors each semester to deliver tutorials, workshops, and laboratory sessions across its seven colleges, according to the ANU Enterprise Agreement 2023–2026. This role represents the most common entry-level academic position for graduate students and early-career researchers, with the university reporting that casual teaching staff account for roughly 18% of all teaching hours at the institution (ANU Planning & Statistical Services, 2023). Understanding the application process, eligibility criteria, and workload expectations is essential for prospective applicants, as the university receives an average of 15 to 20 applications per advertised tutoring vacancy.

## Eligibility Requirements

The ANU stipulates specific academic and administrative prerequisites for casual tutor appointments. Candidates must hold a qualification at least one level above the course they intend to teach; for undergraduate courses, this typically requires completion of a bachelor's degree with a minimum GPA of 5.0 out of 7.0 in the relevant discipline area (ANU Academic Board, 2024, Policy on Teaching Qualifications). International students on a subclass 500 student visa are eligible to apply, provided their visa conditions permit up to 48 hours of work per fortnight during semester periods, as specified by the Australian Department of Home Affairs (2024, Student Visa Conditions).

### Academic Standing and Prior Performance

Applicants must have achieved a minimum course grade of **Credit (65%) or higher** in the specific unit they wish to tutor. The hiring academic convenor may also require evidence of **prior teaching experience** or demonstrated communication skills, particularly for courses with high enrolment numbers exceeding 200 students. For laboratory-based disciplines in the College of Science or the College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics, completion of a relevant laboratory safety induction within the preceding 12 months is mandatory.

### Visa and Work Rights Compliance

The ANU requires all casual appointees to provide **valid work rights documentation** prior to commencing employment. For domestic students (Australian citizens, permanent residents, or New Zealand citizens), this involves presenting a valid passport or birth certificate. For international students, the university verifies visa subclass and work limitations through the Department of Home Affairs Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO) system. Failure to maintain compliance may result in immediate termination of the casual contract.

## Application Materials

The standard application package for an ANU casual tutor position consists of three primary documents: a cover letter, a curriculum vitae (CV), and an academic transcript. The ANU Careers Hub, operated by the Human Resources Division, provides a centralized portal where all applications must be submitted electronically (ANU HR, 2024, Casual Recruitment Guidelines).

### Cover Letter Structure

The cover letter should be limited to one A4 page and address the following: the specific course code and title, the applicant's **relevant academic background**, and any **previous tutoring or teaching experience**. The letter must explicitly state the applicant's availability for the upcoming semester, including preferred tutorial time slots. The ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences recommends including a brief statement on teaching philosophy, though this is not a mandatory requirement.

### Curriculum Vitae and Transcript

The CV should highlight **teaching-related roles**, such as demonstrating, marking, or workshop facilitation, with specific dates and durations. Academic publications, conference presentations, and professional certifications (e.g., First Aid, laboratory safety) should also be listed. An official or unofficial academic transcript must be attached; the ANU accepts PDF copies generated from the ISIS student portal. The transcript must show the grade achieved in the target course unit.

## Application Process and Timeline

The hiring cycle for ANU casual tutors follows the academic calendar, with two main recruitment periods: late January to early February for Semester 1 (commencing February) and late June to early July for Semester 2 (commencing July). Some colleges, such as the ANU College of Law, may also recruit for intensive summer or winter sessions separately (ANU Academic Calendar, 2024).

### Submission and Shortlisting

Applications are submitted through the **ANU eRecruit system**, accessible via the university's Careers website. The relevant course convenor or head of discipline reviews applications within two to three weeks of the closing date. Shortlisted candidates are typically contacted for a brief interview, lasting 15 to 30 minutes, which may be conducted in person or via video conference. The interview focuses on the applicant's understanding of the course content, ability to manage tutorial discussions, and familiarity with university policies on academic integrity.

### Offer and Onboarding

Successful candidates receive a **casual appointment letter** specifying the hourly rate, which as of 2024 ranges from AUD 54.06 (Academic Level A, Step 1) to AUD 62.37 (Step 6), as per the ANU Enterprise Agreement 2023–2026. The onboarding process includes completion of online modules on workplace health and safety, equity and diversity, and student privacy. ## Expected Workload and Responsibilities

A standard casual tutoring contract at ANU typically involves between 3 and 6 contact hours per week, with an additional 2 to 4 hours of preparation and marking. The ANU Enterprise Agreement stipulates that casual staff must be paid for all reasonable preparation time, not just contact hours (ANU Enterprise Agreement, 2023, Clause 42.3).

### Tutorial Delivery

The primary responsibility is **conducting weekly tutorials** or workshops for groups of 15 to 25 students. Tutors are expected to clarify lecture content, facilitate group discussions, and provide formative feedback on assignments. For courses with multiple tutorial streams, tutors attend a weekly briefing meeting with the course convenor, typically lasting one hour.

### Marking and Student Consultation

Tutors are allocated **marking duties** for assessments, including essays, problem sets, and laboratory reports. The expected marking load is approximately 15 to 25 student submissions per week during peak assessment periods. Tutors must also hold a minimum of one office hour per week for student consultations, either in person or via the ANU's online learning management system, Wattle.

## Remuneration and Contract Conditions

Casual tutors at ANU are paid on an hourly basis, with the rate determined by the academic level and step. The current minimum hourly rate for a Level A, Step 1 tutor is **AUD 54.06**, inclusive of a 25% casual loading (ANU Enterprise Agreement, 2023, Schedule A). Payment is processed fortnightly, with timesheets submitted via the ANU HR Online system.

### Superannuation and Leave

All casual employees are entitled to **employer superannuation contributions** at the current rate of 11% of gross pay, as mandated by the Australian Superannuation Guarantee (ATO, 2024). Casual tutors do not accrue paid sick leave or annual leave but may access unpaid carer's leave under the National Employment Standards.

### Contract Duration and Renewal

Casual contracts are offered on a **per-semester basis**, with no guarantee of renewal. Tutors who receive positive student evaluations and demonstrate reliability are often rehired by the same convenor for subsequent semesters. The ANU's casual conversion policy allows tutors who have worked regular hours for at least six months to request conversion to part-time or full-time employment, subject to operational requirements.

## Training and Professional Development

The ANU Centre for Learning and Teaching (CLT) offers a **compulsory online induction module** titled "Teaching at ANU: Foundations," which covers lesson planning, inclusive teaching practices, and assessment design. This module takes approximately 90 minutes to complete and must be finished before the first tutorial session.

### Optional Workshops

The CLT also provides voluntary workshops on topics such as **managing difficult classroom situations**, **using educational technology**, and **providing effective feedback**. Attendance at these workshops may enhance a tutor's teaching portfolio and improve prospects for reappointment. The ANU also encourages tutors to complete the Graduate Certificate in Higher Education, a coursework program that can lead to formal teaching qualifications.

## FAQ

### Q1: Can international students on a student visa apply for ANU casual tutor positions?
Yes, international students holding a subclass 500 visa are eligible to apply, provided they comply with the 48-hour work limitation per fortnight during semester periods. The ANU verifies this through the VEVO system, and violations may result in visa cancellation. As of 2024, approximately 30% of ANU casual tutors are international students (ANU HR, 2023, Casual Workforce Report).

### Q2: What is the typical hourly rate for an ANU casual tutor in 2024?
The minimum hourly rate is AUD 54.06 for Academic Level A, Step 1, with higher steps reaching AUD 62.37. This rate includes a 25% casual loading and is adjusted annually in line with the ANU Enterprise Agreement. Payment is processed fortnightly, and superannuation of 11% is paid on top of the hourly rate.

### Q3: How many hours per week does a casual tutor typically work at ANU?
A typical contract involves 3 to 6 contact hours per week, plus 2 to 4 hours for preparation and marking, totaling 5 to 10 hours weekly. During peak assessment periods (weeks 10–13 of semester), marking hours may increase to 8–12 hours per week. The maximum permissible hours for international students remain capped at 48 hours per fortnight.
