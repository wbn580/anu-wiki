Canberra is a planned city — designed by Walter Burley Griffin in 1913 — and is consistently ranked among the world's most liveable cities. For students, it offers a safe, green, and navigable environment with lower living costs than Sydney or Melbourne.

## Public Transport

**Buses**: Transport Canberra operates the bus network. Key route: the R2/R4 routes run along Northbourne Avenue, connecting Dickson, City, and ANU with Woden, Belconnen, and Gungahlin. Students should get a **MyWay card** for 50% concession fares.

**Light rail**: The first stage (Gungahlin to City) is operational. Extension to Woden (passing through the parliamentary triangle) is under construction (expected 2027–2028).

**Cycling**: Canberra has excellent cycling infrastructure — separated bike paths connect ANU to all major suburbs. The campus is flat and bike-friendly.

**Walking**: The campus is compact. Most residential colleges, Academic buildings, and the city centre are within a 15-minute walk of each other.

Turner and Braddon are the most popular and most expensive — closest to campus and to Braddon's restaurant/bar strip.

## Groceries

- **ANU campus**: Daily Market (convenience store, Union Court)
- **Canberra Centre**: Coles + Aldi (10 min walk from campus)
- **Dickson**: Woolworths + Asian groceries (Dickson is Canberra's Chinatown)
- **Farmers markets**: EPIC Farmers Market (Saturday mornings), Capital Region Farmers Market

## Getting a Part-Time Job

Canberra's job market is government-dominated, which means fewer retail/hospitality jobs than in tourist cities. However, ANU students have access to:
- **ANU Careers casual job board**: On-campus hospitality, admin, tutoring
- **Hospitality in Braddon/Civic**: Cafés, bars, restaurants
- **Tutoring**: High school and university tutoring (ANU students are in demand)
- **Seasonal work**: Summer internships in government departments and agencies (apply through the ANU Australian National Internships Program)

Minimum wage in Australia as of 2026 is approximately A$24/hour (national minimum wage, indexed). International students can work up to 48 hours per fortnight during semester and unlimited hours during holidays.
