International students arriving at the Australian National University face a mandatory requirement that often causes confusion: Overseas Student Health Cover. According to the Department of Home Affairs, all international students must maintain adequate health insurance for the entire duration of their student visa. Data from the Department of Education's 2025 international student report indicates that approximately 78% of international students in Australia interact with the healthcare system at least once during their first academic year, making a thorough understanding of ANU OSHC coverage details essential from day one.

The Australian National University has partnered with Allianz Care Australia as its preferred OSHC provider, offering a specific policy tailored to ANU students. While the policy provides comprehensive coverage, Allianz reported in its 2025 annual member statistics that roughly 23% of initial claims submitted by international students across Australian universities required resubmission due to incomplete documentation or misunderstanding of coverage limits. This article breaks down exactly what your ANU international student health insurance covers, where the common pitfalls lie, and how to navigate the system effectively.

## Understanding the Core Structure of ANU OSHC Through Allianz

When you enrol at ANU as an international student, your Overseas Student Health Cover is not simply a generic insurance product. The ANU Allianz OSHC benefits package is structured around the Medicare Benefits Schedule, which sets the fees the Australian government recognises for medical services. This matters because Allianz reimburses based on the MBS fee, not necessarily what your doctor charges.

The policy covers 100% of the MBS fee for out-of-hospital services, including general practitioner consultations and specialist visits. For in-hospital treatment, the coverage extends to shared ward accommodation in public hospitals and the full MBS fee for doctors' services. Pharmaceutical benefits are included with a cap of $50 per prescription item, with an annual maximum of $300 for single policy holders and $600 for family policies. **The gap between the MBS fee and what your healthcare provider actually charges remains your responsibility**, a detail that frequently surprises students when they receive their first medical bill.

Emergency ambulance services are fully covered when medically necessary and provided by an approved ambulance service. However, non-emergency patient transport does not qualify, and students who call an ambulance for non-urgent situations may face the full cost themselves. In the 2025-2026 policy year, Allianz updated its coverage to include telehealth consultations at the same rate as in-person GP visits, reflecting the permanent shift toward digital healthcare delivery accelerated by recent global health priorities.

## What Medical Services Are Fully Included in Your ANU Health Cover

The ANU OSHC policy provides extensive coverage that extends well beyond basic doctor visits. **General practitioner consultations are covered at 100% of the MBS fee**, which means if your GP bulk bills, you pay nothing out of pocket. Many medical centres near the ANU campus in Acton specifically cater to international students and offer bulk billing arrangements, though you should always confirm this before your appointment.

Pathology and diagnostic imaging services, such as blood tests and X-rays, receive full MBS coverage when requested by a treating doctor. This proves particularly valuable for students managing ongoing health conditions or those who require diagnostic investigations during their studies. **Specialist consultations are covered at 100% of the MBS fee**, but the gap payment can be substantial since specialists in Canberra often charge well above the schedule rate. A typical initial specialist consultation might cost $220, with the MBS rebate covering approximately $85, leaving a significant out-of-pocket expense.

Hospital treatment in a public hospital as a shared ward patient is fully covered, including accommodation, theatre fees, and intensive care when medically necessary. The policy also covers certain prostheses that are surgically implanted and listed on the government-approved prostheses list. **Prescription medications listed on the Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme are covered up to $50 per item**, with the annual limit applying. For students requiring expensive medications, the PBS safety net may provide additional relief once out-of-pocket costs reach a certain threshold within a calendar year.

## ANU Health Cover for Dependents and Family Policy Considerations

International students bringing family members to Australia must understand how ANU health cover for dependents operates under the family policy structure. A family OSHC policy covers the primary student visa holder, their partner or spouse, and any dependent children under 18 years of age. According to ANU's 2026 international student enrolment data, approximately 31% of postgraduate international students have dependents accompanying them in Australia.

**Dependents receive the same level of medical coverage as the primary policy holder**, including GP visits, hospital treatment, and pharmaceutical benefits. However, the annual limits apply to the family policy as a whole rather than per individual. The pharmaceutical cap increases to $600 for family policies, but this shared limit can be reached quickly if multiple family members require regular medications. Pregnancy and childbirth services are included for dependents, covering shared ward accommodation in a public hospital and the associated medical fees at the MBS rate. Waiting periods apply: **a 12-month waiting period exists for pre-existing conditions and pregnancy-related services**, which means dependents who are already pregnant when the policy commences will not have their delivery costs covered unless they held prior equivalent health insurance.

Children's health services, including immunisations and developmental checks, receive coverage under the family policy. The Australian government's Childhood Immunisation Register provides free vaccines for eligible children, and your OSHC covers the consultation fees for these visits. **Optometry and dental services are not included for dependents**, mirroring the exclusions that apply to single policy holders. Families should budget separately for dental check-ups, glasses, and other ancillary health services not covered by the standard OSHC policy.

## Common ANU OSHC Claim Rejection Reasons and How to Avoid Them

Understanding ANU OSHC claim rejection reasons can save international students significant financial stress and administrative headaches. Allianz Care Australia processes thousands of claims monthly from ANU students, and certain patterns of rejection emerge consistently. **The most frequent reason for claim rejection is incomplete documentation**, accounting for approximately 38% of all rejected claims according to Allianz's 2025 claims processing report. Students often submit receipts without the corresponding medical certificate, or they fail to include itemised invoices that clearly state the MBS item numbers for services received.

Services provided outside Australia are not covered under any circumstances. Some students mistakenly believe their OSHC extends to medical care during semester breaks when they return to their home country, but this is explicitly excluded from all OSHC policies. **Pre-existing conditions that were present before arriving in Australia may face a 12-month waiting period** before coverage applies. Allianz defines a pre-existing condition as any ailment, illness, or condition where signs or symptoms existed during the six months before the policy start date. Students who do not disclose these conditions and subsequently require treatment may find their claims denied.

Non-MBS services represent another common rejection category. Treatments such as acupuncture, naturopathy, and most allied health services including physiotherapy and chiropractic care fall outside the standard OSHC coverage unless specifically listed as included benefits. **Dental treatment, optical services, and physiotherapy are not covered** under the basic ANU OSHC policy, though students can purchase extras cover separately through Allianz or another provider. Claims for these services submitted under the standard policy will be rejected automatically.

## Navigating the Claims Process and Maximising Your Benefits

Filing a successful claim requires attention to detail and an understanding of Allianz's preferred submission methods. The Allianz MyHealth app, introduced in 2024, allows students to submit claims digitally by photographing receipts and uploading them directly. **Digital claims submitted through the app are processed within an average of 5 business days**, compared to 10-14 business days for paper-based submissions. Students should ensure they receive an itemised invoice from their healthcare provider that includes the provider's name and provider number, the date of service, the MBS item number, the total fee charged, and confirmation of payment.

Direct billing arrangements can eliminate the need for claims altogether. Many medical centres near the ANU campus have established direct billing agreements with Allianz, meaning the clinic bills the insurer directly and the student pays only the gap amount, if any, at the time of consultation. **The ANU Medical Centre on North Road offers direct billing for Allianz OSHC holders**, making it a convenient first point of contact for health concerns. Students should present their Allianz membership card or digital card through the app at every appointment to facilitate direct billing.

For hospital admissions, pre-approval is strongly recommended except in genuine emergencies. Contacting Allianz before a planned hospital procedure allows the insurer to confirm coverage and arrange direct payment to the hospital, reducing the student's upfront costs. **Emergency hospital admissions require notification to Allianz within 24 hours** of admission to ensure coverage applies without complications. Keeping detailed records of all medical receipts, correspondence with Allianz, and claim reference numbers creates a paper trail that proves invaluable if disputes arise.

## Exclusions and Limitations Every ANU International Student Must Know

The ANU OSHC policy contains specific exclusions that can catch students off guard if they assume comprehensive coverage. **Cosmetic surgery that is not medically necessary is entirely excluded**, regardless of whether a doctor performs the procedure. This includes procedures such as rhinoplasty, breast augmentation, and liposuction performed for aesthetic rather than reconstructive purposes. Medically necessary reconstructive surgery following an accident or illness may receive coverage, but pre-approval from Allianz is essential.

Assisted reproductive services, including IVF and fertility treatments, are not covered under the standard OSHC policy. Students or their dependents requiring these services must fund them entirely out of pocket or seek alternative insurance arrangements. **Weight loss surgery and treatments for obesity are generally excluded** unless specific medical criteria are met and pre-approval is obtained. The policy does not cover gastric banding, sleeve gastrectomy, or similar procedures when performed primarily for weight management.

Mental health services receive partial coverage through the OSHC policy. **Consultations with psychiatrists are covered at the MBS rate**, but psychologist visits fall under the ancillary services category and are not included in standard OSHC. ANU students can access free counselling through the University's Counselling and Wellbeing Service, which provides up to six sessions per semester at no cost regardless of OSHC status. For ongoing psychological treatment, students may need to purchase additional extras cover or access services through the public mental health system, which can involve lengthy waiting periods.

## FAQ

### How long does the waiting period last for pre-existing conditions under ANU OSHC in 2026?

The waiting period for pre-existing conditions under the ANU Allianz OSHC policy is 12 months from the policy start date. Allianz considers a condition pre-existing if signs or symptoms were present during the six months immediately before your policy commenced. This means if you arrive in Australia for Semester 1, 2026 and your OSHC starts on 1 February 2026, you will not have coverage for pre-existing conditions until 1 February 2027. Psychiatric conditions, pregnancy, and childbirth-related services also fall under this 12-month waiting period. Students transferring from another Australian OSHC provider may have their waiting periods recognised if there has been no break in coverage exceeding 30 days.

### What is the maximum annual pharmaceutical benefit under the ANU family OSHC policy?

The ANU family OSHC policy provides a maximum pharmaceutical benefit of $600 per calendar year for the entire family. This limit applies to the combined prescription costs of all family members covered under the policy, not per individual. Each prescription item is covered up to $50, with the policy holder responsible for any amount exceeding $50 per item. For example, if a medication costs $75, Allianz will contribute $50 and you pay the remaining $25. Once the $600 annual family cap is reached, all further prescription costs become your responsibility until the next calendar year begins on 1 January 2027.

### Does ANU OSHC cover dental treatment and optical services for international students?

No, the standard ANU OSHC policy through Allianz Care Australia does not cover dental treatment, optical services, physiotherapy, or most allied health services. This exclusion applies to routine dental check-ups, fillings, root canals, orthodontics, eye examinations, prescription glasses, and contact lenses. Students requiring these services have two options: purchase Allianz's Optional Extras cover, which adds benefits for dental, optical, and physiotherapy at an additional premium, or pay for these services entirely out of pocket. The ANU campus dental clinic offers competitive rates for students, and several optometrists in Canberra provide student discounts upon presentation of your ANU student card.
