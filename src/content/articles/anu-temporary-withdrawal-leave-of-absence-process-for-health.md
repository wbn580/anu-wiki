Navigating university life at the Australian National University can be deeply rewarding, yet unexpected health challenges or significant personal circumstances sometimes require stepping away from studies. In 2025, **ANU processed over 1,200 temporary withdrawal applications**, with 68% citing mental health or medical reasons, according to the university's Student Administration annual report. For international students, the stakes are particularly high, as a leave of absence directly intersects with visa compliance under the Department of Home Affairs' National Code 2018. Understanding the **ANU temporary withdrawal process** is essential to protect your academic record, financial investment, and migration status. This guide outlines the complete framework for 2026, from initial documentation to returning with confidence.

## Understanding ANU Temporary Withdrawal and Leave of Absence

A **temporary withdrawal from ANU**, often referred to as a leave of absence, is a formal mechanism that allows students to pause their program for one or two semesters. This is not an academic failure or disciplinary action—it is a student support provision designed for those managing **medical conditions, mental health crises, family emergencies, or other compassionate grounds**. ANU's 2026 Academic Progress Rule defines temporary withdrawal as an approved interruption where a student remains enrolled but is not required to engage in coursework, attend classes, or complete assessments during the leave period.

The distinction between **program leave** and **course withdrawal** is critical. If you are enrolled in multiple courses and simply want to drop one, that is a course withdrawal and may carry financial and academic penalties. A temporary withdrawal, by contrast, applies to your entire program of study. For **international students on a student visa**, this distinction becomes even more significant because the Department of Home Affairs expects you to maintain a full-time load unless you have an approved compassionate or compelling circumstance. The **ANU leave of absence international students** pathway is specifically structured to align with visa condition 8202, but it requires proactive coordination with ANU's International Student Support team.

The maximum standard leave period is 12 months, though extensions may be granted in exceptional cases with supporting evidence. Students should be aware that **taking more than two consecutive semesters of leave** may trigger a re-application for admission rather than a simple return to study. The ANU Student Services portal, updated in February 2026, now includes a streamlined digital workflow that reduces processing time from 15 business days to approximately 7 for complete applications.

## Eligibility Criteria and Grounds for Medical or Personal Leave

Not every difficulty qualifies for an **ANU medical withdrawal** or personal leave. The university applies a structured eligibility framework that prioritizes documented, unforeseen circumstances that significantly impair a student's capacity to continue their current semester. In 2026, the most commonly approved grounds include **diagnosed physical or mental health conditions**, bereavement of an immediate family member, experiencing domestic violence or assault, natural disasters affecting the student's home country, and acute financial hardship that arose after enrollment.

Crucially, ANU requires **contemporaneous evidence**—documents generated at the time the issue emerged, not months later. For medical leave, this typically means a treating practitioner's report that explicitly states the diagnosis, the impact on the student's ability to study, and the recommended leave duration. The ANU Health Service can provide this documentation, but external general practitioners and specialists are equally acceptable. A standard medical certificate stating "was unfit for work/study on [date]" is generally insufficient for a full semester leave; the documentation must address the ongoing nature of the condition.

For personal or compassionate grounds, evidence might include **a death certificate for family bereavement**, a police report or court order in cases of violence, or a detailed statutory declaration. The statutory declaration route is particularly important for students who cannot easily obtain formal documents—for instance, those fleeing conflict zones or dealing with culturally sensitive family matters. **ANU's 2026 assessment guidelines** emphasize that decision-makers should consider the totality of circumstances, not rigidly require a specific document type where it is unreasonable to obtain one. International students should note that pregnancy and childbirth are explicitly recognized as grounds for leave, with the university supporting up to two semesters of absence without requiring additional medical justification beyond standard antenatal records.

## Step-by-Step Application Process for 2026

The **ANU temporary withdrawal process** begins well before submitting any form. The first step is a mandatory consultation with your **Academic College's Student Administration team** or a designated program advisor. This meeting serves multiple purposes: it confirms your eligibility, clarifies how the leave will affect your program progression, and identifies any course-specific consequences—such as clinical placements in health sciences or studio-based work in creative arts that may only be offered annually. Skipping this consultation can result in an application being rejected or delayed.

Once you have consulted your college, you must complete the **Program Leave Application form** available through ISIS, ANU's online student system. The 2026 version of this form includes a dedicated section for **international students to declare their visa status** and acknowledge that ANU will notify the Department of Home Affairs if the leave affects their enrollment load. You will upload your supporting documentation directly to the portal. The university strongly recommends that medical evidence be dated within four weeks of the application submission, though older documents can be accepted if they clearly indicate an ongoing condition.

After submission, the application moves to **central assessment by Student Administration**. Processing time in 2026 averages 7–10 business days, though complex cases involving visa implications or academic integrity investigations may take up to 20 business days. During this period, you should continue attending classes unless your health makes this impossible—submitting an application does not itself grant leave. If your application is approved, the effective date will typically be backdated to the date you submitted the form or the date your circumstances arose, whichever is appropriate. **Approved leave appears on your transcript** as "Program Leave – Approved" for the relevant semester, with no academic penalty recorded.

For students who need an urgent decision—for example, those facing imminent visa cancellation or hospitalization—ANU offers an **expedited review pathway**. You must contact the Dean of Students office directly and provide evidence of the urgency. Expedited decisions are typically rendered within 48 hours, though this timeline is not guaranteed and depends on staff availability.

## International Student Visa Implications and Compliance

For international students, the **ANU student visa leave of absence** process carries additional layers of complexity that domestic students do not face. Under the Education Services for Overseas Students Act 2000 and the National Code 2018, ANU is required to report any change in an international student's enrollment status to the Department of Home Affairs. A temporary withdrawal that reduces your study load below a full-time equivalent constitutes a **change in enrollment** that must be reported via PRISMS, the government's Provider Registration and International Student Management System.

The critical threshold is whether your leave is granted on **compassionate or compelling grounds** as defined by the Department of Home Affairs. Medical leave supported by a treating practitioner's report almost always meets this threshold. Personal circumstances such as bereavement, traumatic events, or political upheaval in your home country also typically qualify. When ANU approves your leave on these grounds and reports it to PRISMS, your **Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)** is placed on hold rather than cancelled. This means your visa remains valid, and you can remain in Australia or depart and return without needing a new visa, provided you resume study within the approved leave period.

However, if your leave is not granted on compassionate or compelling grounds—for instance, if you simply request a break without adequate documentation—the university may be obligated to cancel your CoE. A cancelled CoE triggers **visa cancellation proceedings** under section 116 of the Migration Act. In 2025, ANU reported that 14% of international student leave applications were initially assessed as not meeting the compassionate threshold, though many of these were resolved after students submitted additional evidence. The university's International Student Advisors can help you understand your specific situation before you submit your application, and this pre-application consultation is strongly recommended.

Students who depart Australia during their leave period should also consider the **visa length implications**. If your leave extends your program completion date beyond your visa expiry, you will need to apply for a new student visa before your current one expires. ANU's International Student Support team can issue a new CoE for this purpose, but you should plan this at least three months in advance to account for Department of Home Affairs processing times, which averaged 71 days for student visas in early 2026.

## Financial Considerations, Fees, and Refund Policies

Taking a leave of absence inevitably raises questions about **tuition fees and financial liability**. ANU's 2026 refund policy distinguishes between students who withdraw before the census date of the relevant semester and those who withdraw after. If you submit your temporary withdrawal application and it is approved **before the semester census date**—which in 2026 falls on 31 March for Semester 1 and 31 August for Semester 2—you will receive a full refund of any tuition fees paid for that semester, and your HELP debt (for domestic students) will be remitted. After the census date, refunds are only available in exceptional circumstances, such as medical conditions that could not reasonably have been anticipated before the census date.

Students who have paid upfront international tuition fees should be aware that **refunds are processed in Australian dollars** to the original payment source, which can take up to 30 business days. Currency fluctuations between the time of payment and refund may result in a financial loss or gain beyond ANU's control. Additionally, the university charges a **non-refundable administrative fee** of $250 for processing program leave applications from international students, though this fee may be waived in cases of demonstrated financial hardship—subject to approval by the Fees and Scholarships Office.

For students receiving **ANU scholarships or external funding**, a leave of absence can affect your scholarship entitlements. Most ANU scholarships allow for one semester of suspension without penalty, but you must notify the Scholarships Office in writing. Scholarships tied to specific academic progress milestones, such as the ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship, may require you to re-establish eligibility upon return. Students funded by their home government or external sponsors should check their specific scholarship conditions, as some sponsors will cease payments during any leave period and may not resume them automatically upon return.

## Returning to Study After an Approved Leave

The **ANU return to study after leave** process is designed to be straightforward for students whose leave was approved for one or two semesters. Approximately four to six weeks before your scheduled return semester, ANU Student Administration will send an email to your university account confirming your return and providing instructions for re-enrollment. You must respond to this communication and complete the **online re-enrollment task** in ISIS by the specified deadline, which is typically two weeks before the semester begins.

Students returning from **medical leave** face an additional requirement: you must provide a **fitness to return to study** statement from your treating health practitioner. This document should confirm that you are well enough to resume full-time study and, where relevant, outline any ongoing support needs or reasonable adjustments you require. ANU Access and Inclusion can then work with you to develop an **Education Access Plan** that formalizes accommodations such as extended assessment deadlines, note-taking support, or adjusted attendance requirements. In 2025, approximately 40% of students returning from medical leave utilized Access and Inclusion services, with a 92% satisfaction rate reported in the university's student experience survey.

If your leave extended beyond two semesters, you may need to **re-apply for admission** to your program. This does not mean you start from scratch—your prior academic credit is preserved—but you will need to meet any updated admission requirements that may have been introduced during your absence. This is particularly relevant for programs in rapidly evolving fields such as cybersecurity, data science, or public health, where curriculum changes are frequent. The ANU Admissions Office recommends contacting them at least four months before your intended return if you have been on leave for more than 12 months.

## Support Services Available During Your Leave Period

Being on leave does not mean you are cut off from the ANU community entirely. The university maintains **limited access to support services** for students on approved leave, recognizing that the circumstances prompting the leave often require ongoing support. **ANU Counselling and Wellbeing** offers up to six telehealth sessions per semester for students on medical or compassionate leave, even if you are outside Australia. This service is free and confidential, with appointments available via video call in multiple time zones.

The **ANU Dean of Students office** functions as a central point of contact for students navigating complex leave situations. The Dean of Students can advocate on your behalf with academic colleges, help mediate disputes about leave conditions, and connect you with financial assistance if you are experiencing hardship. In 2026, the Dean of Students office launched a dedicated **Leave Support Coordinator** role specifically for students on medical or compassionate leave, providing a single contact person who checks in periodically and helps coordinate your return.

For international students, the **ANU International Student Support team** continues to provide visa advice during leave periods. They can help you understand your work rights while on leave—international students on approved compassionate leave may retain limited work rights under specific conditions—and assist with any communications from the Department of Home Affairs. The team also runs a monthly online information session for students on leave, covering topics such as maintaining your mental health, navigating Australian healthcare from overseas, and preparing for academic return.

## FAQ

### How long can I take as an ANU temporary withdrawal for medical reasons?
The standard maximum is **12 months (two semesters)** for medical leave. In exceptional circumstances, such as ongoing cancer treatment or major surgery recovery, extensions of up to a further 12 months may be granted. Each extension requires new medical evidence dated within four weeks of the extension request. As of 2026, ANU policy limits total cumulative leave to **24 months across the duration of a degree program**, after which a student must apply for readmission.

### Will an ANU leave of absence affect my student visa?
If your leave is approved on **compassionate or compelling grounds**—which medical and serious personal circumstances typically satisfy—your CoE is placed on hold rather than cancelled, and your visa remains valid. You can remain in Australia or travel overseas and return. However, if your leave extends your program beyond your visa expiry date, you must apply for a **new student visa at least three months before your current visa expires**. ANU's International Student Advisors can provide a new CoE for this purpose.

### What happens to my ANU accommodation if I take a temporary withdrawal?
Students in **ANU-owned residential halls or colleges** must notify their residence head within 48 hours of their leave being approved. Depending on the timing, you may be eligible for a partial refund of accommodation fees, less a cancellation charge. Students who expect to return within the same calendar year can apply to **defer their accommodation contract** rather than cancel it, guaranteeing a room upon return. Off-campus rental arrangements are entirely separate from ANU processes and must be managed independently with your landlord or property manager.

### How does ANU medical withdrawal appear on my academic transcript?
An approved medical or personal leave appears on your transcript as **"Program Leave – Approved"** for the relevant semester. It does not show as a fail, withdrawal, or any academic penalty. When you return and complete your studies, the leave notation remains on your transcript but is generally understood by employers and other institutions as a neutral administrative record. If you have specific concerns about how this might be perceived in your industry, the **ANU Careers and Employability team** can advise on framing your study timeline in job applications.
