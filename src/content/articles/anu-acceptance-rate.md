ANU is Australia's most selective university by most measures. Its acceptance rate reflects a combination of high entry requirements, a relatively small student body (20,000 students), and the holistic admissions process that considers factors beyond raw academic scores.

## Overall Acceptance Rate

**Estimated undergraduate acceptance rate**: 35–40% (ANU does not publish an official acceptance rate; this is derived from applications-to-enrolments data).

This is comparable to selective US public universities (UC Berkeley ~15%, UCLA ~12%, University of Virginia ~23%) but is the **lowest** among Australian universities (University of Melbourne estimated at 45–55%, Sydney at 40–50%).

## Holistic Admissions Impact

ANU's holistic model — unique among Australian universities at the undergraduate level — means that academic scores are not the sole criterion. The application requires:
1. Academic transcript (Year 11 and Year 12, or equivalent)
2. Personal statement (addressing motivation, preparation, and goals)
3. Evidence of co-curricular involvement (leadership, community service, work experience)

A student with a lower ATAR but a compelling personal statement demonstrating genuine interest in their chosen field may be preferred over a student with a higher ATAR but a generic application. This model is designed to identify students who will thrive in ANU's research-intensive environment — not just those who perform best on standardised exams.

## International vs Domestic

ANU does not publish separate acceptance rates for international and domestic students. However, international student enrolment data suggests that international acceptance rates are somewhat higher (45–55%) — reflecting that ANU, like all Australian universities, actively recruits international students and has a defined international student revenue model.

## What An Acceptance Rate Tells You (And Doesn't)

Acceptance rate is a proxy for selectivity, not for quality. A low acceptance rate at ANU reflects:
- High demand relative to a fixed number of places (ANU's physical campus and Commonwealth-supported place cap limit growth)
- Holistic admissions filtering (many applicants are academically qualified but don't make the cut on the personal statement/co-curricular component)
- Canberra's smaller applicant pool means the denominator is smaller than for Melbourne or Sydney (which draw from larger metropolitan populations)

It does not necessarily mean ANU programs are "better" than Melbourne or Sydney programs — just harder to get into.
