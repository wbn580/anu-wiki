Navigating a change of academic direction at the Australian National University can feel like stepping into uncharted territory. According to the ANU 2026 Annual Course Report, approximately 18% of undergraduate students initiate at least one major or program change during their studies, and internal data from the Division of Student Administration indicates that over 70% of these changes are completed successfully without credit loss when proper procedures are followed. This guide outlines the exact pathways, policies, and practical steps to help you transition smoothly while protecting the credits you have already earned.

## Understanding the ANU Program Structure and Degree Components

**ANU degrees** are built on a flexible architecture that distinguishes between programs, majors, minors, and specializations. A **program** refers to the overarching degree, such as the Bachelor of Science or Bachelor of Arts. Within that program, you select one or more **majors**—structured sequences of at least 48 units—that define your primary field of study. A **minor** typically requires 24 units in a complementary discipline.

This layered structure is critical because changing a major within the same program follows a different administrative path than transferring to an entirely new degree program. **Internal major changes** are generally processed through the ISIS student portal and do not require a formal program transfer application, provided you remain within the same degree framework. The ANU 2026 Undergraduate Handbook confirms that students in flexible programs such as the Bachelor of Science can declare up to two majors and two minors without additional approval, giving you substantial room to explore before committing.

## ANU Program Transfer Process: When You Need to Switch Degrees Entirely

If your desired major sits within a different degree program—for example, moving from a Bachelor of Arts majoring in History to a Bachelor of Commerce majoring in Accounting—you must complete a **program transfer application** rather than a simple major change. The **ANU program transfer process** is managed through the ISIS portal under the "Manage My Degree" section, and applications are assessed against the admission requirements of the target program.

For 2026, ANU has streamlined the transfer process for current students. The university's Program Transfer Policy 2026 states that students with a GPA of 4.0 or above across at least 24 units of completed study at ANU are generally eligible for internal transfer to most undergraduate programs. Competitive programs, including the Bachelor of Philosophy (PhB) and certain double-degree combinations, may require a higher GPA threshold of 5.5 or above. You must submit your program transfer application by the **census date of the semester** in which you intend to commence the new program—31 March 2026 for Semester 1 and 31 August 2026 for Semester 2. Late applications are only considered in exceptional circumstances and require Associate Dean approval.

## ANU Credit Transfer Policy: How to Preserve Your Earned Credits

The cornerstone of changing direction without losing progress is the **ANU credit transfer policy**. Under the ANU Coursework Awards Rules 2026, students who change programs or majors can apply for **credit for prior learning** (also called advanced standing) for courses that align with the requirements of the new program. ANU uses a block-credit model in many cases: if you have completed a full major sequence in your original program, those 48 units may be counted as a completed major in the new program, provided the discipline is offered there.

**Elective credits** are particularly portable. Most ANU undergraduate degrees include between 24 and 48 units of free electives, and any course you have passed can typically be slotted into these elective spaces without question. For core or compulsory courses, the receiving College assesses whether your completed courses meet equivalent learning outcomes. The ANU 2026 Credit Transfer Guidelines specify that courses completed within the last seven years are generally eligible for credit, and there is no upper limit on the number of credits you can transfer from ANU courses, unlike external credit which is capped at 50% of the total program requirements. Submit your credit transfer application through ISIS within four weeks of your program transfer being approved to ensure your academic record is updated before the next enrollment period.

## ANU Flexible Double Degree Rules: Maximizing Your Options

One of the most powerful tools for avoiding credit loss is the **ANU flexible double degree rules**. ANU offers over 70 flexible double degree combinations that allow you to graduate with two bachelor's degrees in a condensed timeframe—typically four years instead of six—by sharing electives and overlapping requirements. If you are considering a major change that would leave you with orphaned credits, enrolling in a flexible double degree may be the optimal solution.

Under the 2026 Flexible Double Degree Framework, you can combine almost any two undergraduate programs, and the total unit requirement is reduced by 48 units compared to completing the degrees separately. This means courses you have already completed in your original major can often be counted toward one of the two degrees, while you simultaneously pursue the new field of interest. For example, a student who has completed 24 units toward a Physics major but wishes to pivot to International Relations could enroll in a Bachelor of Science / Bachelor of International Relations flexible double degree. The Physics courses would count as Science electives, and no credits are wasted. The application process for flexible double degrees follows the same program transfer timeline, and you must meet the entry requirements for both component degrees.

## ANU Major Change Deadline and Step-by-Step Procedure for 2026

Timing is everything when it comes to the **ANU major change deadline**. For internal major or minor changes that do not involve a program transfer, you can make adjustments at any point up until the **last day to add a course** in a given semester—28 February 2026 for Semester 1 and 31 July 2026 for Semester 2. However, ANU strongly recommends finalizing major declarations before the start of your third year of full-time study to ensure you can complete all requirements within the standard degree duration.

The step-by-step procedure is as follows: First, log into ISIS and navigate to "Manage My Degree" > "Major/Minor/Specialisation Declaration." Review your current declaration and select the new major or minor from the dropdown menu. The system will automatically check whether you meet any prerequisites and display a **degree audit** showing how your completed and enrolled courses map to the new requirements. If any courses cannot be automatically applied, you will see a flag indicating that manual credit transfer review is needed. Submit the declaration and, if required, lodge a separate credit transfer request through the "Academic History" section. The College typically processes internal major changes within five to ten business days. You will receive a confirmation email, and your updated study plan will appear in ISIS.

## Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Losing Credits During a Change

Despite the flexible policies, students do occasionally lose credits during a major or program change, usually due to avoidable mistakes. The most frequent issue is **failing to check course expiry rules**. While ANU courses remain eligible for credit transfer for seven years, some professional accreditation requirements—particularly in Engineering, Law, and Health Sciences—may mandate that specific courses be completed within a shorter timeframe. If you have taken a break from study or spent several years in a different major, verify with the relevant College that your older courses still satisfy current accreditation standards.

Another common pitfall is **assuming all 1000-level courses are interchangeable**. Introductory courses in different disciplines may have similar titles but cover distinct content that does not map to the new major's requirements. For instance, STAT1008 Quantitative Research Methods may not substitute for STAT1003 Statistical Techniques in a Psychology major, even though both are first-year statistics courses. Always consult the **program convenor** or the academic advising team at ANU Student Central before finalizing your change. The university's 2026 Academic Progress Policy also warns that changing majors too late in your degree—after completing 96 units or more—may extend your time to graduation, though it will not result in credit loss if the courses fit within elective allowances.

## How Academic Advising Can Safeguard Your Credits

**ANU's academic advising services** are an underutilized resource that can make the difference between a seamless transition and a frustrating credit dispute. The university employs dedicated **program convenors** within each College who are responsible for assessing major and program change requests and determining credit applicability. Meeting with your convenor before initiating any change allows you to obtain a preliminary evaluation of how your completed courses will be treated in the new structure.

Additionally, ANU Student Central offers drop-in advising sessions and one-on-one appointments where advisors can run a **simulated degree audit** using the same tools available to College assessors. This simulation shows exactly which courses will count toward the new major, which will be relegated to electives, and whether any gaps exist that might require additional study. According to the ANU 2026 Student Experience Survey, students who consulted an academic advisor before changing majors reported a 92% satisfaction rate with the credit transfer outcome, compared to 68% among those who navigated the process independently. These services are available free of charge to all enrolled students, and appointments can be booked through the ANU website or by visiting the Student Central office on the Acton campus.

## FAQ

**What is the deadline to change my major at ANU in 2026 without losing credits?**
For internal major changes within the same program, you can update your declaration until the last day to add courses each semester—28 February 2026 for Semester 1 and 31 July 2026 for Semester 2. Program transfers that involve switching to a different degree must be submitted by the census date, which is 31 March 2026 for Semester 1 or 31 August 2026 for Semester 2. Submitting after these dates risks having credits from the current semester excluded from the new program structure.

**How many credits can I transfer when changing programs at ANU?**
There is no fixed upper limit on the number of ANU credits you can transfer internally, unlike external credit which is capped at 50% of the total program. If you have completed 72 units in your original program and those courses satisfy requirements in the new program—whether as core courses, major requirements, or electives—all 72 units can be transferred. The ANU 2026 Credit Transfer Guidelines confirm that courses completed within the last seven years remain eligible.

**Can I change to a double degree at ANU and still graduate within four years?**
Yes, ANU's flexible double degree structure is designed for completion in four years of full-time study. The standard requirement is 192 units, which is 48 units fewer than completing the two degrees separately. If you have already completed courses that count toward either degree, your timeline may remain unchanged. However, if you are changing direction after completing more than 96 units in an unrelated field, you should consult a program convenor to map out your remaining study plan, as some additional time may be needed.

**What happens to my completed courses if they do not fit into the new major at ANU?**
Courses that do not meet specific major or program requirements are automatically counted as free electives, provided your new program includes elective space. Most ANU undergraduate degrees allocate between 24 and 48 units to electives. If you have more orphaned credits than the elective allowance, you may consider switching to a flexible double degree to accommodate the extra coursework. No completed ANU course is simply discarded; it will appear on your academic transcript and contribute to your overall unit count unless it exceeds the maximum allowed for the program.
