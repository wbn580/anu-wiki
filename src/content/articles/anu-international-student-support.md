ANU has one of Australia's most comprehensive international student support ecosystems. With approximately 8,500 international students from 130+ countries, the university invests significantly in dedicated international student services.

## Key Support Services

### ANU International Office
The primary point of contact for international students:
- Pre-departure guidance (visa, accommodation, enrolment)
- Orientation programs (ANU Welcome, International Student Briefing)
- Ongoing advice on visa compliance, OSHC, and academic requirements
- Crisis support and emergency assistance
- Location: Ground floor, Di Riddell Student Centre

### Academic Skills
- **Academic Skills and Learning Centre (ASLC)**: Free one-to-one consultations, workshops, and online resources on academic writing, referencing, time management, and exam preparation
- **English language support**: ANU College of English language programs and free English conversation groups during semester

### Health and Wellbeing

**ANU Counselling**: Free, confidential counselling (up to 6 sessions per semester). Available in person and via telehealth. Crisis appointments available same-day.

**ANU Medical Centre**: Bulk-billed GP services on campus. International students with OSHC can access with minimal out-of-pocket costs.

**ANU Wellbeing**: Workshops on stress management, mindfulness, sleep, and healthy relationships.

### Career Support

**ANU Careers**: Career counselling, resume reviews, mock interviews, job board (ANU CareerHub). Dedicated international student career advisor.

**ANU+, ANU Global Programs**: Volunteer registration, internships, and global study opportunities that count toward the Australian National Internships Program and the ANU Global Certificate.

### International Student Wellbeing Team

A dedicated team within the International Office focusing on:
- Homesickness and cultural adjustment
- Academic transition support
- Financial hardship (emergency grants)
- Family and dependant support
