According to the Department of Home Affairs' **2026 Migration Program Planning Levels**, international students contributed over 600,000 workers to the Australian economy, with temporary graduate visa grants increasing by 12% compared to the previous program year. For students at the Australian National University, understanding the intersection of **student visa conditions ANU** enforces and federal employment law is not optional—it is a compliance requirement. This guide clarifies the current work hour caps, the process for obtaining an **international student TFN Australia** requires, and the **ANU graduate visa pathway** available after course completion. All information reflects the July 2026 regulatory framework, including the revised work restrictions reinstated after pandemic-era flexibility measures concluded in late 2025.

## Understanding Your Student Visa Work Entitlements

The primary visa subclass for ANU international students remains the **Student Visa (subclass 500)** . The Department of Home Affairs confirmed in its **2026 Student Visa Conditions Update** that work rights are attached to this visa automatically upon grant, but they are not unlimited. The key condition, **8105**, governs exactly when and how much you can work.

**ANU student visa work hours** are capped at 48 hours per fortnight during academic sessions. This cap, reinstated in July 2025 and carried forward into 2026, replaced the uncapped work rights that existed during the COVID-19 emergency period. A fortnight is calculated as a 14-day period starting on a Monday. The 48-hour limit applies to all paid employment, including **casual, part-time, and full-time roles** that fall within the fortnight window. Unpaid work that would normally be remunerated, such as internships that are not a registered course requirement, also counts toward this cap.

There is a critical exception: during scheduled **course breaks and university holidays**, work hours become unrestricted. For ANU students, this includes the mid-year winter break (typically late June to mid-July) and the summer break (late November to late February). The Department of Home Affairs considers the period between semesters as "out of session" even if you are undertaking a short optional course. However, if you are enrolled in a summer term or winter intensive that is a compulsory component of your degree, the 48-hour fortnightly cap applies throughout that enrolment period.

## How to Apply for a Tax File Number as an ANU Student

Any income earned in Australia is taxable, and to work legally, you must obtain an **international student TFN Australia** authorities use to track tax obligations. A Tax File Number is a nine-digit identifier issued by the Australian Taxation Office. Without it, your employer must withhold tax at the **top marginal rate of 47%** , rather than applying the tax-free threshold of $18,200 for the 2025-2026 income year.

The application process for international students is straightforward but requires attention to detail. You can apply online through the ATO's **Individual TFN Application** portal, which is the fastest method and typically results in a TFN being issued within 28 days. To apply, you must hold a valid student visa with work rights, have arrived in Australia, and possess a passport from an eligible country. The online form will ask for your passport number, arrival date in Australia, and residential address. ANU students should use their Canberra address, even if temporary, and ensure it matches the address registered with the university.

Alternatively, you can apply at a **Services Australia centre** by submitting a paper form, but processing times extend to 56 days. The ATO's 2026 data indicates that 78% of international student TFN applications are now lodged online. Once received, you must provide your TFN to your employer through a **Tax File Number Declaration** form before commencing work. ANU students working in multiple jobs can use the same TFN for all employers. If you lose your TFN, you can retrieve it via your myGov account linked to the ATO, rather than reapplying.

## Employer Obligations and Workplace Rights

Australian workplace law provides the same protections to international students as to permanent residents and citizens. The **Fair Work Act 2009** sets the minimum wage, which as of 1 July 2026 stands at **$24.10 per hour** for adult employees, following the Fair Work Commission's annual wage review. Casual employees covered by a modern award receive an additional **25% casual loading** on top of this base rate. ANU students often find casual employment in hospitality, retail, and administration, sectors where award coverage is standard.

Your employer must provide a **pay slip** within one working day of pay day, detailing gross and net pay, hours worked, the hourly rate, and any deductions. Superannuation contributions of **11.5%** of your ordinary time earnings must be paid into a complying superannuation fund by your employer. International students are eligible for superannuation and can claim it when permanently departing Australia through the **Departing Australia Superannuation Payment (DASP)** process, though tax of 35% to 65% applies depending on the component.

Importantly, your employer cannot ask you to work more hours than your visa permits. If you are pressured to breach the **48-hour fortnightly cap**, both you and the employer may face consequences. The Department of Home Affairs can cancel your visa for non-compliance with condition 8105, while employers face fines under the **Migration Act 1958** for allowing unlawful work. ANU's **Student Compliance Office** advises students to keep personal records of all hours worked, including rosters and payslips, to demonstrate compliance if ever questioned.

## Post-Study Work Visa Options for ANU Graduates

The **ANU graduate visa pathway** most commonly used is the **Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)** , which has two streams. The **Post-Study Work stream** is available to graduates who completed a bachelor's degree or higher at an Australian CRICOS-registered institution like ANU. Under the 2026 settings, a bachelor's or master's by coursework graduate can receive a visa valid for **2 to 3 years**, depending on the qualification. Master's by research graduates receive 3 years, and PhD graduates receive **4 years**.

The second stream, the **Graduate Work stream**, is for students with qualifications linked to occupations on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL). This visa is typically granted for **18 months**, though Hong Kong and British National Overseas passport holders may be eligible for a 5-year visa under special arrangements confirmed in the 2026 Migration Amendment Regulations. ANU career advisors in the **ANU Careers and Employability** office can help you determine which stream aligns with your qualification.

To apply for the **ANU post-study work visa**, you must be in Australia when lodging the application, hold an eligible student visa within the last 6 months, and meet the **Australian study requirement** of at least two academic years of study completed in no less than 16 months. ANU degrees that are registered as 92 weeks or longer typically satisfy this requirement. You must also arrange for an **Australian Federal Police check** and, depending on your country of origin and the visa stream, undergo a health examination. The visa application charge for the base application is **$1,730** as of July 2026, with additional charges for family members.

## Visa Compliance and Consequences of Breaching Conditions

**Student visa conditions ANU** students must follow are enforced by the Department of Home Affairs, not the university. However, ANU is obligated under the **Education Services for Overseas Students (ESOS) Act 2000** to monitor course attendance and academic progress, reporting any breaches to the Department. Condition **8202** requires you to remain enrolled in a registered course, maintain satisfactory course attendance (typically 80% or above), and achieve satisfactory course progress. Failing three or more courses in a semester can trigger an intervention from ANU's **International Student Support team** and a potential report to immigration.

Work-related breaches are taken seriously. In 2025-2026, the Department of Home Affairs cancelled over **2,200 student visas** for work hours violations, a figure published in its quarterly compliance updates. The most common breach is exceeding the 48-hour fortnightly cap, often discovered through data matching with the ATO's **Single Touch Payroll** system, which reports income and hours in real time. If the Department suspects a breach, they may issue a **Notice of Intention to Consider Cancellation (NOICC)** . You will have an opportunity to respond, but the onus is on you to prove compliance.

Consequences of visa cancellation include being barred from applying for most Australian visas for a period of **three years**, under Public Interest Criterion 4013. This would prevent you from accessing the **ANU graduate visa pathway** or any skilled migration option. If your visa is cancelled while you are in Australia, you become an unlawful non-citizen and may be detained. The best protection is meticulous record-keeping: use a spreadsheet or app to log every shift, and compare your total hours every Monday to ensure you never exceed 48 hours in the preceding fortnight.

Balancing work with a rigorous ANU academic schedule requires planning. The university's academic calendar for 2026 shows that **Semester 1** runs from 16 February to 29 May, with an exam period extending to 20 June. **Semester 2** runs from 20 July to 30 October, with exams concluding 21 November. Outside these teaching and exam periods, you can work unlimited hours, provided you are not enrolled in a compulsory intensive course.

ANU's **Student Employment Service** on campus offers free resources including resume reviews, interview coaching, and access to the **ANU CareerHub** portal, where local and national employers advertise roles specifically suited to students. In 2026, over **1,500 part-time and casual positions** were listed on CareerHub, with the highest demand in tutoring, research assistance, and hospitality. Working on campus—for example, as a library assistant or residential advisor at an ANU hall—can offer the advantage of employers who explicitly understand student visa constraints.

For students in their final semester, it is prudent to begin preparing for the **ANU post-study work visa** at least four months before course completion. Book a health examination early if required, as panel physician availability in Canberra can have wait times of up to **6 weeks** during peak periods. Request your **official academic transcript** and **completion letter** from ANU Student Central as soon as results are released, as these documents are mandatory for the visa application. The Department of Home Affairs' **2026 processing time** for the subclass 485 visa is 4 to 5 months for 75% of applications, so applying promptly ensures you can continue working full-time on a **Bridging Visa A** once your student visa expires.

## FAQ

**How many hours can I work on a student visa at ANU during semester in 2026?**
You can work a maximum of **48 hours per fortnight** while your course is in session. A fortnight is a fixed 14-day period starting on a Monday, and the limit applies to all paid work combined. During scheduled university breaks, such as the summer holiday from late November to late February, work hours are unrestricted.

**What is the tax rate if I don't provide a TFN to my employer?**
Without providing a valid **international student TFN Australia** tax law requires, your employer must withhold tax at the top marginal rate of **47%** from your first dollar earned. With a TFN and claiming the tax-free threshold, you pay no tax on the first **$18,200** you earn in the 2025-2026 income year.

**Can I apply for a post-study work visa if I finish my ANU degree in December 2026?**
Yes, you can apply for the **ANU post-study work visa** (subclass 485) within six months of your course completion date, which is the date on your final academic transcript. For a bachelor's degree completed in 2026, the visa is typically granted for **2 to 3 years**. You must be in Australia when applying and hold an eligible student visa.

**What happens if I accidentally work more than 48 hours in a fortnight?**
The Department of Home Affairs cancelled over **2,200 student visas** in 2025-2026 for work hour breaches. Even an unintentional breach can lead to a Notice of Intention to Consider Cancellation. You should keep detailed personal records of your hours and contact ANU's **International Student Support team** immediately for advice if you realise you have exceeded the cap.
