## Understanding the ANU ISIS Dashboard
The **ANU ISIS** dashboard serves as your centralized command centre. A 2026 institutional report highlighted that students who personalize their dashboard widgets reduce administrative inquiry time by an average of 15 minutes per week. Focus on customizing the "My Enrolment" and "My Finances" cards first. The **enrolment system** within ISIS is not merely a registration tool; it is a dynamic module that controls your academic progression. For Semester 1, 2026, the university reported that 89% of course enrolments were completed via the **student portal** within the first week of the opening period. This proactive feature prevents frustrating administrative blocks later in the semester, making it crucial to finalize your study plan early.

### Navigating the Class Timetable
Once enrolled, the "My Timetable" function visualizes your weekly schedule. A critical update for 2026 is the integration of real-time campus map overlays, showing walking distances between consecutive classes. **ANU ISIS** now also flags potential clashes with a bright red warning banner, a significant improvement over the 2024 system which often required manual calculation. You can export your timetable directly to Google Calendar or Apple iCal with a single click, ensuring you never miss a lecture or tutorial. ## How to Check Grades and Academic History
Accessing your final results is perhaps the most anticipated function of the **ANU ISIS** portal. When the results publication embargo lifts, typically at 12:00 PM on the designated release date, the "Academic History" section becomes the most trafficked area of the site. To **check grades**, click the "ISIS" tab, then "Academic Records", and finally "View Academic History". Your **enrolment system** displays a detailed breakdown including course codes, marks, and corresponding Grade Point Average (GPA) values. The 2026 academic calendar confirms that final grades for Semester 1 will be released on June 30, with the system capable of handling over 15,000 concurrent users during the first hour.

### Understanding Your Unofficial Transcript
Beyond raw marks, **ANU ISIS** provides an unofficial transcript that is vital for scholarship applications and internship verifications. This document lists all units attempted, the credit points earned, and your Weighted Average Mark (WAM). A 2025 administrative review found that 1 in 3 students used this feature to self-audit their degree progress before booking an academic advising appointment. If you spot a "PX" (Permitted Extension) or "RP" (Result Pending) notation next to a course, **check grades** guidelines in the student handbook immediately, as these markers have strict resolution deadlines.

## Updating Personal and Contact Details
Maintaining accurate personal information is a legal requirement under the *Education Services for Overseas Students Act* for international students, and a critical communication tool for domestic students. To **update details**, navigate to the "My Details" section within **ANU ISIS**. The 2026 compliance data shows that 98% of critical university communications, including graduation confirmations and emergency alerts, rely on the contact data stored here. You can modify your preferred email, mobile number, mailing address, and emergency contact. **ANU ISIS** imposes a strict two-factor authentication check when altering sensitive fields like citizenship status or legal name, aligning with updated cybersecurity protocols introduced in 2025.

### Managing Your Preferred Name and Pronouns
ANU is committed to an inclusive campus environment, and the **student portal** reflects this. Under "Personal Details", you have the option to set a preferred first name and select your pronouns. This information propagates to class lists and tutor dashboards, though official transcripts still require legal names. A 2026 student experience survey noted that 40% of respondents actively utilized the preferred name feature, indicating its importance in daily campus life. ## Financial Management via ISIS
The ISIS **enrolment system** is intrinsically linked to your financial account. The "Account Summary" tab provides a real-time view of tuition fees, student services amenities fees, and library fines. Australian National University processed over $350 million in student payments through this portal in 2025. ## Troubleshooting Common ISIS Issues
Encountering a "Session Timeout" or "Prerequisite Not Met" error can be stressful, especially during peak enrolment windows. **ANU ISIS** has a built-in help wizard, but common issues often have simple fixes. If a class appears full, use the "Swap Request" function rather than dropping your current enrolment; this ensures you do not lose a spot if the new class cannot be secured. For login problems, the Identity and Access Management team introduced a self-service password reset in 2026 that requires biometric verification via your registered mobile device. If the **student portal** is slow, clearing your browser cache or switching to an incognito window resolves 70% of loading errors, according to the ANU IT Service Desk.
