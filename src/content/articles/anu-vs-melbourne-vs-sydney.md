International students choosing between Australia's top universities — ANU (Canberra), University of Melbourne, and University of Sydney — face a complex decision. Rankings alone obscure differences in location, cost, teaching model, and student experience.

## Rankings Snapshot

- **QS 2026**: 31 (#1 AU) · 33 (#2) · 41 (#3)
- **THE 2026**: 67 · 37 · 61
- **ARWU 2025**: 77 · 35 · 73
- **QS Employability**: 79 · 8 · 4


## The Melbourne Model Difference

Melbourne uses the **Melbourne Model**: undergraduates complete a broad bachelor degree (Arts, Science, Commerce, etc.) and professional qualifications (Law, Medicine, Engineering, Architecture) are only available as postgraduate degrees. This means:
- You cannot study law, medicine, or engineering as an undergraduate at Melbourne
- Melbourne undergraduates have more flexibility to explore disciplines before committing
- Postgraduate professional degrees are 3–4 years, not 1–2 as at most other universities

**ANU and Sydney offer direct-entry professional programs** — you can start Law, Engineering, Medicine, or any professional program directly from high school.

## Location

- **City size**: 450K · 5.2M · 5.4M
- **Campus location**: CBD (integrated) · Parkville (inner suburb) · Camperdown (inner city)
- **Cost of living (weekly)**: A$450–650 · A$500–700 · A$550–800
- **Part-time job availability**: Moderate · High · High
- **Nightlife/social**: Modest · Excellent (Melbourne culture) · Excellent
- **Weather**: Cold winter (-5°C to 12°C), warm summer · Variable, "four seasons in a day" · Warm (8°C to 26°C)


## Student Experience

**ANU**: Residential college community, smaller campus feel, close access to national institutions. Best for students who value a tight-knit academic community and don't need a big-city experience.

**Melbourne**: Large, diverse campus in a vibrant cultural city. Best for students who want access to Australia's arts, food, and cultural capital.

**Sydney**: Iconic campus in Australia's global city. Best for students who want proximity to industry (finance, tech, professional services) and iconic Australian lifestyle.

## Graduate Outcomes (QILT Graduate Outcomes Survey)

Median starting salary (undergraduate, full-time):
- Melbourne: A$64,000–68,000
- Sydney: A$62,000–67,000
- ANU: A$62,000–68,000

Employment rates are broadly similar across the three. ANU's public policy and international relations graduates have particularly strong placement into the Australian Public Service (Canberra-based).

## Decision Framework

- **Choose ANU** if: You're interested in politics/government/international relations, want a smaller campus community, or want lower cost of living.
- **Choose Melbourne** if: You're open to the Melbourne Model, want a cultural city, or are interested in a broad undergraduate education before specialising.
- **Choose Sydney** if: You want a professional undergraduate program, proximity to industry, or the iconic Australian urban experience.
