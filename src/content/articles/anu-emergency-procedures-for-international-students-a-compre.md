## Understanding the ANU Crisis Protocol Framework

The **ANU crisis protocol** operates under a unified command structure designed to coordinate responses across campus and residential halls. When an emergency occurs, the University activates its Emergency Control Organisation, comprising building wardens, floor wardens, and a central Emergency Operations Centre. International students should know that all emergency communications are issued in English and simplified Chinese, with plans to add Hindi and Vietnamese translations by late 2026.

The University classifies emergencies into three tiers. Tier 1 involves localised incidents such as a small fire or medical episode. Tier 2 covers campus-wide threats like hazardous material spills or active security situations. Tier 3 addresses region-scale disasters, including bushfires threatening multiple suburbs. Each tier triggers specific notification channels. **ANU emergency support** staff coordinate with ACT Emergency Services, ensuring international students receive consistent instructions regardless of their accommodation type.
