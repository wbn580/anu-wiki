Understanding your work rights as an international student at the Australian National University is essential for maintaining visa compliance while gaining valuable professional experience. According to the Department of Home Affairs' 2026 data, over 560,000 international student visa holders were in Australia as of March 2026, with ANU hosting approximately 8,200 international students from more than 130 countries. The **Australian student visa (subclass 500)** framework has undergone significant refinements since the 2024 migration strategy updates, making it crucial to stay informed about current regulations. This guide examines the specific work entitlements, internship pathways, and post-study opportunities available to ANU students, drawing on the latest legislative instruments and university policies effective for the 2026 academic year.

## Understanding the Subclass 500 Visa Work Conditions

The **subclass 500 student visa** is the primary visa category for international students enrolled at ANU. Under the 2026 conditions set by the Department of Home Affairs, primary student visa holders can work up to **48 hours per fortnight** during study periods. This cap applies to all paid employment, whether on-campus or off-campus, and includes casual, part-time, and contract roles. The fortnight is calculated as a 14-day period commencing on any Monday, meaning you cannot average hours across multiple fortnights to circumvent the limit.

Importantly, **no work limits apply during scheduled course breaks**. ANU's academic calendar typically includes a mid-year winter break from late June to mid-July and a summer break from late November to late February. During these periods, you may work unlimited hours without breaching visa conditions. Doctoral students at ANU generally do not have defined semester breaks, but the Department recognises periods when coursework and formal research milestones are not actively underway. The **48-hour fortnightly cap** was reintroduced in mid-2023 after a temporary relaxation during the COVID-19 pandemic, and the 2026 Migration Amendment Regulations confirm this limit remains in place.

## On-Campus Employment Opportunities at ANU

ANU provides a range of on-campus employment pathways that align naturally with student schedules. The **ANU Careers and Employability** platform lists positions across university libraries, administrative offices, research centres, and student services. Common roles include **library assistants, research aides, student ambassadors, and residential hall advisors**. These positions offer the advantage of proximity to classes and supervisors who understand academic commitments.

The university's **Student Employment Guidelines** for 2026 stipulate that casual professional staff rates start at **$31.82 per hour** for Level 1 classifications, increasing with responsibility and experience. International students employed directly by ANU must still adhere to the 48-hour fortnightly limit during teaching periods, and the university's payroll systems are configured to flag potential breaches. Additionally, ANU operates a **Student Casual Employment Register** where departments source temporary staff for events, open days, and administrative peaks. Registering through the ANU HR system ensures you receive notifications when suitable vacancies arise.

## Internships and Work-Integrated Learning Under Visa Conditions

Internships represent a critical bridge between academic study and professional practice, but the visa implications require careful navigation. ANU offers **work-integrated learning (WIL) placements** across disciplines including engineering, business, law, and public policy. When an internship forms a **mandatory component of your enrolled course**, the work hours do not count toward your 48-hour fortnightly cap. The Department of Home Affairs classifies such placements as part of your study requirements, provided they are registered with the university and formally assessed.

For **elective or voluntary internships** that are not course-mandated, the standard work restrictions apply fully. ANU's **Global Programs** office recommends that students undertaking optional internships during semester limit their hours to remain within the 48-hour threshold, inclusive of any other paid employment. The **Fair Work Ombudsman** has clarified in 2026 guidance that unpaid internships are lawful only when they meet the definition of a vocational placement under the Fair Work Act 2009. ANU students should verify through their faculty that any unpaid placement satisfies these criteria, including the requirement that no employment relationship exists and the placement primarily benefits the student.

## Navigating the 48-Hour Fortnightly Cap: Practical Strategies

Managing the **48-hour fortnightly work limit** requires proactive planning, particularly if you hold multiple casual positions. The Department of Home Affairs uses an automated data-matching system with the Australian Taxation Office to monitor work hours, and **breaches can result in visa cancellation**. In the 2025-2026 financial year, over 1,200 student visa cancellations were linked to work hour violations, underscoring the seriousness of compliance.

A practical approach involves maintaining a **personal work diary** that tracks shifts across all employers. Several mobile applications designed for international students can calculate fortnightly totals based on your roster entries. ANU's **International Student Support Services** also offers free advisory sessions where you can review your work patterns with an immigration-specialist advisor. If you anticipate exceeding the cap due to an employer's rostering error, document the circumstances immediately and seek guidance from a registered migration agent. Voluntary disclosure through the Department's online portal may mitigate enforcement action, though each case is assessed individually.

## Post-Study Work Rights: The Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)

Upon completing your ANU qualification, the **Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)** provides a pathway to extended work rights in Australia. The Post-Study Work stream, available to graduates of bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees, grants **two to four years of full work rights** depending on your qualification level and study location. ANU's Acton campus in Canberra falls within a Category 2 regional area, which may confer additional eligibility under the Second Post-Study Work stream.

The 2026 policy parameters specify that **bachelor's graduates receive two years**, **master's by coursework graduates receive two years**, **master's by research graduates receive three years**, and **doctoral graduates receive four years**. Applicants must hold a valid student visa at the time of application, have completed their course within six months of applying, and meet the **Australian study requirement** of at least two academic years of study. ANU's academic programs are registered on CRICOS for durations exceeding 92 weeks, satisfying this criterion. The visa application fee for the subclass 485 is **$1,895 as of July 2026**, and processing times currently average four to six months.

## Skilled Migration Pathways After ANU Graduation

For ANU graduates seeking permanent residency, the post-study work period provides valuable time to accumulate points and employer sponsorship. The **General Skilled Migration (GSM) program** awards points for Australian qualifications, with ANU's strong reputation contributing positively under the points-tested system. Canberra's status as a **regional centre** under the migration framework means ANU graduates may access state nomination through the ACT Migration Program, which maintains a dedicated occupation list updated annually.

The **Employer Nomination Scheme (subclass 186)** and the **Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (subclass 494)** visa offer employer-driven pathways. ANU's **Alumni Mentoring Program** connects graduates with industry professionals, many of whom work in organisations that sponsor international talent. The 2026 ACT Occupation List includes high-demand fields such as **data science, renewable energy engineering, cybersecurity, and health policy**, disciplines where ANU holds particular research and teaching strengths. Graduates should note that the points test now places greater weight on **skilled employment experience** and **partner skills**, making strategic career planning during the post-study work period essential.

## Compliance and Risk Management for International Students

Maintaining visa compliance while working requires attention to documentation and communication. The Department of Home Affairs expects students to notify their education provider within **seven days** of commencing employment, and ANU's **International Student Compliance Portal** facilitates this reporting. Your visa grant letter contains **condition 8104 or 8105**, which explicitly states your work entitlements, and you should retain a digital copy accessible on your mobile device.

The **ANU Student Ombudsman** handles complaints related to workplace exploitation, a recognised risk for international students unfamiliar with Australian employment law. In 2026, the Fair Work Ombudsman reported that international students filed **over 3,400 wage theft complaints**, predominantly in hospitality and retail sectors. ANU has partnered with the **ACT WorkSafe** agency to deliver mandatory workplace rights sessions during orientation week. These sessions cover minimum wage entitlements, which for adults stand at **$23.23 per hour** under the 2026 national minimum wage, superannuation contributions of **11%**, and the right to join a union without employer interference.

## FAQ

**Can ANU students work more than 48 hours per fortnight during exam periods?**
No. The 48-hour fortnightly cap applies throughout all teaching periods, including examination and assessment weeks. The Department of Home Affairs does not recognise exam periods as scheduled course breaks. Only during ANU's official semester breaks, as published in the 2026 academic calendar, may students work unlimited hours. If you have a mandatory internship scheduled during exams, the hours are exempt from the cap, but any separate paid employment remains restricted.

**What happens if I accidentally exceed the 48-hour work limit by a small margin?**
The Department of Home Affairs assesses breaches on a case-by-case basis. A single inadvertent breach of fewer than four hours, reported voluntarily and accompanied by evidence of the employer's rostering error, may result in a warning rather than visa cancellation. However, the 2025-2026 compliance data shows that repeated or significant breaches led to visa cancellation in 78% of cases. ANU's International Student Support Services can assist with making a voluntary disclosure and preparing a statutory declaration explaining the circumstances.

**How long does the post-study work visa take to process for ANU graduates in 2026?**
As of June 2026, the Department of Home Affairs reports a median processing time of 127 days for the subclass 485 Post-Study Work stream, with 75% of applications finalised within 168 days. ANU graduates who apply immediately after receiving their completion letter typically receive a bridging visa that maintains lawful status and full work rights while the substantive application is assessed. The bridging visa activates automatically upon lodgement of a valid subclass 485 application.

**Are PhD students at ANU subject to the same work restrictions as coursework students?**
Yes. Doctoral candidates holding a subclass 500 visa are subject to the same 48-hour fortnightly work cap during their candidature. However, the Department of Home Affairs recognises that PhD students do not have traditional semester breaks. ANU's Dean of Higher Degree Research can issue a letter confirming periods when formal research activities are reduced, such as between thesis submission and examination, during which unlimited work may be permitted. Each determination is individual and requires prior consultation with your supervisory panel.
