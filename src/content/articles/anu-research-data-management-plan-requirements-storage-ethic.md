## Understanding the ANU Research Data Management Plan Framework

The **ANU data management plan** serves as a living document that outlines how research data will be handled throughout the project lifecycle. Unlike many Australian universities that treat data management plans as optional, ANU mandates them for all HDR candidates at the confirmation of candidature stage. The framework requires candidates to address six core domains: data collection methods, storage and backup protocols, ethical considerations, access and sharing arrangements, retention periods, and destruction procedures. Each domain must align with the *Australian Code for the Responsible Conduct of Research*, which received its most recent update in late 2025. **ANU research data storage** requirements specify that primary working data must reside on university-approved infrastructure, with cloud services requiring explicit IT governance approval. Candidates who fail to submit an approved plan before their milestone review face automatic candidature probation, affecting scholarship eligibility and enrolment status.

## FAQ

### How long does ANU ethics approval typically take for HDR research projects involving human participants?
The ANU Human Research Ethics Committee processes standard applications within 8-12 weeks as of 2026. Projects involving vulnerable populations, Indigenous communities, or sensitive health data may require 16-20 weeks due to additional review requirements. Candidates should submit ethics applications at least 3 months before planned data collection to avoid delays in the confirmation milestone, which typically occurs 9-12 months into candidature.

### What happens if my ANU data management plan is rejected during the milestone review?
If a milestone review panel identifies significant deficiencies in your data management plan, you will receive a conditional outcome requiring resubmission within 4 weeks. During this period, candidature continues but scholarship payments may be suspended until compliance is demonstrated. ANU data from 2025 shows that 22% of milestone delays were attributed to data management documentation issues, with most resolved within the 4-week remediation window.

### Can I use external cloud storage services like Dropbox or Google Drive for my research data?
ANU policy as of 2026 prohibits the use of consumer-grade cloud services for primary research data storage. Approved alternatives include the ANU Research Data Storage Service, CloudStor, and institutionally licensed platforms such as Microsoft OneDrive for Business. Exceptions require formal risk assessment through the Digital Research Infrastructure team, with approval documented in your data management plan.

### How much research data storage is provided to ANU HDR candidates at no cost?
Each HDR candidate receives 5 terabytes of active storage through the ANU Research Data Storage Service. Additional capacity can be requested through project-based allocations, with the average approved increase being 3-8 terabytes depending on research discipline and data generation rates. Candidates generating large-scale datasets, such as those in genomics or astronomy, may access up to 50 terabytes through co-investment arrangements with their academic college.

### What are the minimum data retention periods for ANU research data after thesis submission?
ANU's 2026 Research Data Retention Schedule requires minimum preservation of 5 years for most research data, 15 years for clinical trial data, and permanent retention for data identified as having significant community or cultural value. These periods commence from the date of thesis submission or last publication, whichever is later. Candidates must specify retention periods and destruction procedures in their data management plan before the final milestone review.
