ANU College of Law is Australia's #1 ranked law school (QS subject rankings) and the national law school — deeply connected to the Australian Government, High Court, and parliamentary system.

## Programs

**Bachelor of Laws (Honours)** — 4 years. Direct-entry undergraduate law degree. Entry: ATAR 97+. Unique feature: embedded Honours (research thesis in final year). ANU's LLB is a stand-alone degree, not a double-degree programme.

**Flexible Double Law** — LLB(Hons) combined with any ANU degree (Arts, Science, Commerce, IR, etc.). Duration: 5 years. Over 25 available combinations.

**Juris Doctor (JD)** — 3 years. Graduate-entry law degree for students who already hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline.

**Master of Laws (LLM)** — 1 year. Specialisations: International Law, Public Law, Law Reform and Social Justice, and Commercial Law.

## Graduate Outcomes

Law graduates from ANU have the highest public sector placement rate of any Australian law school. Approximately 40% enter government legal roles: Attorney-General's Department, Office of Parliamentary Counsel, DPP, DFAT legal branch, or legal roles in the Australian Public Service. Private sector: top-tier commercial firms (Allens, King & Wood Mallesons, Ashurst) also recruit actively from ANU.
