## Understanding Student Visa Condition 8105: Work Hour Limits at ANU

**Condition 8105** is the cornerstone of work-related compliance for international students in Australia. As of 2026, the Department of Home Affairs mandates that student visa holders can work a maximum of **48 hours per fortnight** during academic sessions. This replaced the earlier 40-hour limit in 2023, reflecting adjustments to support student welfare while balancing study commitments. At ANU, the academic calendar defines session periods, including Semester 1 (February to June) and Semester 2 (July to November). During scheduled breaks, such as the mid-year winter recess and summer holidays, work hours become **unrestricted**, allowing students to take on full-time employment.

**ANU student visa work hours** compliance requires careful tracking. A fortnight is calculated as a rolling 14-day period, not aligned with calendar weeks or pay cycles. For example, if you work 30 hours in Week 1 and 18 in Week 2, you meet the limit. However, exceeding it even once can trigger a breach. The 2026 ANU International Student Handbook emphasizes that **paid and unpaid work** both count toward the cap, including internships, clinical placements, and voluntary roles that would normally be paid. Postgraduate research students on stipends should note that their scholarship activities are generally not considered work, but external employment still falls under the 48-hour rule.

**Key strategies for compliance** include maintaining a personal timesheet, syncing work rosters with the ANU academic calendar, and using the university’s **Student Services** for advice. In 2026, ANU reported that 15% of international student visa compliance checks flagged work hour discrepancies, often due to casual jobs with variable shifts. If you’re unsure whether a specific activity counts as work, consult the **ANU Compliance Office** before proceeding.

## ANU Enrolment Conditions for Visa Holders: Full-Time Study and Course Progress

International students at ANU must meet strict **ANU enrolment conditions visa** requirements to maintain their student status. The primary obligation is enrolling in and maintaining a **full-time study load**, defined as 24 units per semester for undergraduate students and 18 to 24 units for postgraduate coursework programs, depending on the degree structure. In 2026, ANU’s Academic Progress Policy highlights that **unsatisfactory course progress**—failing 50% or more of enrolled units in a semester—can lead to intervention and potential reporting to the Department of Home Affairs.

**Enrolment conditions** also govern how students can change courses. Under the **Education Services for Overseas Students (ESOS) Act**, ANU must ensure that a change of program does not extend the visa duration beyond reasonable limits. If you’re considering a transfer to a lower AQF level or a non-CRICOS registered program, you must obtain a release letter from ANU and secure a new Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE). The 2026 data from ANU’s International Strategy Office shows that 7% of visa compliance issues stem from students reducing their study load without approval, often for financial or personal reasons. **Reduced study loads** are only permitted in exceptional circumstances, such as medical conditions with documented evidence, and must be approved by the **ANU Dean of Students** before the census date.

Another critical element is **CoE validity**. Your visa is tied to a specific CoE, and if your enrolment ends—whether through graduation, deferral, or cancellation—you have 28 days to either depart Australia or apply for a new visa. ANU’s 2026 Compliance Framework underscores that students who breach enrolment conditions risk having their **Confirmation of Enrolment cancelled**, which automatically triggers a visa review. Regularly checking your enrolment status on **ANU ISIS** is essential to avoid unexpected lapses.

## Updating Your Address via ANU ISIS: A Non-Negotiable Obligation

**Condition 8533** of the student visa requires you to notify your education provider of your residential address within **seven days** of arriving in Australia, and to update any change of address within seven days. At ANU, the **ANU ISIS update address** function is the designated tool for meeting this requirement. The 2026 ANU Digital Services Report indicates that over 85% of international students use ISIS for address updates, but approximately 12% fail to do so within the mandated timeframe, risking non-compliance.

**Using ISIS** is straightforward: log into the student portal, navigate to the "Personal Details" section, and enter your current Australian residential address. This cannot be a post office box or an overseas address—it must be where you physically reside. The system timestamps every update, creating an audit trail that ANU can use to verify compliance during reporting cycles. In 2026, the Department of Home Affairs intensified address verification checks, and ANU’s International Student Compliance team now conducts quarterly audits. **International student compliance ANU** records show that address-related breaches are among the most common, yet they are entirely avoidable with a simple digital action.

**What happens if you don’t update?** ANU is obligated to report non-compliance to the Department, which can issue a formal warning or, in repeated cases, consider visa cancellation. The university’s 2026 Student Visa Reporting Guidelines highlight that address updates are not just bureaucratic—they affect emergency contact capabilities and legal service of documents. Students living in **ANU-approved accommodation** should note that moving between residences, such as from a hall of residence to a private rental, still requires an ISIS update. Set a calendar reminder every time you move, and double-check that the address you enter matches official documentation like lease agreements.

## Reporting Obligations: What ANU Must Tell the Department

Under the **ESOS National Code 2026**, ANU has mandatory reporting obligations to the Department of Home Affairs and the Department of Education. These reports cover a range of student status changes that directly impact visa compliance. The most significant is **unsatisfactory course progress**—if you fail to meet academic requirements for two consecutive semesters, ANU must report you, which can lead to a visa cancellation notice. In 2026, ANU reported 120 such cases, representing 1.3% of the international student body, according to the university’s Annual Compliance Review.

**Other reportable events** include enrolment cancellation, deferral of studies for non-medical reasons exceeding six months, and failure to pay tuition fees by the due date. ANU also reports when a student completes their program earlier than the CoE end date, which triggers a change in visa conditions. The **ANU ISIS system** plays a central role here: when you update your enrolment status or address, the data feeds into PRISMS (Provider Registration and International Student Management System), linking directly to the Department’s records. **International student compliance ANU** protocols require that reports be submitted within 14 days of the triggering event, a timeline tightened from 31 days in 2025.

**Students’ own reporting obligations** are equally important. You must inform ANU immediately if your visa is cancelled, if you change your passport details, or if you become subject to external legal proceedings that could affect your student status. The 2026 ANU Visa Compliance Guide advises students to treat ISIS as their primary communication channel, as failure to receive university emails due to outdated contact details is not accepted as a defense for non-compliance. Regular engagement with **ANU Student Central** can help you stay ahead of reporting deadlines.

## Common Compliance Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Despite clear guidelines, international students at ANU encounter recurring compliance issues. The 2026 ANU Student Experience Survey identifies the top three pitfalls: **exceeding work hours during exam periods**, **forgetting to update address after short-term moves**, and **misunderstanding enrolment conditions for research degrees**. Each of these can be mitigated with proactive planning.

**Work hour breaches** often occur when students take on extra shifts during stressful academic times, such as mid-semester exams. Even if the additional work is only for one week, a rolling fortnight calculation can push you over the 48-hour limit. The solution is to use a digital tracker—apps like WorkHourTracker or a simple spreadsheet—and to communicate with employers about your student visa constraints. ANU’s 2026 Career Centre advises students to request contracts that specify a maximum of 24 hours per week during sessions, providing a buffer against unexpected roster changes.

**Address update failures** are frequently linked to students who couch-surf or sublet informally. If you’re between permanent accommodations, enter the address where you primarily sleep, and update it as soon as you secure a stable residence. ANU’s 2026 Housing Service recommends using the ISIS mobile interface for immediate updates, even if you’re in temporary housing. For research students, the pitfall is assuming that **thesis submission** equates to course completion. In reality, your CoE remains active until formal program end dates, and you must continue to meet enrolment conditions, including maintaining health cover and address currency, until that date.

## FAQ

**Q: How many hours can I work on a student visa at ANU during the 2026 academic year?**
A: You can work up to **48 hours per fortnight** when your course is in session. During scheduled breaks, such as the July winter recess and the December to February summer holiday, work hours are unrestricted. Always check the ANU academic calendar for exact session dates, and remember that the fortnight is a rolling 14-day period, not a fixed Monday-to-Sunday cycle.

**Q: What happens if I fail to update my address in ANU ISIS within 7 days of moving in 2026?**
A: ANU is required to report non-compliance with Condition 8533 to the Department of Home Affairs. While a single late update may result in a warning, repeated failures can lead to formal breach notices and potential visa cancellation. The 2026 compliance data shows that address-related breaches account for **18% of all student visa warnings** at ANU. Use the ISIS mobile app to update immediately after any move.

**Q: Can I reduce my study load below full-time at ANU if I’m struggling with course content in 2026?**
A: Only with formal approval from the **ANU Dean of Students**, and only in exceptional circumstances such as documented medical conditions or compassionate grounds. Reducing your load without approval breaches enrolment conditions and can lead to CoE cancellation. In 2026, ANU approved **12% of underload applications** from international students, primarily on medical grounds. You must apply before the semester census date to avoid academic and visa penalties.
