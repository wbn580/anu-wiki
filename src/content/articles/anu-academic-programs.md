The Australian National University (ANU) offers over 300 academic programs across seven academic colleges. It is known for its flexible degree structure — particularly the widely emulated Flexible Double Degree program — and its research-intensive curriculum at both undergraduate and postgraduate level.

## Academic Structure

ANU is organised into seven academic colleges:

1. **College of Arts and Social Sciences (CASS)** — Humanities, social sciences, arts
2. **College of Asia and the Pacific (CAP)** — Asian studies, Pacific studies, international relations, strategic studies
3. **College of Business and Economics (CBE)** — Commerce, economics, finance, actuarial studies
4. **College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics (CECC)** — Engineering, computer science, cybernetics
5. **College of Law** — Undergraduate LLB (Hons), Juris Doctor, postgraduate law
6. **College of Health and Medicine** — Medicine, health sciences, psychology, population health
7. **College of Science** — Physics, mathematics, biology, chemistry, earth sciences, environment

## Undergraduate Programs

### Single Degrees
Standard 3-year bachelor degrees (4 years for honours, engineering, law). Most ANU degrees include a compulsory research component in the final year.

### Flexible Double Degrees
ANU's signature offering: combine any two undergraduate degrees into a single program. Over 750 possible combinations. Duration: typically 4 years (some combinations 5 years). Example: Bachelor of Science / Bachelor of International Relations.

### Vertical Double Degrees
Bachelor + Master combined in a single program. Bachelor of Philosophy (PhB) + Master degrees are common. Duration: 4–5 years.

### Honours
Available as an embedded 4th year in most bachelor degrees, or as a standalone 1-year Bachelor of Philosophy (Honours) or Bachelor of [Discipline] (Honours). ANU has a strong Honours culture — it's the standard pathway to PhD admission.

## Postgraduate Programs

### Coursework Masters
- Master degrees (1–2 years): Advanced professional training + capstone research
- Graduate Diplomas / Certificates (6 months–1 year): Specialised short qualifications

### Research Degrees
- **PhD**: 3–4 years of original research under supervision
- **MPhil (Master of Philosophy)**: 1–2 years research training degree (pathway to PhD or standalone)

## Entry Requirements

### Domestic Students
- ATAR (Australian Tertiary Admission Rank) — varies by program
- Typical ATAR cutoffs: 80–98 depending on program
- Bonus points for relevant subjects (maths, science, languages)

### International Students
- Country-specific academic requirements (A-Level, IB, Gaokao, SAT/AP, etc.)
- English language: IELTS 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0) or equivalent. Higher for Law (IELTS 7.0) and Medicine.
- Foundation programs available for students who don't meet direct entry

## Key Dates

- **Semester 1 intake**: Applications close December (international), January (domestic)
- **Semester 2 intake**: Limited programs available; applications close May
- **Direct applications**: Through the ANU Admissions Office (not through UAC for ANU-specific programs)
