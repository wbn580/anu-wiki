## Understanding the ANU LLB (Hons) Structure

The ANU LLB (Hons) comprises **24 compulsory law courses** worth 96 units, delivered over four years in standard progression. Unlike standalone LLB programs at some Australian universities, ANU embeds honours within the degree, requiring all students to complete a **research thesis (LAWS4300)** worth 12 units in their final year. This distinguishes the ANU model from add-on honours and shapes workload distribution across the double degree.

Core law courses include **Foundations of Australian Law (LAWS1201)**, **Torts (LAWS1203)**, **Contracts (LAWS1204)**, and **Constitutional Law (LAWS2201)**. The 2026 curriculum introduces a revised **Law and Legal Institutions (LAWS1200)** as a first-semester gateway course, replacing the previous two-course introduction. This change reduces first-year law units from 12 to 6, allowing double degree students to front-load partner degree requirements.

**ANU LLB honours workload** intensifies in years three and four when students complete **Administrative Law (LAWS2207)**, **Equity and Trusts (LAWS2205)**, and **Evidence (LAWS2208)** alongside the thesis. The thesis requires original legal research of 10,000-12,000 words, supervised by ANU academic staff. Students typically allocate 15-20 hours weekly for thesis work during their final semester, necessitating careful partner degree scheduling to avoid overload.

## Popular ANU Law Double Degree Combinations

The ANU College of Law offers 15 formal double degree pathways in 2026. **ANU law double degree combinations** fall into three categories: humanities (Arts, International Relations, Languages), business (Commerce, Economics, Finance), and sciences (Science, Psychology, Environment and Sustainability). Each combination presents distinct credit overlap opportunities and workload considerations.

The **ANU law arts double degree** remains the most enrolled combination, attracting approximately 38% of double degree students according to 2025 ANU enrolment data. This combination allows students to major in disciplines like **Political Science**, **History**, **Philosophy**, or **Criminology**, creating natural synergies with legal studies. Arts electives provide flexibility to explore interdisciplinary connections, particularly in human rights law, legal history, and socio-legal studies.

The **ANU law commerce course planning** pathway appeals to students targeting corporate law, taxation, or commercial litigation careers. Commerce majors in **Accounting**, **Finance**, or **Business Information Systems** complement legal training in contracts, corporations law, and taxation law. The 2026 ANU Commerce program requires completion of **BUSN1001 Business Reporting and Analysis** and **ECON1101 Microeconomics 1** as foundation courses, which students typically complete in first year alongside introductory law units.

Other notable combinations include **Law and International Relations**, which prepares students for diplomatic service or international law careers, and **Law and Science**, suited to intellectual property, environmental law, or health law specialisations. Each combination requires 192 total units, though credit overlap provisions can reduce this by 12-24 units in specific pairings.

## Credit Overlap and Course Planning Strategies

**ANU double degree credit overlap** rules permit students to count up to 24 units (four courses) toward both degrees, provided the courses appear on both program lists. The 2026 Academic Progress Rule specifies that overlap courses must be elective components in both programs, not core requirements. This mechanism effectively reduces total unit requirements from 192 to 168 in optimal scenarios.

For the **ANU law arts double degree**, common overlap courses include **POLS2094 Issues in International Political Economy**, **HIST2227 Australian Political History**, and **PHIL2082 Philosophy of Law**. Students can claim these as Arts electives and Law elective courses simultaneously. The ANU Law School permits up to 12 units of non-law electives within the LLB (Hons) program, creating space for strategic overlap planning.

**ANU law commerce course planning** benefits from overlap in **ECON2101 Microeconomics 2** and **BUSN3014 Principles of Taxation Law**, which satisfy Commerce major requirements and Law elective slots. Students pursuing an Accounting major can overlap **BUSN3002 Auditing** with Law electives, though this requires approval from both the College of Law and the College of Business and Economics. The 2026 approval process involves submitting a **Program Variation eForm** through ISIS by the census date of the relevant semester.

Effective course planning requires mapping all 192 units across five years before commencing. Students should identify overlap opportunities in year one, reserve Law elective slots for overlap courses, and ensure partner degree major requirements align with available overlap options. The ANU Law School provides **degree planning templates** through the ANU Program Catalogue, updated annually to reflect curriculum changes.

## Workload Management Across Five Years

Managing the **ANU LLB honours workload** within a double degree demands strategic distribution of intensive courses. A standard full-time load comprises 24 units per semester (four courses), totalling 48 units annually. Double degree students complete 192 units over five years, averaging 38.4 units annually, slightly below the standard full-time load.

Year one typically focuses on partner degree foundation courses plus **LAWS1200 Law and Legal Institutions** and **LAWS1201 Foundations of Australian Law**. This distribution allows students to adjust to university-level legal reasoning while building disciplinary knowledge in their partner field. ANU recommends limiting first-year law courses to 12 units to prevent overload during the transition period.

Years two and three intensify with **LAWS1203 Torts**, **LAWS1204 Contracts**, **LAWS2201 Constitutional Law**, and **LAWS2202 Commonwealth Constitutional Law**. Students in the **ANU law commerce double degree** often complete **BUSN1002 Financial Accounting** and **ECON1102 Macroeconomics 1** concurrently, creating semesters of 24 units with significant reading and assessment demands. ANU Academic Skills reports that law students spend approximately 10-12 hours weekly per law course on readings, case briefs, and tutorial preparation.

Year four introduces **LAWS2207 Administrative Law**, **LAWS2205 Equity and Trusts**, and **LAWS2208 Evidence**, alongside the research thesis preparation. The thesis spans two semesters, with **LAWS4300 Legal Research Thesis** completed in the final semester. Students should reserve partner degree electives for year four to balance the thesis workload, avoiding concurrent enrolment in multiple research-intensive courses.

Year five completes remaining partner degree requirements and any outstanding law electives. Students who effectively used credit overlap may finish with a lighter final year of 36 units, providing space for clerkship applications, mooting competitions, or internship placements through the **ANU Law Internship Program (LAWS4301)**.

## FAQ

**How many units does the ANU Law double degree require, and can credit overlap reduce this?**

The ANU LLB (Hons) double degree requires 192 total units, comprising 96 law units and 96 partner degree units. Credit overlap provisions permit up to 24 units (four courses) to count toward both degrees, potentially reducing the total to 168 units. Overlap courses must appear on both program elective lists and cannot substitute for core requirements in either degree. The 2026 Academic Progress Rule governs this mechanism, and students must apply through ISIS before the relevant census date.

**What is the weekly workload for an ANU law double degree student in 2026?**

ANU Academic Skills estimates law courses require 10-12 hours weekly per course, including lectures, tutorials, readings, and assessment preparation. A full-time load of four courses demands approximately 40-48 hours weekly. Double degree students in years two and three, when law and partner degree courses run concurrently, should expect 45-50 hours weekly during peak assessment periods. The thesis semester in year four may require 15-20 additional hours weekly for research and writing.

**Can I complete the ANU law arts double degree in four years instead of five?**

The standard duration for the ANU law arts double degree is five years (10 semesters) of full-time study. Accelerated completion in four years would require enrolling in 24 units every semester including summer sessions, totalling 48 units annually. ANU permits summer enrolment through the **ANU Summer Session program**, offering 6-12 units between November and February. However, the LLB (Hons) thesis (LAWS4300) runs only in Semester 1 and Semester 2, limiting acceleration options. Most students complete the program in five years to maintain manageable workloads and access internship opportunities.

**What law electives are available in the 2026 ANU LLB (Hons) for double degree students?**

The 2026 ANU LLB (Hons) offers 28 law electives, including **LAWS4228 International Humanitarian Law**, **LAWS4237 Cyber Law**, **LAWS4213 Environmental Law**, and **LAWS4246 Indigenous Peoples and the Law**. Double degree students can enrol in up to 24 units of law electives, though credit overlap may reduce this requirement. Some electives, such as **LAWS4267 Human Rights Litigation** and **LAWS4254 Climate Law and Policy**, run biennially, so students should check the ANU Programs and Courses website for 2026 availability.

**How does the ANU law commerce double degree prepare students for corporate law careers?**

The ANU law commerce double degree combines legal training in **LAWS2203 Corporations Law**, **LAWS2210 Taxation Law**, and **LAWS2211 Commercial Law** with commerce majors in Accounting or Finance. This combination satisfies the academic requirements for **Chartered Accountants ANZ** and **CPA Australia** accreditation, depending on course selection. Graduates pursuing corporate law typically complete **BUSN3014 Principles of Taxation Law** as a credit overlap course, gaining both legal and accounting perspectives on taxation. The 2025 ANU Graduate Outcomes Survey reported that 34% of law commerce graduates entered commercial law firms within six months of admission.
