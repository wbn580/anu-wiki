ANU Engineering sits within the College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics (CECC). It offers a distinctive engineering education model — systems-focused rather than traditional discipline-focused — reflecting ANU's research strengths in complex systems, control theory, and cybernetics.

## Programs

**Bachelor of Engineering (Honours)** — 4 years. Majors: Electrical, Mechanical, Mechatronics, Renewable Energy, Environmental, Systems. The ANU engineering curriculum is systems-oriented from the first year — all students study systems engineering alongside their chosen major.

**Master of Engineering** — 2 years. For graduates of non-engineering degrees or engineers seeking a different specialisation. Accredited by Engineers Australia.

**Master of Engineering in Renewable Energy** — 2 years. Specialised pathway reflecting ANU's strong renewable energy research (tracking toward 100% renewable electricity grid integration).

## Accreditation & Careers

ANU Engineering is fully accredited by Engineers Australia. Graduates qualify for Washington Accord recognition (international recognition of Australian engineering degrees).

Median starting salary: A$66,000–72,000. Common employers: defence and systems engineering firms, the ACT Government engineering divisions, energy and renewables companies, consulting engineering (AECOM, Aurecon, GHD).
