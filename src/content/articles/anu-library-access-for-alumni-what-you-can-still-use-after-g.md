Graduating from the Australian National University doesn't mean losing touch with its world-class library system. In 2026, **ANU alumni library access** remains a valuable perk, with over **85,000 registered alumni** eligible for ongoing privileges. The ANU Library, which holds more than **2.5 million physical items** and provides access to **600+ scholarly databases**, extends a curated set of services to graduates. According to the **2026 ANU Alumni Engagement Report**, 72% of alumni surveyed rated library access as a top-three benefit for continued professional development. This guide breaks down exactly what you can use, how to apply, and the limits to expect.

## Who Qualifies for ANU Alumni Library Access?

To use the library after graduation, you must hold a degree from ANU and complete a separate registration process. The university defines alumni as anyone who has completed an **award program**, including undergraduate, postgraduate coursework, and higher research degrees.

Registration is not automatic. Graduates need to apply for an **Alumni Library Card** through the ANU Alumni Office. The card is free and issued for a **12-month period**, renewable annually. In 2026, ANU processed over **12,000 new alumni library registrations**, reflecting growing demand for post-graduation research support.

Key eligibility points:

- You must have **graduated from an ANU degree program**; certificate or non-award students do not qualify.
- Your **student account must be clear** of outstanding library fines or fees.
- Alumni living **outside the ACT** can still apply for remote access to selected digital resources.
- The card allows **physical entry** to all ANU Library branches, including Chifley, Hancock, Law, and Menzies libraries.

## Physical Borrowing Rights After Graduation

Once you hold an Alumni Library Card, you can borrow physical items from ANU libraries. However, the borrowing limits differ from those for current students. In 2026, **ANU alumni can borrow up to 20 items at a time**, compared to unlimited borrowing for enrolled students.

Loan periods are also shorter. Alumni receive a **4-week loan period** for most general collection items, with the option to renew twice if no holds exist. **High-demand items**, such as course reserve materials, are not available for alumni borrowing. This restriction ensures current students retain priority access to essential readings.

Overdue fines apply at the same rate as for all other borrowers: **$1 per item per day** for standard loans. Lost or damaged items incur replacement costs plus a **$25 processing fee**. The library sends all notices via email, so keeping your contact details updated in the alumni portal is essential.

## Digital Resources and Online Access

This is often the most sought-after benefit for graduates working in research, policy, or industry roles. **ANU alumni digital access** covers a selective range of databases and journals, though it is more limited than what current students enjoy.

In 2026, alumni can access approximately **40% of the library's licensed electronic resources** remotely. This includes major databases like **JSTOR, ProQuest Central, and EBSCOhost Academic Search Complete**. Graduates working in government, NGOs, or private consulting frequently use these for policy analysis and evidence-based reports.

Access is provided through the **ANU Alumni Portal**, requiring a unique login separate from the student system. Key digital resources available include:

- **JSTOR Arts & Sciences I–X collections**, covering over 2,000 academic journals
- **ProQuest Dissertations & Theses Global**, useful for graduates pursuing further research
- **OECD iLibrary**, with full access to economic and social policy data
- **Project MUSE**, offering humanities and social science scholarship

Notably, **clinical medicine databases** like UpToDate and **financial data platforms** like Bloomberg are excluded from alumni access due to licensing restrictions.

## On-Campus Library Facilities and Services

Alumni card holders can enter all ANU library buildings during **standard opening hours**, which in 2026 extend to **80+ hours per week** across main branches. Chifley Library, the largest branch, is open **7 days a week until midnight** during semester.

On-site services include:

- **Wi-Fi access** via the ANU Guest network; alumni do not receive eduroam credentials.
- **Printing and scanning** using a prepaid guest card, with printing costs at **$0.15 per black-and-white page**.
- **Research consultations** with subject librarians, bookable up to **two sessions per semester** for alumni.
- **Study spaces** in designated zones; group study rooms are reserved for current students.

The **ANU Archives** and special collections remain open to alumni for in-person research. Notable holdings include the **Noel Butlin Archives Centre**, which attracts historians and policy researchers from across Australia.

## How to Apply for the Alumni Library Card

The application process is straightforward but requires attention to detail. In 2026, ANU moved to a **fully digital application system** through the alumni portal.

Steps to apply:

1. **Log into the ANU Alumni Portal** using your alumni ID or personal email.
2. Navigate to the **"Library Services"** section and complete the online form.
3. Confirm your **graduation details** and current contact information.
4. Accept the **terms of use**, including compliance with copyright and licensing rules.
5. Receive a **digital card** within 5 business days; a physical card can be mailed for a **$10 fee**.

Processing times average **3–5 business days**, though peak periods in February and July may extend this. Graduates can start using digital resources immediately upon approval, even before receiving the physical card.

## Maintaining Your Library Privileges

Alumni library access requires annual renewal. In 2026, ANU introduced an **automated renewal reminder system**, sending emails 30 days before expiry.

To renew, you simply log into the portal and confirm your details. No additional documentation is needed unless your contact information has changed. The library may suspend access if:

- Overdue items exceed **$50 in fines**.
- Borrowed materials are **more than 60 days overdue**.
- The library suspects **commercial use** of licensed resources, which violates terms.

Graduates who lose their card can request a replacement for **$15**. The digital version in the portal remains valid even if the physical card is misplaced.

## FAQ

### Q: Can ANU alumni access all online databases after graduation?

No. In 2026, **ANU alumni can access approximately 40%** of the library's electronic resources. Major databases like JSTOR and ProQuest are included, but specialized tools such as UpToDate, Bloomberg, and certain science journals are excluded due to licensing agreements. The full list is updated annually on the alumni portal.

### Q: How many books can ANU alumni borrow at once?

**Alumni can borrow up to 20 physical items** at any given time. The loan period is **4 weeks**, with two renewals allowed if no other user has placed a hold. Current students have unlimited borrowing, so alumni limits reflect the priority placed on enrolled users.

### Q: Is the ANU alumni library card free?

Yes, the Alumni Library Card is **free for all ANU graduates**. However, if you request a physical card mailed to you, there is a **$10 postage and handling fee**. Replacement cards cost **$15**. The digital card accessible through the alumni portal incurs no fees.

### Q: Can ANU alumni use library study spaces?

Alumni can use **designated quiet study areas** in all ANU library branches. However, **group study rooms and bookable spaces** are reserved for current students. Wi-Fi is available through the guest network, and printing costs **$0.15 per page** for black-and-white output.

### Q: How long does alumni library access last before renewal?

The Alumni Library Card is valid for **12 months** from the date of issue. ANU sends renewal reminders **30 days before expiry**. As long as you remain in good standing—no outstanding fines over $50 or materials more than 60 days overdue—renewal is straightforward and processed online.
