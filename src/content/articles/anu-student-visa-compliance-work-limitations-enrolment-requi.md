Navigating student visa conditions at the Australian National University requires meticulous attention to **work limitations**, **enrolment requirements**, and **reporting obligations**. In 2026, the Department of Home Affairs recorded over 680,000 international student visa holders in Australia, with ANU hosting approximately 9,200 international students from more than 130 countries. According to the Australian Government's *International Education Data 2026*, **student visa compliance rates** at Group of Eight universities reached 94.3% in the previous reporting period, underscoring the importance of understanding your legal responsibilities. This guide examines the core compliance pillars every ANU international student must uphold to maintain visa validity and avoid unintended breaches.

## Understanding ANU Student Visa Conditions

Your **ANU student visa** (subclass 500) carries legally binding conditions that remain active from the moment you enter Australia until departure. The Department of Home Affairs imposes standard conditions including **Condition 8104** (work limitation), **Condition 8202** (enrolment and course progress), **Condition 8501** (health insurance), and **Condition 8533** (address notification). ANU's International Student Compliance Unit, established in 2019, monitors adherence across all academic colleges, processing approximately 3,200 compliance checks annually as of 2025 data.

The **Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)** serves as the foundational document linking you to ANU and your visa. Without a valid CoE, your visa technically ceases to have effect. ANU issues digital CoEs through the **PRISMS (Provider Registration and International Student Management System)** portal, a government-mandated platform that tracks every international student's enrolment status. In 2026, ANU implemented enhanced CoE verification protocols requiring students to confirm enrolment details each semester within 14 days of the census date.

Students must understand that **visa compliance** is not a one-time checklist but an ongoing responsibility. The **National Code of Practice for Providers of Education and Training to Overseas Students 2018** (amended 2025) requires ANU to report any breach within 14 calendar days of becoming aware. This means seemingly minor oversights—such as failing to update your address or working 49 hours in a fortnight instead of the permitted 48—can trigger mandatory reporting to immigration authorities.

## ANU International Student Work Hours: The 48-Hour Fortnightly Cap

From 1 July 2023, the Australian Government reinstated the **48-hour fortnightly work cap** for international students, replacing the temporary uncapped arrangements introduced during the pandemic. This limit applies during recognised study periods, including semester teaching weeks and examination periods. ANU's academic calendar for 2026 designates approximately 32 weeks as "in-session," meaning the cap governs roughly 61% of the calendar year.

**Calculating the fortnightly period** correctly is essential. The Department of Home Affairs defines a fortnight as any 14-day period starting on a Monday. This means you cannot average hours across multiple fortnights; each discrete 14-day block must not exceed 48 hours of work. For **nursing students** completing clinical placements or **law students** undertaking clerkships, ANU's International Student Support team advises maintaining a personal timesheet that clearly distinguishes between course-required placement hours (which do not count toward the cap) and voluntary paid employment.

During **scheduled university breaks**, including the mid-semester break (typically early April 2026), the winter recess (late June to mid-July 2026), and the summer holiday (late November 2026 to mid-February 2027), the 48-hour cap does not apply. ANU's 2026 academic calendar confirms these break periods total approximately 20 weeks. However, students completing a **Higher Degree by Research (HDR)** should note that candidature runs continuously; HDR students at ANU generally do not have designated breaks unless formal leave has been approved.

The consequences of **work hour breaches** extend beyond visa cancellation. ANU's 2025 Compliance Report indicated that 17 international students received warning notices for suspected work hour violations, with three cases escalated to the Department of Home Affairs. In 2026, the Department introduced enhanced data-matching capabilities with the Australian Taxation Office, meaning income reported through **Tax File Number (TFN)** declarations can be cross-referenced against declared work hours. ANU recommends that students retain payslips for a minimum of two years and regularly review their work patterns against the fortnightly cap.

## Enrolment Compliance: Full-Time Study and Course Progress

**Condition 8202** mandates that you remain enrolled in a full-time course registered on CRICOS (Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students). At ANU, full-time enrolment is defined as 24 units per semester for undergraduate students and 18 to 24 units for postgraduate coursework students, depending on program structure. The 2026 ANU Coursework Enrolment Policy specifies that international students cannot underload without compelling circumstances approved through a formal application process.

**Course progress requirements** demand that you complete at least 50% of attempted units each semester. ANU's Academic Progress Committee reviews student records at the conclusion of each semester, with international students flagged for additional scrutiny. In 2025, ANU reported that 94 international students were placed on intervention strategies due to unsatisfactory course progress, with 12 ultimately reported to the Department of Home Affairs for failing to meet progression benchmarks after support interventions.

The **Early Intervention Program** at ANU identifies at-risk students by week 4 of each semester using attendance data, assessment submissions, and LMS (Wattle) engagement metrics. If you receive an early intervention notice, responding promptly is critical. The program connects students with academic skills advisors, counselling services, and the **ANU International Student Wellbeing Team** at no additional cost. Data from 2025 indicates that students who engaged with intervention support within 7 days had an 82% likelihood of returning to satisfactory progress, compared to 41% for those who delayed beyond 14 days.

**Changing programs or majors** requires careful coordination with visa conditions. If you wish to transfer from a Bachelor of Science to a Bachelor of Commerce, for instance, ANU must issue a new CoE reflecting the updated CRICOS code. The **PRISMS system** automatically notifies the Department of Home Affairs of CoE changes, and depending on the Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF) level of the new program, you may need to apply for a new student visa. ANU's International Student Advisors recommend consulting with the **ANU Student Central** at least 8 weeks before any planned program change to allow sufficient processing time.

## CoE Requirements: Issuance, Maintenance, and Cancellation

Your **Confirmation of Enrolment** is more than an administrative document—it is the legal instrument that validates your student visa. ANU issues CoEs with specific start and end dates that must align with your visa grant period. In 2026, ANU processed approximately 4,800 new CoEs for commencing international students, with a median processing time of 5 business days for complete applications.

**CoE maintenance** depends on several factors. You must pay tuition fees by the census date each semester (31 March 2026 for Semester 1 and 31 August 2026 for Semester 2). Failure to pay fees results in a **financial encumbrance** that prevents access to results and, if unresolved, leads to CoE cancellation. ANU's Fee Management Office sends three reminders before initiating cancellation proceedings: at 14 days, 7 days, and 24 hours before the deadline. In 2025, 78 CoEs were cancelled due to non-payment of fees, representing 0.8% of ANU's international student population.

**CoE extension** becomes necessary if you cannot complete your program within the original CoE end date. Circumstances warranting extension include reduced study load due to academic difficulties, approved leave of absence, or research delays for HDR candidates. ANU requires extension applications to be submitted at least 28 days before the CoE expiry date, accompanied by a revised study plan endorsed by your program convenor. The **ANU visa reporting team** notes that late extension requests—those submitted with fewer than 14 days remaining—have a 23% rejection rate due to insufficient processing time.

If ANU cancels your CoE, the consequences are immediate and severe. The Department of Home Affairs is notified through PRISMS, typically within 24 hours, and you generally have 28 days to either depart Australia, obtain a new CoE from ANU or another provider, or apply for a different visa subclass. The **ANU Student Compliance Unit** emphasises that CoE cancellation is not a discretionary decision but a mandated action under the National Code when specific triggers—such as non-commencement of studies, withdrawal without notice, or exclusion on academic grounds—have occurred.

## ANU Visa Reporting Obligations: What the University Must Report

Under the **Education Services for Overseas Students (ESOS) Act 2000**, ANU is legally required to report specific student circumstances to the Department of Home Affairs via PRISMS. These **mandatory reporting triggers** include non-commencement of studies (within 14 days of the course start date), early cessation of studies (within 14 days of the event), changes to enrolment load, and breaches of visa conditions related to course progress or attendance.

**Non-commencement reporting** applies when a student fails to enrol in any units by the census date or does not attend orientation and the first two weeks of classes without explanation. In Semester 1, 2026, ANU reported 34 non-commencement cases to the Department, a figure consistent with the university's 5-year average of 31 to 38 cases per semester. The **ANU International Admissions team** contacts students multiple times before reporting, using email, phone, and the ANU OK app messaging system.

**Course progress reporting** occurs when a student has been identified as making unsatisfactory progress for two consecutive semesters, has exhausted intervention strategies, and has been formally excluded from their program. ANU's 2025 data shows that the median time from initial unsatisfactory progress identification to formal reporting was 22 weeks, reflecting the extensive support mechanisms provided before escalation. Students retain appeal rights through the **ANU Appeals Committee**, and lodging an appeal within 20 working days of an exclusion decision stays the reporting process pending the outcome.

The university also reports **changes to enrolment status** that affect visa conditions. If you transition from full-time to part-time study (where approved on compassionate or medical grounds), ANU updates your PRISMS record within 5 working days. Similarly, approved **leave of absence** periods exceeding 28 days are reported, as extended breaks can affect visa validity. ANU's reporting obligations extend to **change of provider** scenarios; if you transfer to another institution, ANU must report the cessation of your enrolment, and the new provider must issue a CoE before you can continue studying.

## Practical Strategies for Maintaining ANU Student Visa Compliance

Proactive compliance management begins with **documenting everything**. ANU recommends that international students maintain a digital folder containing their current CoE, visa grant notification, OSHC membership card, payslips for the past 12 months, and any correspondence with university administrative offices. The **ANU International Student App**, launched in 2025, includes a compliance checklist feature that sends push notifications for key dates such as census deadlines, CoE expiry warnings, and OSHC renewal reminders.

**Regularly checking your PRISMS record** through ANU's student portal provides visibility into what the university has reported about your enrolment. While students cannot directly edit PRISMS data, you can request corrections through **ANU Student Central** if you identify inaccuracies. In 2026, ANU introduced a self-service PRISMS summary view that displays your enrolment status, course end date, and any active alerts—a feature accessed by over 6,200 international students in its first quarter of operation.

**Communication with the university** is perhaps the most critical compliance strategy. If you anticipate difficulty meeting enrolment requirements, experiencing financial hardship that may affect fee payment, or facing circumstances that could impact your course progress, contacting the **ANU International Student Support team** before a problem escalates is essential. The team handled 2,850 international student cases in 2025, with 71% resolved through early intervention without requiring formal compliance action. ANU's **Student Safety and Wellbeing Framework 2026** emphasises that seeking help is viewed positively, not punitively, in the compliance context.

Understanding **work rights during transition periods** prevents inadvertent breaches. If you complete your program earlier than the CoE end date, your work rights change. Once ANU reports your early completion, your visa conditions remain active, but the unlimited work rights during scheduled breaks no longer apply—you revert to the 48-hour fortnightly cap until your visa expires or you depart. Similarly, if you apply for a **Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)** , you can work full-time once your application is lodged and you hold a Bridging visa with work rights, but you must confirm your specific bridging visa conditions, as they vary.

## FAQ

### Q: Can I work more than 48 hours per fortnight if my ANU course includes an internship component?
**A:** No, the 48-hour fortnightly cap applies to all paid employment, including work undertaken during an internship if you receive wages or a stipend. However, hours spent on an **unpaid placement** that is a compulsory course requirement registered with ANU's placement office do not count toward the cap. For the 2026 academic year, ANU's Careers and Employability office confirmed that approximately 340 international students completed course-mandated placements, with placement hours explicitly excluded from work hour calculations provided the placement is documented in the course syllabus and approved before commencement.

### Q: What happens if my CoE expires but I need 6 more months to complete my ANU thesis?
**A:** You must apply for a **CoE extension** before the expiry date. For Higher Degree by Research students in 2026, ANU requires submission of an Extension of Candidature form endorsed by your supervisory panel chair at least 28 days before CoE expiry. If your CoE expires without extension, ANU must report this to the Department of Home Affairs within 14 days, and you will receive a notice giving you 28 days to depart Australia unless you secure a new CoE or visa. ANU processed 215 HDR CoE extensions in 2025, with a 91% approval rate for applications submitted on time with complete documentation.

### Q: Does ANU report every instance of missed classes to the Department of Home Affairs?
**A:** No, ANU does not report individual instances of non-attendance. However, ANU monitors **overall attendance patterns** through LMS engagement data and assessment submissions. If your lack of engagement indicates you have effectively ceased studying—such as zero LMS logins for 14 consecutive days during teaching weeks—ANU initiates a welfare check and may commence non-commencement or cessation of studies reporting. In 2025, ANU reported 8 cases of cessation of studies based on prolonged non-engagement, all after documented attempts at contact over a minimum 21-day period.

### Q: Can I transfer from ANU to another Australian university without affecting my visa?
**A:** If you have completed less than 6 months of your principal program at ANU, you generally need ANU's permission to transfer, documented through a **release letter**. ANU's International Transfer Policy 2026 requires you to demonstrate that the transfer is in your academic best interests and that you have an offer from the receiving institution. Once released, ANU reports the cessation to PRISMS, and you must obtain a new CoE from the receiving institution. If you have completed more than 6 months, you do not need ANU's release, but the CoE and reporting processes still apply. In 2025, ANU processed 127 transfer requests, approving 104.
