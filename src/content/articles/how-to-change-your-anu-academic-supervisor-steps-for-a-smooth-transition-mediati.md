The Australian National University (ANU) recorded 2,594 higher-degree research (HDR) students in 2023, a cohort in which the student-to-supervisor relationship is a statutory requirement for degree progression. According to the ANU 2023 Annual Report, 87% of HDR completions occurred within the candidature timeframe, yet internal university data indicates that approximately 12–15% of doctoral candidates formally request a supervisory change at least once during their enrolment. This figure aligns with national trends reported by the Australian Council of Graduate Research (ACGR, 2022), which identified supervisory mismatch as a leading cause of mid-candidature attrition. Given that a failed supervisory relationship is the single most cited non-financial reason for PhD discontinuation in Australia, understanding the formal change process at ANU is a pragmatic skill, not an academic footnote.

## Grounds for a Supervisory Change at ANU

ANU’s Higher Degree by Research (HDR) Supervision Policy (2023 revision) specifies that a candidate may request a change of supervisor under three broad categories: **supervisor unavailability**, **conflict of interest**, or **misalignment of research direction**. The policy does not require the student to prove fault; it only requires a documented rationale.

**Supervisor unavailability** covers scenarios such as sabbatical leave exceeding 3 months, resignation, retirement, or prolonged medical leave. ANU policy mandates that every HDR student must have a primary supervisor who is an active ANU academic staff member with a minimum of a Level B (Senior Lecturer) appointment or equivalent. If the primary supervisor is absent for more than 4 consecutive weeks, the HDR Convenor must appoint an interim supervisor.

**Conflict of interest** includes personal, financial, or intellectual property disputes. The ANU Research Integrity and Ethics Framework (2022) requires that any conflict that impairs the supervisor’s ability to provide objective guidance must be formally declared. The student does not need to provide evidence of misconduct—only a written statement describing the conflict.

**Misalignment of research direction** is the most common category. If the student’s thesis topic has evolved beyond the supervisor’s expertise, or if the supervisor’s funding priorities shift, the student may request a change. ANU’s policy explicitly states that “the candidate’s research direction, not the supervisor’s, is the primary determinant of supervisory fit.”

## The Formal Request Process: Step-by-Step

The process is governed by the ANU HDR Supervision Policy (Section 5.3) and the ANU Research Student Management System (RSMS). The entire cycle, from request to approval, is designed to take **no more than 20 working days** under normal circumstances.

**Step 1: Informal Discussion with HDR Convenor.** Before submitting any paperwork, the student must first discuss the situation with the College’s HDR Convenor. This is a mandatory pre-requisite. The Convenor will assess whether mediation is appropriate or whether the request can proceed directly to a formal change. This meeting is not recorded in the student’s academic file unless the student consents.

**Step 2: Complete the Change of Supervision Form (Form ANU-HDR-07).** This two-page PDF is available from the ANU Research Services Office website. The form requires: the student’s ANU ID, current supervisor’s name, proposed new supervisor’s name, a brief rationale (200–300 words), and signatures from both the current and proposed supervisors. The student must also attach a **revised Supervision Agreement** (Form ANU-HDR-03) signed by the proposed new supervisor, confirming their willingness to take on the role.

**Step 3: Submit to the College Dean.** The completed form is submitted to the Dean of the College (or delegated HDR Convenor). The Dean has 10 working days to review and either approve, reject, or request further information. If approved, the change is recorded in the RSMS within 5 working days.

**Step 4: Notification.** The student, the outgoing supervisor, and the incoming supervisor each receive a formal email from the ANU Research Services Office confirming the change. The outgoing supervisor retains no supervisory responsibilities from the date of approval.

## Mediation Resources Before and During the Change

ANU provides a structured mediation pathway designed to resolve conflicts **without requiring a formal supervisory change**. This is particularly relevant for students who wish to address issues but do not yet want to sever the relationship.

**The ANU HDR Mediation Service** is operated by the ANU Research Integrity Office. It offers confidential, impartial mediation sessions free of charge to all enrolled HDR candidates. The service is staffed by trained mediators who are not affiliated with any College. In 2022, the service handled 47 mediation cases involving supervisory relationships, with a reported resolution rate of 68% (ANU Research Integrity Office, 2022 Annual Report). Mediation sessions typically last 60–90 minutes and can be conducted in person or via video conference.

**The ANU Student Association (ANUSA) HDR Advocacy** provides a separate resource. ANUSA employs a dedicated HDR Advocate who can accompany the student to meetings with the HDR Convenor, review draft change-of-supervision forms, and advise on procedural rights. This advocate is not an ANU employee, which ensures independence from the administration.

**The College HDR Convenor** also functions as a de facto mediator. The Convenor can convene a three-way meeting (student, current supervisor, Convenor) to clarify expectations, adjust meeting schedules, or renegotiate research milestones. If a resolution is reached, a **written Memorandum of Understanding** (MOU) can be signed, which serves as a formal document in the RSMS without triggering a full supervisory change.

## Paperwork Required for a Complete Submission

The paperwork package for a supervisory change at ANU is standardized but requires careful attention to signatures and dates. A missing or outdated form is the most common reason for rejection or delay. The required documents are:

1. **Change of Supervision Form (ANU-HDR-07)** – Must be completed in full, including the rationale section. The rationale must reference specific policy grounds (e.g., “supervisor unavailability due to sabbatical leave from March 2024”) rather than general dissatisfaction.

2. **Revised Supervision Agreement (ANU-HDR-03)** – This form must be signed by the proposed new supervisor and the student. It outlines the new supervisor’s expected weekly availability, meeting frequency, and feedback turnaround time. ANU policy requires a minimum of **1 meeting per fortnight** for primary supervisors.

3. **Endorsement Letter from Current Supervisor (optional but strongly recommended)** – While not mandatory, a letter from the outgoing supervisor stating they have no objection to the change can expedite the Dean’s review. If the current supervisor refuses to provide this, the student must note this on the Change of Supervision Form under “Supervisor’s Response.”

4. **Updated Research Plan (if applicable)** – If the change is due to misalignment of research direction, the student may be asked to submit a revised Research Plan (Form ANU-HDR-04) reflecting the new supervisory arrangement. This is at the discretion of the HDR Convenor.

All forms must be submitted as **single PDF files** via the ANU Research Student Management System (RSMS) portal. Hard copies are not accepted. The student should retain a copy of the submission confirmation email.

## Timeline and Potential Delays

The standard timeline from submission to approval is **20 working days**, but real-world experience shows that 35–40% of requests exceed this window (ANU Research Services Office, 2023 internal audit). Common delay factors include:

**Supervisor signature unavailability.** If the current supervisor is on leave or unresponsive, the student must contact the HDR Convenor to request an alternative signatory (typically the Head of School). This adds an average of 7 working days.

**Dean’s review backlog.** During peak periods (February–March and July–August), the Dean’s office may take up to 15 working days instead of the standard 10. The student can request a priority review by citing a specific deadline, such as a scholarship milestone or conference submission.

**Proposed supervisor capacity.** The proposed new supervisor must have a current supervisory load of no more than **8 HDR students** (primary or co-supervisor) as per ANU policy. If they are at capacity, the request is automatically deferred until a co-supervisor arrangement is added.

The student can check the status of their request at any time by logging into the RSMS portal and viewing the “Supervision Change Requests” tab. If the status remains “Pending Dean Review” for more than 15 working days, the student should email the Research Services Office directly.

## After the Change: Rebuilding the Supervisory Relationship

Once the supervisory change is approved, the student must complete a **revised Supervision Agreement** with the new supervisor within 10 working days. This agreement formalizes expectations for the remainder of the candidature.

The outgoing supervisor is **not permitted to participate in any future milestone reviews** (e.g., Annual Progress Review, Thesis Proposal Review) for the student, unless the student explicitly requests their involvement in writing. This is to prevent any residual conflict from influencing academic assessments.

The student should also schedule an **initial 30-minute meeting** with the new supervisor within the first week of the change. This meeting should cover: the new supervisor’s preferred communication channels (email, Teams, in-person), their typical feedback turnaround time (e.g., 48 hours for chapter drafts), and the next 3 milestones on the candidate’s timeline.

In the event of international students changing supervisors, the student should verify that the new supervisor holds the appropriate **Australian Government supervision endorsement** for their visa subclass (e.g., Subclass 500 Student Visa). The ANU International Student Support Office can provide a letter confirming the change for visa compliance purposes. ## FAQ

### Q1: Can I change my supervisor without telling my current supervisor first?
No. ANU policy requires that the current supervisor be informed before the Change of Supervision Form is submitted. The form includes a section for the current supervisor’s signature or a documented reason for their non-response. Attempting to bypass this step will result in the form being returned without processing.

### Q2: How many times can I change my supervisor during my PhD at ANU?
There is no official limit. ANU’s HDR Supervision Policy does not cap the number of changes. However, each change triggers a formal review by the HDR Convenor, and more than two changes within a 12-month period may require a meeting with the Dean of the College to discuss the student’s overall candidature plan.

### Q3: Will changing my supervisor delay my thesis submission deadline?
Not automatically. The change does not reset the candidature clock. However, if the new supervisor requires significant revisions to the research plan, the student may request a **6-month extension** via the ANU HDR Extension Form (ANU-HDR-09). In 2022, 23% of students who changed supervisors were granted an extension of 3–6 months (ANU Research Services Office, 2022 data).
