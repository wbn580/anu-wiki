## Introduction: Why ANU Library Research Skills Workshops Matter for Your Academic Journey

The Australian National University Library supports over **22,000 enrolled students** and **4,000 academic staff** across its campuses in 2026, offering a comprehensive suite of research skills workshops designed to build core competencies for successful scholarship. According to the **2026 QS World University Rankings**, ANU remains firmly within the global top 40 research institutions, and its library services consistently earn high satisfaction ratings from postgraduate researchers. The ANU library research workshops program has expanded significantly this year, with more than **120 scheduled sessions** covering everything from introductory database searching to advanced systematic review methodologies. Whether you are a first-year PhD candidate or a seasoned research fellow, these sessions provide targeted training that directly supports your research output, grant applications, and publication goals.

## What Are ANU Library Research Skills Workshops?

**ANU library research workshops** are free, structured training sessions delivered by specialist librarians and research support staff throughout the academic year. The program covers five core domains: information discovery and search strategies, reference management tools, research data management, scholarly communication, and evidence synthesis methods. Each workshop combines theoretical instruction with hands-on practice, typically running between **60 and 120 minutes** depending on the complexity of the topic. In 2026, ANU has introduced **hybrid delivery formats** for most workshops, allowing participants to join either in person at the Chifley Library training rooms or online via Microsoft Teams. The curriculum is aligned with the **ANU Researcher Development Framework**, ensuring that workshop content maps directly to the skills required at each stage of a research career. Importantly, these sessions are not recorded lectures; they are interactive environments where you can ask specific questions about your own research challenges.

## How to Book ANU Library Research Workshops in 2026

Booking your place in an ANU library research workshop follows a straightforward process through the university's central events platform. Current ANU students and staff can log in using their **uni ID and password** on the ANU Library Events Calendar, where all upcoming workshops are listed with full descriptions and learning outcomes. Each listing displays the session date, time, delivery mode, and remaining capacity, with most workshops capped at **25 participants** to ensure quality interaction. You will receive an automated confirmation email immediately after booking, and a reminder notification **24 hours before** the session begins. For workshops that fill up quickly, such as the **ANU EndNote training** sessions during March and August peak periods, a waitlist function becomes available automatically. The library also accommodates group bookings for research teams or coursework cohorts of **10 or more participants**, which can be arranged by emailing the Research Skills team directly. International students and remote researchers should note that all online workshops operate on **Australian Eastern Standard Time**, so it is advisable to double-check the time zone conversion when booking from overseas.

## ANU EndNote Training: Mastering Reference Management

The **ANU EndNote training** workshops represent one of the most consistently popular offerings in the library's research skills calendar, with sessions scheduled weekly throughout both semesters in 2026. EndNote is the university's officially supported reference management software, and all ANU affiliates have access to the full desktop version through the institutional license. The introductory workshop covers creating and organising an EndNote library, importing references from major databases such as Scopus and Web of Science, and using the **Cite While You Write** plugin in Microsoft Word. The advanced session delves into customising output styles, managing large libraries of over **5,000 references**, and troubleshooting common formatting issues that arise during thesis submission. Participants are encouraged to bring their own laptops with EndNote pre-installed, and librarians provide one-on-one assistance during the final **20 minutes** of each session. A key benefit highlighted by past attendees is learning how to use EndNote's **group and smart group functions** to organise literature for multiple chapters or papers simultaneously, a skill that saves significant time during the writing phase.

## ANU Research Data Management: Building Skills for Compliance and Impact

Research data management has become a critical competency for all researchers, particularly with the **Australian Code for the Responsible Conduct of Research 2025** mandating detailed data management plans for funded projects. The **ANU research data management** workshop series addresses this need through three progressive modules. The foundational module explains the principles of data organisation, file naming conventions, and version control, using real examples from ANU researchers across disciplines. The intermediate module focuses on writing a compliant Data Management Plan using the university's **DMP tool**, which aligns with funder requirements from the Australian Research Council and National Health and Medical Research Council. The advanced module covers data deposit procedures in the **ANU Data Commons repository**, including metadata standards, licensing options, and the process for obtaining a Digital Object Identifier. In 2026, ANU has reported that over **85% of PhD candidates** who completed the full research data management workshop series successfully submitted their data management plans on time for their confirmation reviews.

## ANU Systematic Review Workshop: Structured Approaches to Evidence Synthesis

The **ANU systematic review workshop** is an intensive, multi-session program designed for researchers undertaking evidence synthesis projects in health sciences, social sciences, and environmental disciplines. This workshop series typically spans **four consecutive weeks**, with each session building on the previous one. The first session introduces systematic review protocols and guides participants through registering their reviews on **PROSPERO** or the Open Science Framework. The second session focuses on developing comprehensive search strategies across multiple databases, teaching advanced Boolean operators, proximity searching, and the use of subject headings in databases such as MEDLINE and Embase. The third session covers the screening process using **Covidence systematic review software**, which ANU provides free access to for all researchers. The final session addresses data extraction techniques and quality assessment tools, including the **Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 tool** and the **JBI Critical Appraisal Checklists**. Librarians who teach these workshops hold specialist qualifications in evidence synthesis, and many have co-authored published systematic reviews themselves.

## Specialised Workshops for Discipline-Specific Research Needs

Beyond the core offerings, ANU library research workshops include discipline-specific sessions that cater to the unique requirements of different academic fields. Humanities researchers can attend workshops on using **archival finding aids** and navigating the **National Library of Australia's Trove database**, which contains over **6 billion digital records**. Science and engineering researchers benefit from sessions on **citation metrics and researcher profiles**, learning how to maintain accurate ORCID and Scopus Author ID profiles to enhance the visibility of their work. Law researchers have access to workshops on using **Westlaw and LexisNexis** for case law research, while social science researchers can attend sessions on locating and using **large-scale survey datasets** from the Australian Data Archive. The library also offers workshops on **data visualisation techniques** using tools such as R and Python, which have become increasingly popular in 2026 as more ANU researchers incorporate computational methods into their projects.

## What You Will Actually Learn: Practical Outcomes from Workshop Participation

Attending ANU library research workshops delivers tangible, immediately applicable skills that accelerate your research productivity. After completing the **ANU EndNote training**, you will be able to insert and format citations in your thesis or manuscript in under **30 seconds**, compared to the manual approach that often takes several minutes per reference. The research data management workshops teach you to structure your project folders so that any collaborator can locate a specific data file within **two minutes**, a practice that prevents data loss and miscommunication in team-based research. Participants in the systematic review workshop leave with a completed search strategy for at least one database, which typically retrieves **20-30% more relevant studies** than a basic keyword search. The library also provides post-workshop support through **30-minute individual consultations**, where you can work through specific challenges in your own research context with the same librarians who delivered the training. This continuity of support is a distinctive feature of the ANU program that distinguishes it from one-off external training courses.

## FAQ

**How much do ANU library research workshops cost in 2026?**
All ANU library research workshops are completely free for current ANU students and staff. This includes the full ANU EndNote training series, research data management modules, and the multi-session systematic review workshop. External researchers who hold a library borrower card can attend selected introductory workshops for a fee of **AUD 45 per session**, subject to availability.

**Can I attend ANU library workshops if I am based at an interstate campus or overseas?**
Yes, approximately **70% of workshops in 2026** are offered in hybrid format with a live online option via Microsoft Teams. You will receive the Teams link in your confirmation email after booking. The library recommends testing your connection at least **10 minutes before** the session start time, as technical support cannot be provided once the workshop is underway.

**How many workshops should I attend as a new PhD candidate?**
The library recommends that new PhD candidates attend at least **five core workshops** during their first six months: the library orientation for researchers, introductory EndNote training, research data management fundamentals, advanced database searching, and the scholarly communication session on publishing strategies. This combination covers the essential skills needed for confirmation and early-stage research productivity.

**Are ANU library workshops recorded for later viewing?**
Most workshops are not recorded because the sessions involve hands-on activities and interactive discussions that do not translate well to passive viewing. However, the library provides **written guides and tutorial videos** for the software-based components, such as EndNote and Covidence, which you can access at any time through the ANU Library Research Skills Wattle site.
