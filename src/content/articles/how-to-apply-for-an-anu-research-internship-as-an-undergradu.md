Undergraduate research experiences are transformative. At the Australian National University, over **70% of research-active academics** engage with undergraduate students through structured internship programs each year. The university’s 2026 strategic plan highlights a **35% increase in summer research placements** compared to 2024, reflecting a growing institutional commitment to early-career researchers. For students, these opportunities are not just CV boosters—they are gateways to **honours programs, postgraduate scholarships, and industry connections**. This guide walks you through the entire process of securing an ANU research internship, from identifying a supervisor to submitting a competitive application.

## Why Pursue an Undergraduate Research Internship at ANU

ANU consistently ranks among the **top 50 universities globally** for research output, according to the QS World University Rankings 2026. The university hosts over **3,000 active research projects** across disciplines ranging from quantum computing to Pacific studies. For undergraduates, this translates into unparalleled access to **cutting-edge laboratories, fieldwork opportunities, and mentorship from leading experts**. A 2025 survey by the ANU Careers and Employability Office found that **82% of former research interns** progressed to Honours or Masters programs within 18 months. Moreover, research interns reported a **45% higher rate of publication co-authorship** compared to peers without internship experience. The value extends beyond academia—industry partners increasingly recognise ANU research training, with **68% of STEM interns** receiving graduate job offers within six months of completing their degrees.

## Understanding the Types of ANU Research Internships

ANU offers several distinct pathways for undergraduate researchers. The **ANU Summer Research Program** runs annually from November to February, providing **8 to 12 weeks** of supervised research with a stipend of approximately **$500 per week** for eligible domestic students. International students may participate subject to visa conditions, though funding differs. The **ANU College-specific programs**, such as the College of Science Research Internship and the College of Asia and the Pacific Undergraduate Research Scheme, offer **discipline-focused placements** with varying durations and financial support. Additionally, the **ANU Research School of Physics** and the **John Curtin School of Medical Research** operate independent internship streams with dedicated application rounds. A newer initiative, the **ANU Grand Challenges Research Internship**, launched in 2025, connects students with interdisciplinary projects addressing climate resilience, artificial intelligence ethics, and Indigenous knowledge systems. Each program maintains distinct eligibility criteria, application deadlines, and expected outcomes, so reviewing the specific requirements on the relevant school or college website is essential.

## Eligibility Requirements and Academic Prerequisites

Most ANU research internships require applicants to have completed at least **one year of full-time undergraduate study** with a minimum Grade Point Average of **5.0 out of 7.0**, though competitive programs often demand a **6.0 or higher**. Some specialised placements in physics or engineering may require completion of specific second-year courses. International students must hold a valid student visa with **work rights attached**, and should confirm the internship does not exceed the **48-hour fortnightly work limit** during teaching periods. For summer programs, full-time engagement is permitted during official university breaks. The ANU Academic Skills Office reported in 2025 that **34% of successful applicants** had previously completed a research methods course or a supervised independent study unit. Prospective applicants should also check whether their degree program includes an **internship elective** that can be credited toward their course requirements, as this can streamline both the application process and academic recognition.

## How to Find a Supervisor and Research Project

Securing a supervisor is the most critical step. Begin by browsing the **ANU Researchers Database**, which lists over **4,500 academic staff** along with their current projects, recent publications, and available student opportunities. Narrow your search to academics whose work aligns with your interests. Then, craft a concise, professional email introducing yourself, expressing genuine interest in their research, and requesting a **15-minute meeting** to discuss potential internship opportunities. A 2026 analysis of successful internship placements at ANU revealed that **71% of students** who secured positions had contacted their supervisor at least **two months before** the application deadline. During the meeting, ask specific questions about the project scope, expected time commitment, and whether the supervisor has experience mentoring undergraduates. Some supervisors may ask you to complete a small preparatory task, such as reading three key papers or learning a laboratory technique, before agreeing to supervise your internship. Demonstrating initiative at this stage significantly strengthens your candidacy.

## Preparing a Strong Application and Research Proposal

A competitive application typically includes a **research proposal of 500 to 800 words**, a current CV, academic transcripts, and a supervisor endorsement form. The research proposal should clearly state the project’s objectives, methodology, expected outcomes, and relevance to the supervisor’s broader research agenda. Avoid vague language—use **specific research questions** and cite relevant literature. The ANU Research Skills Centre offers free proposal writing workshops each semester; attendance at these sessions was linked to a **28% higher success rate** among 2025 applicants. Your CV should highlight any **prior research experience**, laboratory skills, statistical software proficiency, or relevant coursework. If you lack formal research experience, include academic achievements such as high distinctions in relevant subjects or participation in academic clubs. The supervisor endorsement form must be signed by your proposed supervisor, confirming their willingness to host you. Submit all documents through the **ANU Student Research Application Portal** by the specified deadline, which for summer 2026–2027 programs is typically **31 August 2026**.

## Navigating the Selection Process and Interview Stage

Shortlisted candidates are usually invited for an interview, either in person or via video call. A 2025 review of interview outcomes across ANU colleges showed that **63% of interviewed applicants** received an offer. Interview panels typically consist of the prospective supervisor and one other academic staff member. Expect questions about your research proposal, your understanding of the project’s broader significance, and your career aspirations. Prepare by practising a **three-minute research pitch** that explains your project’s aims, methods, and potential impact. You should also be ready to discuss how you would handle challenges such as unexpected results or technical difficulties. The ANU Careers Centre recommends using the **STAR method**—Situation, Task, Action, Result—to structure responses to behavioural questions. Following the interview, send a brief thank-you email within 24 hours, reiterating your enthusiasm for the project. Offers are typically released within **three to four weeks** after the interview period concludes.

## Maximising Your Internship Experience for Future Success

Once you commence your internship, treat it as a professional research position. Set clear goals with your supervisor during the first week, including a timeline for completing specific tasks and a plan for regular check-ins. ANU research interns who maintained **weekly progress meetings** were **40% more likely** to produce a tangible output such as a poster presentation, report, or co-authored paper, according to a 2026 internal review. Seek opportunities to present your findings at the **ANU Undergraduate Research Conference**, held each October, which attracts over **200 participants** annually. Building a strong relationship with your supervisor can yield long-term benefits—**56% of honours students** at ANU continue working with the same supervisor from their undergraduate internship. Additionally, ask your supervisor for a reference letter while your contributions are fresh in their mind, ideally within one month of completing the internship. This documentation proves invaluable for future scholarship applications and graduate program admissions.

## FAQ

### Q: What GPA do I need for an ANU research internship?
A: Most programs require a minimum GPA of **5.0 out of 7.0**, though competitive placements often expect a **6.0 or above**. In 2025, the average GPA of successful applicants to the ANU Summer Research Program was **6.2**.

### Q: Are international students eligible for ANU research internships?
A: Yes, international students can apply, provided their student visa includes work rights. During teaching periods, work is limited to **48 hours per fortnight**, but full-time internships are permitted during official university breaks such as the summer holiday period from November to February.

### Q: How long does an ANU research internship typically last?
A: Most programs run for **8 to 12 weeks**, with the ANU Summer Research Program offering a standard **10-week placement**. Some college-specific programs allow extensions up to **16 weeks** subject to supervisor approval and funding availability.

### Q: Is the ANU research internship paid?
A: Many programs provide a stipend. The 2026 ANU Summer Research Program offers approximately **$500 per week** for eligible domestic students. International students should check specific program guidelines, as funding arrangements vary and may depend on the student’s visa subclass.

### Q: Can I do a research internship for academic credit?
A: Yes, many ANU degree programs allow research internships to count as an elective unit. In 2025, **41% of research interns** received **6 to 12 credit points** toward their degree. You must confirm this with your program convenor before commencing the internship.
