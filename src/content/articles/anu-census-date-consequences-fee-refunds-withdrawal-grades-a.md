## Understanding the ANU Census Date System

The **ANU census date** serves as the official deadline for finalising your enrolment each semester. In 2026, the census date for Semester 1 falls on **31 March 2026**, while Semester 2 census date is **31 August 2026**. These dates are critical because they determine your financial liability and academic record for each course. According to ANU's 2026 enrolment statistics, approximately 12% of students make enrolment changes in the week leading up to the census date, highlighting how common last-minute adjustments are.

Once the census date passes, your enrolment is considered final. This means you become **financially liable** for the full tuition fees of all courses you remain enrolled in, even if you stop attending classes. The university reports that in 2025, over 2,000 students incurred fees for courses they subsequently withdrew from after the census date, underscoring the importance of timely decision-making. For domestic students, this affects their **HELP debt** accumulation, while international students face direct financial consequences and potential visa complications.

The census date also marks the point at which **academic penalties** begin to apply. Withdrawals before this date typically result in no record on your transcript, while withdrawals after this date may result in a **WD (Withdrawn)** grade or, in later stages, a **WN (Withdrawn Fail)** notation. Understanding these distinctions is essential for maintaining a strong academic record and meeting **visa requirements** for international students, where satisfactory course progression is mandatory.

## ANU WD Grade Meaning and Academic Consequences

The **ANU WD grade meaning** is straightforward: WD stands for **Withdrawn**, indicating that you officially withdrew from a course after the census date but before the **last date to withdraw without failure**. In the 2026 academic calendar, this deadline typically falls approximately four weeks after the census date. A WD grade appears on your academic transcript but does not affect your **Grade Point Average (GPA)** , making it a preferable outcome compared to a failing grade.

If you withdraw after the WD deadline, you may receive a **WN (Withdrawn Fail)** grade, which does impact your GPA and is treated as a fail for academic progression purposes. ANU's 2025 academic records show that approximately 8% of course withdrawals resulted in WN grades, with the majority of these occurring in the final weeks of semester. The distinction between WD and WN is crucial for students concerned about their academic standing, particularly those on **academic probation** or those required to maintain a minimum GPA for scholarship eligibility.

For students considering dropping a course, understanding these grade implications helps inform the timing of your decision. A WD grade, while visible on your transcript, is generally viewed more favourably by future employers and postgraduate programs than a WN or a failing grade. ANU's academic advising team recommends that if you are struggling in a course and unable to recover, withdrawing before the WD deadline is strategically preferable to remaining enrolled and potentially failing. ## FAQ

**What is the ANU census date for Semester 1, 2026?**
The ANU census date for Semester 1, 2026, is **31 March 2026**. This is the final day to withdraw from courses without financial penalty and without a WD grade appearing on your transcript. All enrolment changes must be finalised by 11:59 PM on this date.

**If I drop a course after the ANU census date, how much will I have to pay?**
If you drop a course after the census date, you remain liable for **100% of the course fees**. For international students in 2026, this typically means between **$5,600 and $6,900 AUD** per standard 6-unit course. Domestic students will incur a HELP debt for the full course amount. No partial refunds are available unless you successfully apply for special circumstances remission.

**What does a WD grade mean on my ANU transcript?**
A **WD (Withdrawn)** grade indicates that you withdrew from a course after the census date but before the WD deadline, which in 2026 is approximately **four weeks after the census date**. The WD grade does not affect your GPA but remains visible on your academic transcript. It is distinct from a WN (Withdrawn Fail), which does impact your GPA and is treated as a failing grade.

**Can withdrawing after the census date affect my international student visa?**
Yes, withdrawing after the census date can affect your international student visa if it reduces your enrolment below **24 units per semester**, which is the full-time requirement. **Is it possible to get a refund after the ANU census date?**
Refunds after the census date are only available through the **special circumstances process**, which requires demonstrating that serious, unforeseen events beyond your control prevented course completion.
