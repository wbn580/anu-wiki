Managing tuition payments as an international student at the **Australian National University (ANU)** requires careful planning and a clear understanding of the available payment structures. With annual international tuition fees ranging from approximately AUD 39,000 to over AUD 53,000 depending on your program, knowing when and how to pay can significantly reduce financial stress. According to the Australian Government's Department of Education 2025 international student data, over 720,000 international enrolments were recorded across the higher education sector, with ANU remaining one of the most popular destinations. This guide breaks down every aspect of ANU's fee payment plans, including **installment options**, critical **deadlines for the 2025 academic year**, and the **late payment penalties** you need to avoid.

## Understanding ANU's Fee Structure for International Students

International students at ANU are required to pay **tuition fees upfront** for each semester or session, unless they have secured a formal payment arrangement. The university operates on a standard two-semester academic year, with some programs offering additional summer or winter sessions. Your total annual fee is calculated based on your specific program and the number of units you enrol in.

For 2025, ANU has published its international student fee schedule with clear **per-unit costs** that vary by discipline. A standard full-time load is 24 units per semester, or 48 units annually. Undergraduate arts and humanities courses typically fall in the AUD 41,000–45,000 range per year, while laboratory-based science and engineering programs can exceed AUD 50,000. Postgraduate coursework fees follow a similar pattern, with MBA programs often reaching higher thresholds. The university reviews these fees annually, and increases are common, so you should budget for a 3–5% rise each calendar year.

Unlike domestic students who access government-supported Commonwealth Supported Places (CSP), international students must pay the full international tuition rate. However, ANU does offer **scholarships** that can offset costs, including the ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship, which provides a 25% or 50% tuition reduction for high-achieving students. Receiving a scholarship does not change the payment deadlines, but it reduces the total amount you need to pay each semester.

## ANU Tuition Installment Plans: Paying in Two Parts

ANU allows international students to pay their semester tuition in **two instalments** rather than one lump sum. This is the standard **ANU tuition installment plan**, and it is automatically available to all students without a separate application process. The first instalment covers 50% of your semester fees and is due before the semester begins. The second instalment, covering the remaining 50%, is due approximately midway through the semester.

For Semester 1, 2025, the first instalment deadline falls on **Friday, 14 February 2025**. This date aligns with the university's enrolment deadline and is non-negotiable unless you have an approved extension. The second instalment for Semester 1 is due on **Friday, 4 April 2025**. For Semester 2, 2025, the first instalment deadline is **Friday, 18 July 2025**, with the second instalment due on **Friday, 5 September 2025**. These dates are published on the ANU fees and payments calendar and are updated annually, so you should always verify the current year's deadlines through your ISIS student portal.

It is important to note that the instalment plan is a **payment structure**, not a loan. You are not charged interest on the deferred portion, but you must pay both instalments by their respective due dates to avoid penalties. If you withdraw from units after the census date, you remain liable for the full fee, even if you have only paid the first instalment so far. This is a critical point that catches many students off guard, particularly those who reduce their study load mid-semester.

## Critical ANU Fee Deadlines for 2025

Knowing the exact **ANU fee deadlines 2025** is essential to maintaining your enrolment and avoiding financial penalties. ANU operates on a strict schedule, and missing a payment date triggers an automatic administrative process that can escalate quickly. Below is a consolidated view of the key dates for the 2025 academic year.

**Semester 1, 2025 deadlines:** The enrolment and first fee instalment deadline is **14 February 2025**. The census date, which is the last day to withdraw from units without financial liability, is **31 March 2025**. The second fee instalment deadline is **4 April 2025**. If you are commencing your program in Semester 1, you must also ensure your **Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)** is paid and valid before enrolment can be finalised.

**Semester 2, 2025 deadlines:** Enrolment and first fee instalment are due by **18 July 2025**. The census date falls on **31 August 2025**, and the second instalment deadline is **5 September 2025**. For students starting in Semester 2, the same OSHC requirements apply. ANU also offers a limited number of **summer session units** between December 2025 and February 2026, with separate fee deadlines published closer to the session start date. These summer units are charged on a per-unit basis and must be paid in full before the session commences, as no instalment option is typically available for non-standard teaching periods.

## What Happens If You Miss a Payment: ANU Late Payment Penalties

Missing a fee payment deadline at ANU triggers a cascade of consequences that can affect your enrolment status and academic progress. The university's **late payment penalties** are designed to encourage timely payment, and they are enforced systematically through the student administration system.

The first consequence is a **late payment fee** of AUD 200, which is applied to your account shortly after the due date passes. This fee is non-refundable and is added to your outstanding balance. More significantly, if your fees remain unpaid beyond a grace period of approximately 10 business days, ANU may place a **financial encumbrance** on your account. This encumbrance prevents you from accessing your academic transcript, re-enrolling in future semesters, or graduating. In practical terms, a financial hold means you cannot view your final results, apply for a new student visa using updated enrolment documents, or receive your testamur upon completion of your degree.

If the debt remains unresolved for an extended period, ANU may refer the matter to an external debt collection agency. This step incurs additional recovery costs that are passed on to you, and it can have serious implications for your credit history in Australia. In the most severe cases, the university may cancel your enrolment entirely. For international students, this triggers a mandatory notification to the **Department of Home Affairs**, which can lead to the cancellation of your student visa under the Education Services for Overseas Students (ESOS) Act. The 2025 ESOS legislative framework continues to enforce strict compliance, and ANU is obligated to report non-payment that results in enrolment cancellation.

## How to Set Up and Manage Your Payment Plan Through ISIS

The **ANU Interactive Student Information System (ISIS)** is the central platform for managing your fees and payment plans. After you accept your offer and receive your student ID, you can log into ISIS to view your fee statement, check deadlines, and make payments. The system displays your total semester liability, any credits from scholarships or upfront payments, and the remaining balance.

To view your instalment schedule, navigate to the **"Fees & Payments"** section within ISIS. Here you will see a breakdown of the two instalments, including the amounts and due dates. You can pay each instalment directly through the platform using a credit card, BPAY, or an international bank transfer via the university's preferred payment partner. ANU has partnered with **Convera** (formerly Western Union Business Solutions) to facilitate international payments in your local currency, which can reduce exchange rate markups and bank fees compared to a standard wire transfer.

If you are experiencing genuine financial hardship, ANU offers a **Fee Payment Extension** application. This is not an automatic right but is assessed on a case-by-case basis. You must submit the application through ISIS before the payment deadline, providing evidence of your circumstances, such as a delay in scholarship disbursement, unexpected medical expenses, or currency transfer issues caused by banking restrictions in your home country. Extensions are typically granted for a maximum of 20 business days and do not waive the late payment fee, though in exceptional cases the fee may be reversed subject to approval by the Fees and Scholarships Office. You should never assume an extension will be granted; always pay by the deadline if you are able, and only request an extension as a last resort.

## Strategies to Avoid Late Fees and Financial Holds

Avoiding **ANU late payment penalties** starts with proactive financial planning before you even arrive in Canberra. One of the most effective strategies is to set up a dedicated Australian bank account as early as possible and transfer your semester funds in a single transaction to minimise international transfer delays and fees. Many students underestimate how long international transfers can take—some corridors, particularly from South Asia and parts of Africa, can require 7–10 business days for funds to clear. Initiating your transfer at least two weeks before the deadline provides a buffer.

Another practical approach is to align your payment schedule with any scholarship disbursements. If you hold an external scholarship from your home government or a private sponsor, confirm the disbursement dates well in advance. Some sponsors release funds only after you have commenced classes, which can conflict with ANU's upfront payment requirement. In these cases, you may need to arrange a **bridging payment** from personal funds or negotiate a payment extension with the university, supported by a letter from your sponsor confirming the pending disbursement.

You should also monitor your ANU student email account and ISIS notifications regularly. The university sends automated reminders 14 days and 7 days before each deadline, as well as an overdue notice the day after a missed payment. Ignoring these emails is the most common reason students incur late fees. If you receive an overdue notice, contact the **ANU Fees and Scholarships Office** immediately. While the late fee may already have been applied, prompt communication can prevent the escalation to a financial encumbrance or enrolment cancellation. In some cases, if you can demonstrate that the late payment was caused by a technical error on the university's side or an emergency beyond your control, the late fee may be waived subject to review.

## Comparing ANU's Payment Flexibility with Other Australian Universities

ANU's two-instalment model is relatively standard among Australian universities, though some institutions offer more flexible arrangements. The **University of Melbourne**, for example, also allows international students to pay in two instalments per semester, with deadlines roughly aligned to ANU's schedule. The **University of Sydney** offers a similar structure but provides a monthly payment plan option for students who can demonstrate ongoing financial capacity. **Monash University** has introduced a four-instalment plan for international students in certain programs, reducing the per-payment burden but extending the payment period across the entire semester.

Where ANU differs is in its strict enforcement of deadlines and the relatively short grace period before financial encumbrances are applied. Some universities allow a 30-day grace window before imposing restrictions; ANU's process is often faster. This makes it especially important for ANU students to treat deadlines as firm. On the positive side, ANU's partnership with Convera and its transparent fee publication schedule make it easier to plan ahead compared to universities that update fees less predictably. The **ANU Fees and Scholarships Office** is also known for being responsive to student enquiries, particularly when contacted before a deadline rather than after it has passed.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I pay my ANU tuition in more than two instalments?**
A: The standard ANU instalment plan allows two payments per semester. For the 2025 academic year, the first 50% is due on 14 February (Semester 1) or 18 July (Semester 2), and the second 50% is due on 4 April or 5 September respectively. If you require a more flexible payment schedule due to documented financial hardship, you can apply for a Fee Payment Extension through ISIS, but this does not change the underlying two-instalment structure. Full payment plans with three or more instalments are not currently available for international students.

**Q: What is the exact late payment penalty amount at ANU in 2025?**
A: The late payment fee for 2025 is AUD 200. This fee is applied to your account if the payment is not received by the due date. Additionally, if your account remains unpaid for more than 10 business days, a financial encumbrance is placed on your record, preventing access to transcripts and re-enrolment. If the debt exceeds 30 days overdue, ANU may refer it to an external collection agency, adding recovery costs that can exceed AUD 500 depending on the total amount owed.

**Q: Does ANU offer any discounts for early or upfront annual payment?**
A: ANU does not currently offer a discount for paying the full annual tuition upfront. However, paying the full semester amount by the first instalment deadline ensures you avoid any risk of missing the second deadline. The university's scholarship programs, such as the ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship, provide tuition reductions of 25% or 50% for eligible students, but these are merit-based and applied before payment is calculated, not a discount for early payment.
