Navigating your degree structure at the Australian National University (ANU) can feel complex, especially when your academic interests evolve. In 2026, ANU continues to refine its flexible double-degree and vertical double-degree frameworks, making it crucial to understand the mechanics of an **ANU change major**. According to the Australian Government Department of Education's 2026 Higher Education Statistics, approximately 18% of domestic undergraduate students change their field of study within the first two years. For international students, the Tertiary Collection of Student Information (TCSI) reported a 12% internal program variation rate in 2025, highlighting the importance of mastering the **program variation** process early.

This guide provides a step-by-step breakdown of declaring or changing your major at ANU, focusing on eligibility, strict deadlines, and the construction of a compliant **academic plan**. Whether you are moving from a Bachelor of Arts to a Bachelor of Science, or simply switching from Political Science to History within the same degree, you must adhere to specific governance policies set by ANU Academic Board.

## Understanding ANU's Degree Structure and Flexible Learning

Before initiating an **ANU change major**, you must grasp the anatomy of your current enrollment. ANU operates on a modular system, but the flexibility varies significantly between single degrees and vertical double degrees.

A standard single degree typically requires 144 units (24 courses). Within this, you have **majors** (48 units), **minors** (24 units), and electives. If you are in a specialist program like the Bachelor of Philosophy (PhB), the **degree structure** is research-intensive and less flexible for lateral transfers. **Vertical double degrees**—such as the Bachelor of Arts/Master of Public Policy—are governed by strict progression rules. If you are in a vertical pathway, a standard program variation might not be possible without exiting the accelerated master’s component.

The key to a successful transition lies in the **program variation** mechanism, which is the formal governance term for altering your enrollment. You are not simply "switching classes"; you are requesting a change to your Program and Plan combination in the Interactive Student Information System (ISIS). For 2026, ANU has tightened the rules around **major declaration** for students who have exceeded 72 units without a declared specialization, potentially delaying graduation if action is not taken.

## Eligibility Criteria: Who Can Request an ANU Change Major?

Not every student is eligible to submit a **program variation** request. The eligibility hinges on your citizenship status, academic standing, and unit progression. **International student visa holders** face the strictest scrutiny due to the Education Services for Overseas Students (ESOS) Act.

According to the Department of Home Affairs 2025-2026 visa integrity framework, changing your major must not inadvertently extend your Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) beyond the permissible study duration without a visa extension. **ANU requires international students** to maintain a full-time load of 24 units per semester. If your new **academic plan** for the major change results in a part-time load due to prerequisite gaps, the variation will be rejected.

Domestic students have more flexibility but must meet the **Academic Progress Rule**. If you are on academic probation (academic standing of "At Risk" or "Show Cause"), your request to change majors is automatically flagged. You must demonstrate that the new major aligns with a recovery strategy. Furthermore, **GPA prerequisites** apply. For high-demand majors like Computer Science or Psychology in 2026, internal transfers require a minimum ANU GPA of 5.0 out of 7.0, and completion of specific gateway courses with a Distinction average.

## Critical Deadlines for Program Variation in 2026

Timing is the most common pitfall in the **ANU change major** process. ANU does not allow unlimited variation windows. The university operates on a pre-census and post-census variation system, governed by the Principal Dates calendar for 2026.

The most critical deadline is the **Semester 1 2026 Census Date: March 31, 2026**. To change your major effective for Semester 1, you must submit the **program variation** request before this date. If you miss it, you can still change your major effective for Semester 2, but you will be financially and academically liable for any Semester 1 courses that do not count toward your new major.

For students looking to change between Semester 1 and Semester 2, the **Semester 2 2026 Census Date is August 31, 2026**. However, a strategic window opens after Semester 1 results are released on July 2, 2026. If you failed a prerequisite for your current major but passed a gateway course for a new interest, you can apply for a variation during the first two weeks of Semester 2 without financial penalty. **Late variation requests** (post-census) require a special permission form and are only approved in exceptional circumstances, such as documented medical issues.

## How to Build a Compliant Academic Plan

A request to change your major is only as strong as the **academic plan** you submit alongside it. ANU’s Program and Course Approval Committee (PCAC) uses this plan to check for "degree completion feasibility." You cannot simply state you want to change; you must map out every unit until graduation.

Start by downloading the **2026 Program Requirements** for your target degree from the ANU Programs and Courses website. Identify the **compulsory core courses** for the new major. Most ANU majors require 48 units: typically two 1000-level introductory courses, two 2000-level core courses, and two to four 3000-level advanced courses. Your plan must prove you can fit these 8 courses into your remaining units without exceeding the maximum 144-unit cap for a single degree.

A common mistake is ignoring **requisite waivers**. If you are a second-year student in 2026 trying to switch to a major like Mathematics, you might lack the 1000-level calculus prerequisite. Your academic plan must explicitly state when you will take the catch-up introductory course (e.g., "MATH1013 in Semester 2 2026") and how it sequences into the advanced courses in 2027. The **ANU Academic Progress Committee** rejects plans that show a student taking 3000-level courses before completing the 1000-level foundation, as this violates the **degree structure** rules.

## The ISIS Variation Request: A Step-by-Step Walkthrough

The technical execution of an **ANU change major** happens through ISIS (Interactive Student Information System). However, the "Manage My Degree" interface can be misleading. You must select the "Apply for Program Variation" tile, not just "Enrollment" changes.

Step one: Log into ISIS and navigate to the "Degree Management" dashboard. Step two: Select "Program Variation." You will see your current Program (e.g., BARTS—Bachelor of Arts) and Plan (e.g., HIST-MAJ). Step three: Click "Request Variation." If you are staying within the same degree but changing the major, select "Plan Change." If you are moving to an entirely different degree (e.g., from Arts to Commerce), select "Program Transfer." **International students must be aware** that a Program Transfer is a higher-risk transaction that triggers a new CoE assessment, while a Plan Change usually does not.

After selecting the new major, you must upload the **mandatory supporting documents**. This includes your proposed **academic plan** (an Excel spreadsheet or PDF matrix) and, for international students, a brief statement addressing how the change aligns with your visa conditions and does not extend your study timeline beyond the CoE end date of December 31, 2026 (for mid-year completions) or June 30, 2027. The 2026 processing time for standard variations is 10 to 15 business days.

## Overcoming Common Rejection Scenarios

Approximately 22% of **program variation** requests at ANU are rejected in the first round, based on internal audit data from the Division of Student Administration in 2025. Understanding why will save you weeks of delay.

The primary reason for rejection is **unit overload**. If your academic plan requires you to enroll in 30 units (5 courses) in a single semester to catch up on prerequisites, the system will automatically block the request. ANU strictly enforces a maximum of 24 units per semester without a High Distinction average approval from the Associate Dean. The second reason is **degree incompatibility**. A student in the Bachelor of International Relations might try to change to a major in Engineering. Without a full program transfer application to the Bachelor of Engineering (Honours), which has a separate ATAR or GPA cutoff of 5.5 in 2026, the plan change will fail.

A third hidden trap is the **"24-unit rule" for graduation**. ANU requires that the final 48 units of your degree include the completion of your declared major. If you try to change your major in your final semester with only 24 units remaining, you will be rejected unless you already have the majority of the new major completed as electives. You must have at least 48 units of runway left to declare a new major.

## FAQ

### Q: Can I change my major at ANU if I am an international student on a student visa?
A: Yes, but strict conditions apply under the 2026 ESOS framework. You must maintain a full-time load of 24 units per semester. If the major change extends your study timeline beyond your CoE end date (e.g., beyond December 2026 for Semester 2 completions), you must apply for a new CoE and potentially a new student visa. ANU will not approve the variation if it creates a study gap of more than 2 months in your sequence.

### Q: What is the latest deadline to declare a change of major for Semester 2 2026?
A: The final financial and academic deadline is the Semester 2 Census Date, **August 31, 2026**. However, to avoid learning disruptions, you should submit the variation within the first two weeks of the semester (by August 7, 2026). If you submit after the census date, you will retain liability for your old major's courses and the change will only take effect for Semester 1 2027.

### Q: How many units do I need to have completed before I can declare a new major at ANU?
A: You can technically declare a major as early as 0 units, but to *change* a major successfully, you typically need at least 48 units remaining in your degree. If you have completed 120 units out of a 144-unit degree, you likely cannot switch to a new 48-unit major, as you would exceed the degree cap or lack the required 48 units of advanced-level runway. The system requires a feasible completion path within the standard 3-year (144-unit) duration.

### Q: Does changing my major affect my GPA or academic standing at ANU?
A: No, changing your major does not erase or reset your GPA. All previous grades remain on your transcript. However, if you were on a "Show Cause" status with a GPA below 4.0 in 2025, your variation request will require a supporting letter from the Academic Skills and Learning Centre to prove you have a recovery strategy. High-demand majors in 2026, such as Psychology, require a minimum GPA of 5.0 for internal transfers.
