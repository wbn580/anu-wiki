Canberra is more affordable than Sydney or Melbourne for students, but costs are not negligible. This article provides a detailed budget breakdown for ANU students. All figures in Australian dollars (AUD).

## Tuition Fees (2026)

### International Undergraduate Students

- **Arts, Humanities, Social Sciences**: $43,000–$45,000
- **Business, Commerce, Economics**: $45,000–$48,000
- **Law (LLB)**: $48,000–$50,000
- **Science**: $47,000–$50,000
- **Engineering, Computing**: $49,000–$52,000
- **Medicine (MChD)**: Approx. $75,000–$80,000


Range reflects variation by specific program. Check ANU Programs and Courses for exact fees.

### Domestic Students (Commonwealth Supported Places)

Domestic undergraduate students pay student contribution amounts, which vary by discipline band:
- Band 1 (humanities, law, commerce): ~$4,000–$12,000/year
- Band 2 (science, health, engineering): ~$9,000–$12,000/year
- Band 3 (medicine): ~$12,000/year

HELP loans (HECS-HELP) cover the student contribution. No upfront payment required for domestic students.

## Accommodation Costs

### On-Campus (Residential Colleges and Halls)

- **Catered colleges (Burgmann, John XXIII, Ursula Hall)**: $400–$550 · Room + 3 meals/day + utilities
- **Self-catered halls (Burton & Garran, Fenner, Wamburun)**: $250–$400 · Room + utilities (kitchen shared)
- **UniLodge @ ANU**: $280–$450 · Room + utilities + facilities
- **Davey Lodge / Lena Karmel Lodge**: $280–$420 · Room + utilities + facilities


### Off-Campus Shared Housing

- Room in shared house (near campus: Turner, Braddon, Acton): $220–$320/week
- Room in shared house (further out: Belconnen, Gungahlin): $180–$250/week
- One-bedroom apartment (near campus): $380–$500/week

## Living Expenses (Weekly Budget)

- **Accommodation (see above)**: $250–$500
- **Food (if self-catered)**: $80–$150
- **Utilities (electricity, gas, internet)**: $30–$50
- **Transport**: $20–$40
- **Phone plan**: $10–$25
- **Entertainment, social**: $50–$100
- **Health insurance (OSHC for international students)**: $10–$15


**Total weekly budget (self-catered, shared housing)**: ~$450–$650
**Total weekly budget (catered college)**: ~$500–$700
**Annual total (40 weeks academic year)**: ~A$18,000–$28,000 in living costs

## Hidden Costs to Budget For

- **Textbooks and course materials**: $500–$1,000/year (varies by program; science/medical textbooks are more expensive)
- **Student Services and Amenities Fee (SSAF)**: ~$350/year
- **Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)**: ~$600–$800/year (mandatory for international students; ANU preferred provider: Allianz)
- **Lab coat, safety equipment**: ~$100 (one-off, for science/medical students)
- **Printing and photocopying**: ~$50–$100/semester

## Canberra-Specific Savings

Canberra has several student-friendly cost advantages:
- **Free city bus (during the day)**: Transport Canberra operates a free city loop bus
- **Student concession on public transport**: 50% off MyWay fares (domestic students only; international students may not qualify for concessions in ACT)
- **National institutions are free**: National Gallery, National Museum, National Library, Parliament House — all free admission
- **Cheaper eating out than Sydney/Melbourne**: Typical student meal $12–$18 in Canberra vs $15–$22 in Sydney
- **Flatter rental market**: Canberra rental yields are lower than Sydney/Melbourne, translating to more affordable share housing near campus
