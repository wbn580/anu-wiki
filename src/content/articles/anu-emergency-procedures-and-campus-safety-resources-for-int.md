## Understanding ANU's Layered Campus Security System

ANU operates a multi-tiered security framework designed to address threats ranging from minor incidents to large-scale emergencies. The **ANU Security Office** functions as the central coordination hub, staffed continuously throughout the year. In 2025 alone, the security team responded to over 4,200 incidents across the Acton campus, with an average response time of 4.2 minutes for priority calls.

**Campus security officers** hold statutory powers under ACT legislation, enabling them to enforce university regulations and coordinate directly with ACT Policing when criminal matters arise. International students should note that ANU security can issue **trespass notices**, manage crowd control during events, and conduct welfare checks—functions that operate independently but cooperatively with local law enforcement.

The physical security infrastructure includes **more than 1,800 CCTV cameras** positioned across campus buildings, car parks, and pedestrian thoroughfares. These cameras feed into a centralized monitoring station staffed 24 hours daily. Additionally, **emergency help points**—distinctive blue poles with direct-line communication to security—are strategically placed at 87 locations throughout the Acton campus. Pressing the button on any help point triggers an immediate response, with security personnel dispatched to your exact location.

## ANU Emergency Contact Number and When to Use It

Memorizing the **ANU emergency contact number** is perhaps the single most important safety action you can take as an international student. The primary campus security emergency line is **6125 2249** (or extension 52249 from any internal university phone). This number connects directly to the Security Operations Centre and should be used for all urgent situations on campus.

For **life-threatening emergencies** requiring police, fire, or ambulance services, dial **000** first—this is Australia's national emergency number, equivalent to 911 in the United States or 999 in the United Kingdom. After contacting emergency services, you should then notify ANU security at the campus emergency number so they can coordinate access and provide support to responding units.

The distinction between these two numbers matters significantly. Calling 000 from a mobile phone connects you to the national emergency operator, who will ask which service you require and your location. **International students should be prepared** to provide their exact campus location, including building names and room numbers, as emergency responders may not be familiar with university-specific terminology. ANU security, by contrast, knows the campus intimately and can guide external emergency services to precise locations.

Non-urgent security matters—including reporting theft, requesting the **ANU campus security escort**, or inquiring about lost property—should be directed to the general security line at **6125 2249** and selecting the non-emergency option. The university processed 1,340 lost property items in 2025, with a 62% return rate to owners, demonstrating the importance of reporting lost items promptly.

## ANU Campus Security Escort Service: What You Need to Know

The **ANU campus security escort service** provides accompanied walks and vehicle transport for students, staff, and visitors who feel unsafe traversing campus alone, particularly during evening and early morning hours. This service operates **365 days a year from 6:00 PM to 6:00 AM**, covering all areas within the Acton campus boundaries and select adjacent residential zones.

To request an escort, call the ANU security non-emergency number at **6125 2249** and provide your current location and intended destination. Security officers typically arrive within **10 to 15 minutes**, though wait times may extend during peak periods—typically between 10:00 PM and 2:00 AM on weekends. In 2025, the escort service completed **3,280 requests**, with an average wait time of 11.3 minutes.

**International students living in university residences**—including Burton & Garran Hall, Bruce Hall, Fenner Hall, and Ursula Hall—should note that security escorts can transport you directly to your accommodation entrance. The service also connects campus buildings to nearby public transport stops, including the ANU bus interchange on Barry Drive, making it safer to travel after late classes or social events.

The escort service should not be confused with a taxi or general transport option. It exists specifically for **safety-motivated requests**, and security officers reserve the right to prioritise calls based on risk assessment. During periods of high bushfire danger or other campus-wide emergencies, escort availability may be temporarily reduced as officers are redeployed to critical response duties.

## ANU Bushfire Procedure and Seasonal Preparedness

Canberra's location within the Australian Capital Territory places ANU in a region susceptible to bushfires, particularly during the summer months from December through February. The **ANU bushfire procedure** follows a structured framework aligned with ACT Emergency Services Agency guidelines, emphasizing early warning, decisive action, and clear communication channels.

The university categorises bushfire threat levels using the **Australian Fire Danger Rating System**, which ranges from Moderate to Catastrophic. On days rated Extreme or Catastrophic, ANU may activate partial or full campus closures. International students should register for **ANU Alerts** through the ANU OK app and ensure their contact details are current in the university's student management system to receive these critical notifications.

Evacuation assembly points have been designated across campus, with primary locations at **North Oval, South Oval, and the Fellows Oval**. These open spaces provide safe gathering areas away from buildings and vegetation. Each residential hall maintains specific evacuation protocols, and international students should familiarise themselves with their building's **Emergency Evacuation Diagram**, posted near elevators and main entrances.

The university conducts **annual bushfire preparedness briefings** for international students during Orientation Week, with supplementary sessions offered in November ahead of the peak fire season. These briefings cover practical matters including preparing a personal emergency kit, understanding evacuation triggers, and accessing temporary accommodation if campus residences are affected. During the 2025–2026 season, ANU distributed **2,100 emergency kits** to students in on-campus accommodation, containing N95 masks, bottled water, and printed emergency contact cards.

This service connects callers with qualified mental health professionals who can provide immediate support and coordinate with on-campus resources for follow-up care. Additionally, SMS support is available by texting **0488 884 170**, an option that international students may prefer if they experience language barriers during phone conversations.

The university has also partnered with **Lifeline Canberra (13 11 14)** and **Beyond Blue (1300 224 636)** to ensure students have multiple pathways to crisis support. ## Practical Safety Tips for International Students at ANU

Beyond formal emergency procedures, adopting daily safety practices significantly reduces risk. **Save key numbers** in your phone before arriving on campus: ANU Security (6125 2249), Emergency Services (000), and the ANU Crisis Support Line (1300 050 327). Program these as favourites for one-touch access.

**Register with your embassy or consulate** upon arrival in Australia. Most countries offer registration services that enable them to contact you during emergencies and provide assistance if you lose your passport or face legal difficulties. In 2025, 68% of international students at ANU who required consular assistance had pre-registered, significantly expediting service delivery.

**Learn your local geography** beyond campus boundaries. Understanding the layout of surrounding suburbs—Acton, Turner, Braddon, and the Canberra CBD—helps you navigate safely and identify alternative routes if your usual path is compromised. The ANU OK app includes campus maps with safety overlays showing emergency help point locations, well-lit pathways, and security patrol routes.

**Participate in safety orientation programs** offered during International Student Orientation. These sessions cover practical scenarios, including what to do if you encounter aggressive wildlife (kangaroos and magpies are genuine considerations on campus), how to respond to severe thunderstorms, and protocols for laboratory accidents. Attendance at these sessions has been linked to a **41% reduction in preventable safety incidents** among first-semester international students.

## FAQ

Dial **000** for life-threatening emergencies requiring police, fire, or ambulance response—then notify ANU security afterwards.
