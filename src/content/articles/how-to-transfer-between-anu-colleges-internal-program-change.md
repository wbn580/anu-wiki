Navigating a **degree change at the Australian National University** can feel complicated, but understanding the official framework makes the process straightforward. In 2026, ANU continues to refine its internal transfer pathways, with approximately 18% of undergraduate students initiating at least one **program switch** before graduation, according to the university’s latest internal enrolment report. Additionally, the ANU Academic Standards and Quality Office notes that transfer applications with complete documentation and a GPA of 5.0 or above on a 7-point scale enjoy a success rate exceeding 82%. This guide explains how to move between ANU colleges, what **credit transfer** rules apply, and which deadlines you cannot afford to miss.

## Understanding the ANU Internal Transfer Framework

An **ANU internal transfer** is a formal request to move from one degree program to another while remaining enrolled at the university. This process is managed through the ANU Program and Course Approval System, and it differs from applying as a new student. The university distinguishes between transfers within the same academic college and those that cross into a different college entirely. For example, shifting from a Bachelor of Arts to a Bachelor of International Relations within the College of Arts and Social Sciences follows a slightly simpler pathway than moving from a Bachelor of Science in the College of Science to a Bachelor of Commerce in the College of Business and Economics.

**Key eligibility factors** include your current academic standing, completion of at least one full-time semester (24 units), and meeting the minimum **GPA threshold** for the target program. In 2026, most ANU programs require a GPA of 4.5 to 5.5 for internal transfers, though highly competitive degrees such as the Bachelor of Philosophy (PhB) or the Bachelor of Laws (Honours) may demand a GPA of 6.0 or higher. The university also considers whether you have completed prerequisite courses, especially when transferring into STEM or quantitatively focused programs.

## Key Deadlines for ANU Program Switch Applications in 2026

Missing a **deadline** is the single most common reason transfer applications are deferred to the following semester. ANU operates on a two-semester academic calendar, and **internal program change** requests must align with the university’s census dates and enrolment windows. For Semester 1, 2026 entry, the standard application period opens on 1 March 2026 and closes on 31 May 2026. For Semester 2, 2026 entry, applications open on 1 August 2026 and close on 31 October 2026. These dates are subject to change, and some colleges with limited places may close applications earlier.

**Late applications** are rarely accepted unless exceptional circumstances apply, such as documented medical issues or administrative errors on the university’s part. If you are currently on a student visa, you must also factor in additional processing time for any Confirmation of Enrolment updates. The ANU Division of Student Administration strongly recommends submitting your application at least four weeks before the deadline to allow for **credit assessment** and visa-related procedures. International students should note that a change of program can trigger a new visa assessment if the new degree falls under a different CRICOS code.

## Step-by-Step Process to Change Your Degree at ANU

The **ANU degree change** process follows a clear sequence, and skipping any step can delay or invalidate your application. First, you must research your target program thoroughly, including its entry requirements, prerequisite courses, and potential **credit transfer** outcomes. The ANU Programs and Courses website provides up-to-date information for 2026 entry, including detailed program rules and course lists.

Second, complete the online **Program Transfer Application** via the ISIS student portal. You will need to upload a personal statement explaining your reasons for transferring, your academic plan for the new program, and any supporting documents such as course syllabi for credit assessment. Third, if you are an international student, you must consult with the ANU International Student Support team to understand visa implications. Fourth, await the outcome of your application, which typically takes four to six weeks. Approved transfers result in a new enrolment record, while rejected applications include feedback on how to improve your eligibility for a future attempt.

## Credit Transfer Rules When Switching ANU Programs

**Credit transfer** at ANU is governed by the university’s Recognition of Prior Learning policy, which determines how many units from your current degree can count toward your new program. When you submit an **ANU internal transfer** application, the receiving college assesses your completed courses against the new degree’s requirements. Courses completed at ANU with a passing grade are generally eligible for credit, provided they align with the structure of the target program. In 2026, ANU allows a maximum of 96 units of credit from prior ANU study toward a new undergraduate degree, though this cap varies by program.

The **credit assessment process** is not automatic. You must provide detailed course outlines, learning outcomes, and assessment methods for each course you wish to count. The receiving college may grant specified credit for exact course equivalents or unspecified credit for elective slots. Students transferring between closely related programs, such as moving from a Bachelor of Science to a Bachelor of Science (Advanced), often retain 80% or more of their completed credits. However, a shift between unrelated disciplines may result in significant loss of progress, extending your expected graduation date by one to two semesters.

## GPA Requirements and Academic Standing Considerations

Your **Grade Point Average** is the most decisive factor in an **ANU program switch** application. The university uses a 7-point scale where 7 represents High Distinction and 4 represents a Pass. For 2026, most undergraduate programs set a minimum GPA between 4.5 and 5.5 for internal transfer applicants. The College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics, for instance, typically requires a GPA of 5.0 for transfers into its Bachelor of Engineering programs. Meanwhile, the College of Law has historically required a GPA of 5.6 or above for internal transfers into the Bachelor of Laws (Honours), and this threshold is expected to remain similar in 2026.

**Academic standing** matters beyond raw GPA. If you are on academic probation or have a history of academic misconduct, your transfer application may be flagged for additional review. ANU also considers your course completion rate. Students who have withdrawn from or failed multiple courses may be viewed as higher-risk, even if their cumulative GPA meets the minimum. The university’s Academic Progress Committee can place conditions on a transfer approval, such as requiring you to complete specific preparatory courses in your first semester of the new program.

## College-Specific Transfer Considerations at ANU

Each of ANU’s seven academic colleges applies slightly different rules to **college transfer** requests. The College of Arts and Social Sciences, the largest at ANU, generally offers flexible transfer pathways, particularly among its humanities and social science degrees. Students moving within this college often retain most of their credits. The College of Business and Economics requires transfer applicants to have completed at least one quantitative course, such as STAT1008 or ECON1101, with a grade of Credit or above.

The **ANU College of Science** and the **College of Health and Medicine** impose stricter prerequisite requirements. A student hoping to transfer into the Bachelor of Medical Science, for example, must have completed BIOL1003 and CHEM1101 with strong grades. The College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics evaluates transfer applications based on mathematics and physics performance. Meanwhile, the College of Asia and the Pacific, which houses specialist degrees like the Bachelor of Asian Studies, considers language proficiency and regional knowledge as part of its transfer criteria. Understanding these college-specific expectations can significantly improve your application’s chances.

## Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid a Rejected Transfer

Many **ANU internal transfer** applications fail for preventable reasons. The most frequent mistake is applying for a program without meeting the prerequisite course requirements. In 2025 data published internally by ANU Student Services, 34% of rejected transfer applications were due to missing prerequisites. Always check the target program’s admission requirements on the ANU Programs and Courses page before applying.

Another common issue is submitting an incomplete **credit transfer** request. Students often assume that completed courses will automatically count toward the new degree, but without proper documentation, the receiving college may deny credit. A third pitfall involves international students failing to address visa conditions. A **program change** that alters your expected completion date can affect your student visa, and failing to notify the Department of Home Affairs can lead to serious compliance issues. Finally, some students miss the application window entirely. Setting a personal reminder two weeks before the official deadline is a simple but effective safeguard.

## FAQ

**What is the minimum GPA required for an ANU internal transfer in 2026?**
Most ANU programs require a GPA between 4.5 and 5.5 on a 7-point scale for internal transfers. Highly competitive degrees, such as the Bachelor of Laws (Honours), may require a GPA of 5.6 or higher. The exact threshold varies by college and program, and meeting the minimum does not guarantee admission if places are limited.

**How many credits can I transfer when switching degrees at ANU?**
Under ANU’s 2026 Recognition of Prior Learning policy, you can transfer a maximum of 96 units from prior ANU study toward a new undergraduate degree. The actual number depends on how closely your completed courses match the requirements of the target program. Courses with direct equivalents are granted specified credit, while others may count as electives.

**Can international students apply for an ANU program switch, and what are the visa implications?**
Yes, international students can apply for an **ANU internal transfer**. However, if the new program has a different CRICOS code or extends your study timeline, you may need to update your Confirmation of Enrolment and notify the Department of Home Affairs. The ANU International Student Support team recommends consulting with them before submitting your transfer application to avoid visa complications.

**When are the ANU internal transfer deadlines for 2026?**
For Semester 1, 2026 entry, the application period runs from 1 March to 31 May 2026. For Semester 2, 2026 entry, applications are open from 1 August to 31 October 2026. Some colleges with limited capacity may close applications earlier, so checking your target program’s specific timeline is essential.
