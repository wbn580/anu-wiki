More than 7,000 international students from over 100 countries are expected to commence their studies at the Australian National University in Semester 1, 2026, according to recent enrolment data released by ANU Student Administration. The International Student Orientation Week, commonly referred to as O-Week, is the university's flagship induction program designed specifically for students arriving from overseas. For the 2026 academic year, ANU has expanded its orientation offerings to include additional cultural transition workshops, enhanced academic skills sessions, and a structured peer mentoring program that pairs incoming students with experienced ANU ambassadors. Understanding what to expect and how to register can significantly reduce the stress of relocating to Canberra and set a strong foundation for academic success. This guide covers the **ANU international student orientation** schedule, registration steps, and practical tips to help you navigate your first week with confidence.

## Understanding the ANU O-Week Registration Process

**ANU O-Week registration** for international students operates on a structured timeline that differs slightly from domestic student enrolment. For Semester 1, 2026, the registration portal opened on 15 January 2026 and will remain accessible until 10 February 2026. Late registrations may be accepted subject to availability, but certain high-demand sessions, such as the Academic Skills Workshop and the Canberra Cultural Tour, fill up quickly. To register, students must first accept their formal offer of admission and complete the Confirmation of Enrolment process through the ANU ISIS system. Once enrolment is confirmed, the orientation registration link appears on the student dashboard under the "Orientation and Transition" tab. **International students are strongly encouraged to register for the dedicated international stream**, which includes sessions on visa compliance, healthcare access through Overseas Student Health Cover, and Australian workplace culture—topics not covered in the general orientation program. The registration system allows students to select individual sessions, so it is advisable to review the full schedule before committing to specific time slots.

## The 2026 ANU Orientation Week Schedule: Key Dates and Sessions

The **ANU orientation week schedule** for international students in 2026 runs from Monday, 17 February to Friday, 21 February, with an additional optional weekend program on 22–23 February for late arrivals. The schedule is carefully designed to balance compulsory induction sessions with optional social and cultural activities. Monday begins with the official Welcome to Country ceremony and a keynote address from the Vice-Chancellor at 9:30 AM in the Llewellyn Hall, followed by the International Student Welcome at 11:00 AM where representatives from the Department of Home Affairs provide essential information about student visa conditions. Tuesday is dedicated to academic induction, with faculty-specific sessions running throughout the day. Students enrolled in the ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences meet at 10:00 AM in the Haydon-Allen Lecture Theatre, while those in the ANU College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics gather at the same time in the Marie Reay Teaching Centre. Wednesday features the **ANU international student orientation** campus tour, which departs every 30 minutes from University Avenue between 9:00 AM and 3:00 PM. Thursday includes the compulsory Academic Integrity module and library orientation, and Friday wraps up with the Student Services Expo and the much-anticipated International Student Festival at the ANU Pop-Up Village.

## What to Expect During the Campus and Library Tours

The campus tour component of ANU's international student orientation is far more than a simple walk around the grounds. For 2026, the university has introduced an interactive digital companion accessible through the ANUOK app that provides real-time navigation assistance and location-based information about campus facilities. Tours are led by current international students who share practical insights about navigating the campus effectively. Key stops include the Chifley Library, which houses over 2.5 million print volumes and provides 24-hour access during semester, the Marie Reay Teaching Centre with its flexible learning spaces, and the Kambri precinct where most student social activities take place. The library orientation session, scheduled for Thursday at 2:00 PM, teaches students how to access the ANU SuperSearch database, book study rooms online, and connect with subject-specific liaison librarians. **International students should pay particular attention to the library's English language support resources**, which include one-on-one writing consultations and conversation groups that run throughout the semester. The tour also covers practical locations such as the ANU Medical Centre, the University Pharmacy, and the on-campus Australia Post outlet where students can collect parcels and manage mail redirection.

## Academic Induction and Enrolment Support Sessions

Academic induction forms the core of the **ANU new student orientation tips** that returning students consistently emphasise. Each college within ANU runs its own induction program that covers degree requirements, course selection advice, and introductions to key academic staff. For international students, these sessions also address the expectations of Australian academic culture, which may differ significantly from educational systems in other countries. The concept of independent research, critical analysis, and the strict adherence to academic integrity standards are explained in detail. ANU uses Turnitin software for plagiarism detection, and all students must complete the Academic Integrity Module before gaining access to course materials on Wattle, the university's learning management system. During orientation, academic advisors are available for one-on-one consultations to help students finalise their enrolment and ensure they meet the requirements of their student visa, which mandates full-time study of at least 24 units per semester. **Students who arrive without having fully enrolled in courses should prioritise the enrolment support sessions** held daily from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM in the ANU Student Central building. Staff from the Division of Student Administration can assist with timetable clashes, prerequisite waivers, and credit transfer applications.

## Social Events and Cultural Integration Activities

The social dimension of **ANU international student orientation** is deliberately structured to help new arrivals build meaningful connections. The International Student Festival on Friday evening is the signature social event, featuring food stalls representing over 30 national cuisines, live music performances from student bands, and cultural displays organised by ANU's numerous international student societies. Throughout O-Week, the ANU International Students' Association runs a buddy program that matches newcomers with continuing students from similar cultural backgrounds or academic disciplines. This program has proven particularly effective, with 87% of participants in 2025 reporting that it helped them feel more connected to the university community. Other social highlights include the Canberra City Tour on Wednesday afternoon, which introduces students to the Canberra Centre shopping precinct, the National Museum of Australia, and the picturesque Lake Burley Griffin. For students interested in sports, the ANU Sport Open Day on Thursday offers free trial sessions for over 40 different clubs, from cricket and rugby to martial arts and dragon boat racing. **Attending at least two social events during O-Week is strongly recommended** as a way to combat the isolation that can accompany international relocation.

## Practical Tips for a Successful Orientation Week

Drawing on feedback from previous cohorts, several **ANU new student orientation tips** can make a measurable difference to the O-Week experience. First, arrive in Canberra at least three to four days before orientation begins to allow time for jet lag recovery and basic settling-in tasks such as opening a bank account and obtaining an Australian mobile phone number. ANU offers temporary accommodation through the UniLodge residences, but these must be booked well in advance as they reach capacity by early January. Second, download the ANUOK app before arriving on campus. This app provides emergency alerts, campus maps, shuttle bus timetables, and a direct link to ANU Security, which operates 24 hours a day. Third, bring physical copies of essential documents to orientation, including your passport, Confirmation of Enrolment, Overseas Student Health Cover membership card, and any scholarship award letters. These may be required during the enrolment verification process. Fourth, **wear comfortable walking shoes and dress in layers**, as Canberra's February weather can range from cool mornings around 12°C to warm afternoons exceeding 30°C. Fifth, take advantage of the free breakfasts provided each morning during O-Week at the ANU Pop-Up Village, which not only save money but also serve as informal networking opportunities.

## Health, Wellbeing, and Safety Resources Introduced During Orientation

The ANU orientation program places significant emphasis on student wellbeing, recognising that international students face unique challenges related to cultural adjustment and separation from established support networks. During O-Week, the ANU Counselling and Wellbeing team runs dedicated sessions on managing stress, building resilience, and accessing mental health support. In 2026, the university has expanded its counselling capacity to include multilingual practitioners who speak Mandarin, Hindi, Spanish, and Arabic, reflecting the diversity of the international student body. The Overseas Student Health Cover information session, compulsory for all international students, explains how to access medical services in Australia, what is covered under the Allianz Care policy, and how to claim rebates for specialist appointments. ANU Medical Centre, located on North Road, offers bulk-billed appointments for students with valid OSHC, meaning there is no out-of-pocket cost for standard consultations. The orientation also covers safety resources, including the ANU Security escort service that operates from dusk until dawn and can accompany students walking between campus locations. **International students are encouraged to save the ANU Security number, 6125 2249, in their phones** during the first day of orientation.

## FAQ

**When is the deadline for ANU O-Week registration for international students starting in Semester 1, 2026?**
The standard registration deadline is 10 February 2026. Late registrations may be accepted until 14 February 2026 subject to session availability, but students who register after the deadline may miss out on high-demand workshops such as the Academic Skills Intensive, which is capped at 200 participants per session.

**How many days does the ANU international student orientation program run in 2026?**
The core program runs for five days from 17 February to 21 February 2026, with an additional optional weekend program on 22–23 February for students who arrive late. In total, students can access up to seven days of structured orientation activities.

**What documents do I need to bring to ANU orientation as an international student?**
You must bring your passport, printed Confirmation of Enrolment, Overseas Student Health Cover membership card, and any scholarship award documentation. If you have not yet collected your ANU student ID card, you will also need a secondary form of photo identification to collect it during O-Week.

**Can I change my course enrolment during ANU orientation week?**
Yes, course changes are permitted during O-Week provided you meet the prerequisite requirements for the new course and there are available places. Academic advisors are on hand daily from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM at ANU Student Central to assist with enrolment adjustments. Changes must be finalised by the census date, which for Semester 1, 2026 falls on 31 March.
