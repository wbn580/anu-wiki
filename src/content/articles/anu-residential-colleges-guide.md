ANU has one of Australia's most developed residential college systems, with approximately 5,000 students living in 10 affiliated colleges and halls on or near campus.

## Catered Colleges

These provide three meals a day, academic support, and a strong collegiate identity:

**Burgmann College**: Postgraduate and undergraduate. Known for academic intensity and strong arts/performance culture. Cost: ~$500/week.

**John XXIII College**: Catholic foundation, ecumenical in practice. Strong sports and music programs. Cost: ~$480/week.

**Ursula Hall**: The largest catered hall. Diverse international community. Cost: ~$460/week.

**Bruce Hall**: Undergoing redevelopment (expected completion 2028). Currently operating from temporary accommodation.

## Self-Catered Halls

Residents share kitchen facilities and have more independence:

**Burton & Garran Hall (B&G)**: Largest self-catered hall. High proportion of international students. Strong community events. Cost: ~$320/week.

**Fenner Hall**: Relocated to Kambri precinct. Modern facilities, strong sustainability focus. Cost: ~$340/week.

**Wamburun Hall**: Socially active, strong music/interhall sports culture. Cost: ~$330/week.

**Wright Hall**: Newest hall, emphasis on leadership development. Cost: ~$350/week.

## UniLodge

UniLodge @ ANU (Davey Lodge, Lena Karmel Lodge, Kinloch Lodge) is a commercial student accommodation provider offering studio and shared apartments with independent living. Cost: $280–420/week. Less collegiate atmosphere but more independence and privacy.

## How to Choose

- **Want community + meals**: Catered college (Burgmann, John XXIII, Ursula Hall)
- **Want independence + community**: Self-catered hall (B&G, Fenner, Wamburun)
- **Want independence + privacy**: UniLodge
- **Budget priority**: Self-catered hall or off-campus share housing

Apply through the ANU Accommodation Portal. Early applications (August–October) are strongly recommended — colleges fill quickly, especially the catered options.
