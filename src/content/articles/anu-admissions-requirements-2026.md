ANU has a unique admissions process in Australia — the most selective university for domestic admissions and one of the most flexible for international admissions. This article covers entry requirements for both cohorts.

## Domestic Students (Australia and New Zealand)

### ATAR Requirements (2026 indicative)

| Program | Minimum ATAR |
|---|---|
| Bachelor of Philosophy (PhB) | 98+ |
| Law (Honours) | 97+ |
| Advanced Computing | 95+ |
| International Relations | 90+ |
| Engineering | 85–90 |
| Commerce | 85–87 |
| Science | 80–85 |
| Arts | 80–83 |

**Wait, these are lower than expected?** ANU's published ATARs are "guaranteed entry" ATARs — the threshold at which admission is guaranteed. The actual "cut-off" is often higher because ANU also uses a **holistic admissions** approach: personal statement, co-curricular activities, and academic performance in relevant subjects are all considered. A student with an ATAR of 93 and a strong personal statement may be admitted to Law, while a student with 99 and a weak statement may be waitlisted.

This is different from other Australian universities, which typically offer purely ATAR-based admission.

### Alternative Pathways
- **ANU College Foundation Studies**: For students who don't meet direct entry; 1-year program → guaranteed pathway to ANU (upon meeting GPA)
- **TAFE/VET pathway**: Diploma-to-degree articulation in selected programs
- **STAT (Special Tertiary Admissions Test)**: For mature-age students without recent ATAR

## International Students

### Academic Requirements by System

| Qualification System | Competitive Range | Law/PhB Range |
|---|---|---|
| International Baccalaureate (IB) | 29–35 | 39+ |
| GCE A-Levels (best 3 subjects) | 11–14 points | 16–17 |
| Gaokao (China) | 70–85% (varies by province) | 90%+ |
| India (CBSE/ISC Year 12) | 85–92% average | 95%+ |
| SAT (US) + AP | 1250+ SAT + 2 APs (3+) | 1400+ SAT + 3 APs (4+) |
| STPM (Malaysia) | 2.5–3.5 GPA | 3.8+ |

### English Language Requirements

Standard: IELTS 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0), TOEFL iBT 80, PTE 64.
Higher requirements: Law (IELTS 7.0, no band below 6.5), Medicine (IELTS 7.0, no band below 7.0).

English language tests are valid for 2 years. ANU accepts IELTS One Skill Retake (for test-takers who narrowly missed one component).

## Direct Application (Not UAC)

ANU processes undergraduate applications directly — not through UAC (Universities Admissions Centre), unlike most NSW/ACT universities. This means:
- One application to ANU (via ANU's online portal)
- Multiple preferences within ANU
- Results released independently of other universities (typically August–September for Semester 1 intake)
