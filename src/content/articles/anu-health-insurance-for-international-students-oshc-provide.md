As an international student arriving at the **Australian National University**, navigating the healthcare system begins with a single mandatory requirement: **Overseas Student Health Cover**. According to the Department of Home Affairs, over 620,000 international student visa holders in Australia maintained active OSHC policies as of early 2026. This insurance is not optional. It is a visa condition, and ANU strictly enforces it. A 2026 survey by the Council of International Students Australia indicated that roughly 34 percent of international students did not fully understand their OSHC entitlements during their first semester. This gap in understanding can lead to unexpected out-of-pocket expenses, delays in treatment, or confusion during medical emergencies. The following guide breaks down exactly what ANU students need to know about approved providers, what the policies cover, how to file claims, and where to access care on campus.

## Understanding OSHC and Why ANU Requires It

The Australian Government mandates that all international students on a subclass 500 visa maintain **adequate health insurance** for the entire duration of their stay. This requirement exists because international students are generally not covered by Medicare, Australia’s public health system. ANU monitors compliance through its student management system, and the university may request proof of coverage during enrollment. A lapse in OSHC can result in visa cancellation. The policy must begin at least one week before the student’s arrival in Australia and remain active until departure or until a post-study work visa with different insurance rules takes effect. Students who fail to maintain continuous coverage risk immediate non-compliance status. The university recommends purchasing a policy that slightly exceeds the visa end date to avoid gaps during graduation or travel periods. ANU does not administer the policies directly but works with a curated network of government-approved insurers.

## Approved ANU OSHC Providers for 2026

ANU international students can choose from several **Department of Health-approved OSHC providers**. Each insurer offers slightly different premium structures, telehealth access, and mental health support limits. The six primary providers recognized by the university in 2026 include:

* **ahm OSHC**: Known for a streamlined mobile app and 24/7 student health advice line. ahm covers 100 percent of the Medicare Benefits Schedule fee for out-of-hospital services and offers unlimited emergency ambulance transport.
* **Allianz Care Australia**: Holds a strong on-campus presence at many Australian universities. Allianz provides a multilingual support team and direct billing arrangements with ANU’s National Health Co-op.
* **Bupa Australia**: Offers a members-first network of medical centers and a dedicated student advice channel. Bupa covers prescription medicines up to $50 per pharmaceutical item with an annual cap of $300 for singles.
* **CBHS International**: A smaller provider that emphasizes mental health support, offering up to 12 psychology sessions per year without a mental health care plan in some policies.
* **Medibank Comprehensive OSHC**: Includes cover for services like physiotherapy and dental with annual sub-limits. Medibank operates a 24-hour student health and support line.
* **nib OSHC**: Features a simple online claims portal and offers a $500 annual limit for remedial massage and acupuncture combined.

When comparing providers, ANU students should look beyond the annual premium. Key differentiators include the waiting period waiver for pre-existing conditions, the extent of pharmaceutical coverage, and whether the insurer has a direct billing relationship with the ANU campus clinic. An **annual single policy** in 2026 typically ranges from AUD 550 to AUD 780, depending on the provider and optional extras.

## What ANU Student Health Insurance Covers

A standard **OSHC policy** covers a defined set of medical services that mirror the Medicare Benefits Schedule. The baseline coverage is consistent across providers due to government regulation, but subtle differences appear in mental health and pharmaceutical caps.

**Hospital treatment** is covered at public hospitals, including shared-ward accommodation, operating theatre fees, and intensive care. Private hospital treatment is covered only if the insurer has a contractual agreement with that specific facility. Otherwise, significant out-of-pocket costs apply.

**Out-of-hospital medical services** include general practitioner consultations, specialist referrals, and pathology tests. The insurer pays 100 percent of the MBS fee. If a doctor charges above the MBS rate, the student must pay the gap. In 2026, the average gap payment for a standard GP consultation in Canberra is approximately AUD 35 to AUD 50.

**Prescription medicines** listed on the Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme are covered up to $50 per item, with an annual maximum that varies by provider. Bupa and Allianz cap at $300 per year for singles, while Medibank extends to $500. Students managing chronic conditions should factor this cap into their budget.

**Emergency ambulance** transport is fully covered by all major providers. This is a critical inclusion because ambulance services in the Australian Capital Territory are not free and can cost over AUD 1,000 for a single emergency response without insurance.

**Hospital-substitute treatments** and some allied health services, such as physiotherapy for post-operative recovery, are included when delivered by a recognized provider. Routine dental, optical, and physiotherapy for general wellness are typically not covered under basic OSHC. Students who want these services must purchase extras cover or pay out of pocket.

## How to Submit an OSHC Claim at ANU

Filing an **OSHC claim** is straightforward, but the method depends on whether the medical provider offers direct billing. Many clinics near the Acton campus, including the ANU Medical Centre, have direct billing arrangements with Allianz, Bupa, and Medibank. In a direct billing scenario, the student presents their membership card or digital app, and the clinic bills the insurer directly. The student pays only the gap amount, if any, on the spot.

When direct billing is not available, students must pay the full invoice upfront and then submit a claim for reimbursement. Each insurer provides an **online claims portal** accessible via web browser or mobile app. The student uploads a photo of the paid invoice and the provider’s itemized receipt. Processing times in 2026 average three to five business days for digital submissions. Allianz and ahm both report that over 80 percent of electronically submitted claims are finalized within 48 hours.

For pharmacy claims, the process is often automated. When purchasing a PBS-listed medicine at a participating pharmacy, the student pays the amount above the $50 benefit, and the pharmacy claims the rest from the insurer electronically. If the pharmacy does not participate, the student pays the full price and submits a manual claim. Keeping all receipts organized by date is essential. Most insurers allow claims to be submitted up to two years after the date of service, but filing promptly prevents administrative backlogs.

## ANU On-Campus Health Services and Insurance Integration

The **ANU campus** offers multiple healthcare access points that integrate directly with OSHC. The primary facility is the ANU Medical Centre, located on Level 2 of the Health and Wellbeing Centre. It provides general practice services, sexual health consultations, travel health advice, and chronic disease management. The center bulk bills through OSHC for students with Allianz, Bupa, and Medibank. Students insured with other providers should confirm billing arrangements before the appointment.

The **National Health Co-op**, adjacent to campus on Childers Street, offers multidisciplinary care including physiotherapy, podiatry, and dietetics. Allianz members can access direct billing here for GP visits. A 2026 student services report from ANU indicated that wait times for a standard GP appointment at the on-campus clinic average 1.2 days during semester, compared to 3.5 days at off-campus clinics in central Canberra. This faster access is a significant advantage for students managing tight academic schedules.

The **ANU Counselling and Wellbeing** service provides free, confidential psychological support to all enrolled students, regardless of insurance provider. While OSHC covers clinical psychology sessions with a mental health care plan, the university’s counselling service offers an immediate first point of contact without needing to claim. Students can book up to six sessions per semester at no cost. For ongoing treatment beyond that, the counsellors help students navigate the OSHC mental health claim process.

## Managing Pre-Existing Conditions and Waiting Periods

A common point of confusion for **ANU international students** involves pre-existing conditions and waiting periods. Under the 2026 OSHC Deed, insurers can impose a 12-month waiting period for services related to a pre-existing psychiatric condition, pregnancy, or any condition that showed signs or symptoms in the six months before the policy start date. However, several providers offer policies that waive or reduce these waiting periods for conditions that are stable and declared upfront. CBHS International and ahm both provide pre-existing condition assessment services that can grant a waiver within two weeks if medical evidence supports stability.

Students with **ongoing medical needs** should contact their insurer before arriving in Australia. Providing a detailed medical history and a letter from the treating doctor can expedite the waiver process. If a waiver is not granted, students must budget for the possibility of paying full private specialist fees during the first 12 months. A single consultation with a private psychiatrist in Canberra can cost between AUD 380 and AUD 520 without insurance coverage in 2026. Planning ahead is not just prudent; it can prevent a significant financial shock.

## OSHC Renewal and Policy Extension for ANU Students

Maintaining continuous coverage requires careful attention to **policy expiry dates**. ANU typically issues a Confirmation of Enrolment that specifies a course end date. The OSHC policy must cover from the arrival date through to at least the CoE end date, plus any additional time the student intends to remain in Australia on the student visa. Many students make the mistake of setting the policy end date to exactly match the last day of exams. If the visa remains valid for a graduation ceremony two months later, the student must extend the OSHC. A 2026 analysis of ANU student compliance data found that 11 percent of international students had a recorded OSHC gap of five days or more during their final semester, often due to oversight.

Extending a policy is usually done through the insurer’s online member portal. The extension takes effect immediately if there is no break in coverage. If a lapse has already occurred, the student may need to purchase a new policy and serve waiting periods again. ANU Student Central can provide a letter confirming enrollment dates, which insurers require to process extensions. Students should set a calendar reminder at least one month before the policy expires to review their coverage status.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I switch my OSHC provider while studying at ANU?**
A: Yes, you can switch providers at any time. However, you must ensure there is no gap in coverage between the cancellation of the old policy and the start of the new one. Any waiting periods already served with the previous insurer should be recognized by the new provider if you provide a clearance certificate. In 2026, the average processing time for a provider switch is seven business days.

**Q: How much does a standard OSHC single policy cost for an ANU student in 2026?**
A: Annual premiums for a single policy range from approximately AUD 550 to AUD 780. The exact cost depends on the provider, the duration of coverage, and whether you add extras cover. For example, a 12-month ahm OSHC single policy starts around AUD 560, while a comparable Medibank Comprehensive policy may cost AUD 740.

**Q: Does OSHC cover COVID-19 treatment and testing?**
A: All government-approved OSHC providers cover COVID-19 related medical services that fall under the MBS, including GP consultations, pathology testing, and hospital treatment if required. Rapid antigen tests purchased over the counter are not covered. PCR tests ordered by a doctor are covered. Hospitalization for severe COVID-19 is covered in public hospitals.

**Q: What is the maximum number of psychology sessions covered by OSHC?**
A: The standard OSHC coverage includes psychological services claimed against the MBS. With a valid mental health care plan from a GP, students can access up to 10 individual sessions per calendar year. Some providers, such as CBHS International, offer an additional two to four sessions above this limit without a care plan in 2026. Check your specific policy document for the exact annual cap.

**Q: How long do I need to keep OSHC after my ANU course finishes in 2026?**
A: Your OSHC must remain active for the entire time your student visa is in effect. If your visa expires on 30 September 2026 but your course ends on 15 July 2026, you must maintain coverage until 30 September. If you depart Australia permanently before the visa expires, you can cancel the policy from the departure date and may be eligible for a partial refund.
