ANU offers a substantial scholarship programme for international students, with awards ranging from 25% to 100% tuition fee remission. This guide covers the major scholarship schemes, eligibility, and application strategy.

## Major International Scholarships

### ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship

The flagship scholarship for international undergraduates and postgraduates:

**Value**: 25% or 50% tuition fee reduction for the duration of the program.
**Eligibility**: Based on academic merit. No separate application — all international applicants are automatically considered.
**Allocation**: 200+ awards annually across all academic colleges.
**Renewal**: Maintain a GPA of 5.0/7.0 (approximately credit average) each semester.

### ANU College-Specific Scholarships

Each academic college offers targeted scholarships:

- **CBE**: CBE International Merit Scholarship · 50% tuition reduction
- **CECC**: CECC International Scholarship · 50% tuition reduction
- **CAP**: CAP International Scholarship · 25–50% tuition reduction
- **Science**: ANU Science International Scholarship · 25% tuition reduction
- **Law**: ANU Law International Merit Scholarship · 50% tuition reduction


### Australia Awards Scholarship (AAS)

Fully funded scholarships for students from developing countries, administered by the Australian Government through DFAT:

**Value**: Full tuition, living allowance (A$30,000+/year), airfares, health cover, establishment allowance.
**Eligibility**: Citizens of participating countries in Asia, Pacific, Africa, and the Middle East.
**Priority areas**: Public policy, health, education, infrastructure, agriculture.
**Application**: Through Australian embassies/high commissions in the applicant's home country.

### Destination Australia Scholarship

For international students studying at ANU campuses outside metropolitan areas (Kioloa Coastal Campus):

**Value**: A$15,000 per year.
**Purpose**: Encourage international students to study in regional Australia.

## Research Scholarships

### ANU HDR Fee Remission Merit Scholarship

For international PhD and MPhil students:

**Value**: Full tuition fee remission for up to 4 years (PhD) or 2 years (MPhil).
**Eligibility**: Strong academic record (First Class Honours or equivalent). Research proposal aligned with supervisor expertise.

### ANU PhD Scholarship

**Value**: Full tuition + living stipend (A$34,000/year, tax-free, indexed annually).
**Duration**: 3.5 years, with possible 6-month extension.
**Competition**: Highly competitive — typically awarded to First Class Honours graduates with research publications.

## Application Strategy

1. **Apply early**: Scholarships are awarded on a rolling basis. Early applications (August–October for Semester 1 intake) have higher success rates.

2. **Maintain a high GPA**: The threshold for a 50% scholarship is typically equivalent to an ATAR of 98+ or an IB of 40+. The 25% threshold is somewhat lower but still competitive.

3. **Write a strong personal statement**: Even when scholarships are "automatic," the personal statement in the ANU application influences the scholarship committee's decision.

4. **Check country-specific awards**: Some countries have bilateral scholarship agreements with Australia that stack with ANU scholarships.

5. **Consider Research Training Program (RTP)**: For research degrees, the Australian Government RTP scheme provides fee offset + stipend — ANU administers RTP allocations for domestic and international students.

## Common Questions

**Can I hold multiple scholarships?** Generally no — ANU prohibits holding multiple ANU-funded scholarships simultaneously. External scholarships (AAS, home-country government awards) may be combinable.

**Is the scholarship renewable?** Yes, subject to meeting the GPA threshold each semester (typically 5.0/7.0). Failing one semester may result in a warning; failing two consecutive semesters typically revokes the scholarship.

**What if I change programs?** The scholarship typically follows the student if transferring within the same academic college. Transferring between colleges may require re-application.
