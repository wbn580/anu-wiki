International students at the **Australian National University (ANU)** are increasingly prioritising practical work experience alongside their academic qualifications. According to the 2026 QS World University Rankings, ANU maintains its position as Australia's top-ranked institution, with employer reputation scores reflecting strong industry connections. The **ANU Career Hub** reported that over 1,800 students participated in work integrated learning programs during the 2025 academic year, with international student participation growing by 23 percent compared to the previous period.

This guide examines the structured pathways available for international students seeking internships, industry placements, and work integrated learning opportunities at ANU in 2026. From credit-bearing coursework programs to competitive placement schemes, understanding the application processes and eligibility requirements is essential for maximising these career-building experiences.

## Understanding ANU Work Integrated Learning for International Students

**Work Integrated Learning (WIL)** at ANU encompasses a range of programs designed to integrate academic theory with professional practice. For international students, these opportunities provide exposure to Australian workplace culture, industry networks, and practical skill development that complements classroom learning. The university's commitment to WIL reflects broader sector trends, with Universities Australia data from early 2026 indicating that 78 percent of domestic employers value work experience when evaluating graduate applications.

The **ANU work integrated learning application** process varies depending on the specific program type. Some opportunities are embedded within degree structures as compulsory or elective coursework units, while others operate through competitive application rounds managed by the ANU Career Hub. International students must pay careful attention to **visa work restrictions**, which typically permit up to 48 hours per fortnight during teaching periods and unlimited hours during scheduled breaks under the updated 2026 student visa framework.

Key WIL categories available to international students include **for-credit internships** integrated into specific degree programs, **industry research projects** supervised by academic staff, **virtual internships** that accommodate remote participation, and **co-curricular placement programs** that sit outside formal coursework requirements. Each pathway has distinct eligibility criteria, application timelines, and assessment requirements that students should understand before applying.

## ANU Internship Programs Available to International Students

The **ANU College of Business and Economics (CBE)** offers one of the most structured internship pathways through its **CBE Internship Program**, which places students with Canberra-based organisations, government departments, and private sector firms. In 2026, the program expanded to accommodate 320 placement positions annually, with approximately 35 percent allocated to international students. Participants complete 120 to 140 hours of workplace activity alongside reflective assessment tasks, earning **6 units of academic credit** toward their degree.

For students in the **ANU College of Arts and Social Sciences**, the **Australian National Internships Program (ANIP)** provides a distinctive opportunity to work within the Australian Parliament, government agencies, and diplomatic missions. International students have participated in ANIP placements with organisations including the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade and various embassies in Canberra. The program requires a strong academic record, with a minimum **GPA of 5.0 out of 7.0** typically expected for competitive applications.

The **ANU College of Engineering, Computing and Cybernetics** facilitates **industry placement credit** through its **Engineering Work Experience** requirement and optional **Computing Internship** units. Engineering students must complete a minimum of **12 weeks (420 hours)** of approved industry experience before graduation, which can be undertaken in Australia or internationally. Computing students can enrol in **COMP3820** or **COMP4820**, which combine 120-hour placements with academic supervision and assessment.

**Research-focused internships** are available through the **ANU Science Industry Placement** program and various **Summer Research Scholarships**. These opportunities allow international students to work within ANU research laboratories, partner organisations, or industry research facilities. The 2026 Summer Research Scholarship round received over 850 applications for 180 available positions, highlighting the competitive nature of these placements.

## How to Apply Through the ANU Career Hub Internship System

The **ANU Career Hub internship** platform serves as the central portal for discovering and applying to work integrated learning opportunities. International students can access the system using their university credentials at **careerhub.anu.edu.au**, where listings are updated regularly throughout the academic year. The platform aggregates opportunities from internal ANU programs, external partner organisations, and third-party internship providers.

To begin the **anu work integrated learning application** process, students should first complete their **Career Hub profile**, including academic details, work eligibility status, and areas of interest. The system uses this information to recommend relevant opportunities and alert students to upcoming application deadlines. **International students must ensure their visa details are current** in the university's student management system, as this information may be verified during the placement approval process.

Application requirements typically include a **tailored resume following the ANU Career Hub template**, a **cover letter addressing specific selection criteria**, and an **unofficial academic transcript**. Some competitive programs may request additional materials such as **academic references**, **portfolios of relevant work**, or **responses to behavioural interview questions**. The ANU Career Hub offers free resume review services and mock interview sessions specifically designed for international students navigating Australian recruitment practices.

**Application timelines vary significantly between programs**. CBE internship applications for Semester 1 2026 placements closed in October 2025, while Semester 2 applications will open in March 2026. ANIP operates on a rolling deadline structure aligned with parliamentary sitting periods. Students should consult the specific program pages on the Career Hub platform for precise dates and plan applications at least **three to four months in advance** of their intended placement period.

## Credit-Bearing Industry Placements: Structure and Assessment

**ANU industry placement credit** programs operate under formal coursework unit codes, meaning students enrol in these units as part of their standard semester enrolment. The credit value typically ranges from **6 to 12 units**, depending on the placement duration and academic workload. International students must ensure that adding placement units does not exceed their **maximum study load** under visa conditions, which generally permits up to 24 units per semester for full-time enrolment.

Assessment structures for credit-bearing placements combine **workplace performance evaluations** with **academic reflection components**. Workplace supervisors complete mid-placement and end-placement assessments evaluating professional conduct, skill development, and task completion. Academic components may include **reflective journals**, **industry research reports**, **case study analyses**, or **oral presentations** that connect workplace experiences to theoretical frameworks from coursework.

The **ANU Learning Outcomes Framework** for WIL units requires students to demonstrate specific competencies including **professional communication in workplace contexts**, **application of disciplinary knowledge to practical problems**, **ethical decision-making in professional settings**, and **critical reflection on personal career development**. International students should note that these learning outcomes are assessed using Australian academic standards and workplace cultural expectations, which may differ from practices in other countries.

**Placement duration requirements** vary by unit code. A standard 6-unit internship typically requires **120 to 150 hours** of workplace engagement, usually completed over **12 to 15 weeks** during a semester. Intensive summer or winter session placements may compress these hours into **4 to 6 weeks** of full-time work. Students must negotiate their work schedule with host organisations before the placement begins and submit a **Learning Agreement** signed by both the workplace supervisor and the academic convenor.

**ANU's own research schools and administrative divisions** also serve as host organisations for internal internships. The **ANU International Office** regularly employs international student interns to support orientation programs and student services, while research schools offer laboratory and field research internships. Internal placements often provide more flexible arrangements for international students who may face external application barriers.

## Eligibility Requirements and Visa Considerations for International Students

International students pursuing **anu internship international students** pathways must satisfy both university eligibility criteria and Australian visa requirements. Academic eligibility typically requires **completion of at least 48 units** (equivalent to one year of full-time study) toward the current degree program, a **minimum GPA of 4.5 to 5.0 out of 7.0** depending on the program, and **good academic standing** without unresolved misconduct matters.

**Student visa (subclass 500) conditions** permit international students to work up to **48 hours per fortnight** while courses are in session. This limitation applies to both paid and unpaid internships when the placement occurs during teaching periods. During scheduled university breaks, including the summer vacation period from late November to late February and the winter break in July, **unlimited work hours are permitted** under the 2026 visa framework.

**Unpaid internships** require careful consideration under Australian workplace law. The Fair Work Act establishes that unpaid work arrangements are lawful only when they form part of a **vocational placement** as defined by the legislation, meaning the placement must be required or authorised under a course of study and must provide no entitlement to pay. ANU credit-bearing internships generally satisfy this definition, but international students should verify with the **ANU Legal Service** if uncertain about their specific arrangement.

**Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)** requirements remain in effect throughout internship periods. Students undertaking placements that involve **physical or laboratory work** should confirm that their OSHC policy provides adequate coverage for workplace activities. Some host organisations may require additional **public liability insurance**, which ANU typically provides for students enrolled in credit-bearing placement units.

**Post-internship reflection and documentation** significantly enhances the value of placement experiences. Students should maintain a **professional portfolio** including project descriptions, quantifiable achievements, and supervisor feedback. The Career Hub recommends updating **LinkedIn profiles** within two weeks of completing an internship, adding the experience with specific details about responsibilities and outcomes. **Articulating internship experiences in job applications** requires specific attention to **Australian employer expectations**. The **STAR method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) is widely used in Australian recruitment for structuring responses to selection criteria. ANU Career Hub workshops specifically address how international students can frame internship experiences using this framework, with practice sessions available throughout the academic year.

## FAQ

### Can international students at ANU complete internships during their first year of study?

Most ANU internship programs require completion of **at least 48 units (one year of full-time study)** before eligibility. However, some co-curricular opportunities through the **ANU Career Hub** may accept first-year students for short-term projects or virtual internships. International students should check specific program requirements, as competitive programs like the **Australian National Internships Program (ANIP)** typically expect students to be in their second year or above with a **minimum GPA of 5.0 out of 7.0**.

### How many hours can international students work during an ANU internship placement?

Under the **2026 student visa (subclass 500)** framework, international students can work **up to 48 hours per fortnight** during teaching periods. For credit-bearing internships counting as part of the academic workload, the hours spent on placement activities are included in this limit. During scheduled breaks, including the **summer vacation (late November to late February)** and **winter break (July)**, unlimited work hours are permitted. Students should consult the ANU International Student Support team to confirm their specific circumstances.

### What GPA is required for ANU credit-bearing internship programs?

GPA requirements vary across ANU internship programs. The **CBE Internship Program** typically requires a **minimum GPA of 5.0 out of 7.0**, while some **College of Science placements** accept students with a **GPA of 4.5 or above**. The **Australian National Internships Program (ANIP)** is highly competitive and generally expects a **GPA of 5.5 or higher** for international student applicants. Students with lower GPAs may still access non-credit internship listings through the **ANU Career Hub** platform.

### Are ANU internships paid or unpaid for international students?

Most **ANU credit-bearing internships** are structured as unpaid vocational placements under the Fair Work Act definition, as they form part of an approved course of study. Some **industry partner placements** may offer stipends or scholarships, particularly in fields such as **engineering, technology, and finance**. The **ANU Career Hub** listings clearly indicate whether positions are paid, unpaid, or stipend-supported. International students should note that unpaid placements still count toward the **48-hour per fortnight work limit** during teaching periods.
